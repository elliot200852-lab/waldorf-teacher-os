A Winter Walk and Finding Strength
(Scene: A group of teenagers is walking through a snowy forest  in Dongshan on a winter afternoon. The air is cold, and the sun is setting.)
Anna: (pulling her scarf tight) It’s getting so cold! Do you feel it too?
Jake: (stomping his feet) Yes, it really feels cold. But look at the sky. Doesn’t it look so pretty?
Liam: (staring up) It looks beautiful. But when it gets dark, the forest becomes a little scary.
Maya: (laughing) Scary? Liam, it sounds like you’re afraid of the dark!
Liam: (smiling) Maybe I am. But this place stays so quiet. It makes me feel nervous.
Anna: (nodding) I know what you mean. 
            The silence feels heavy, like the forest is hiding something.
Jake: (picking up a stick) If the forest is hiding something, it might taste bittersweet, just like 
           wild berries we found last summer!
Maya: (laughing) Or maybe it tastes like sadness. Winter gets darker and colder, but I think it’s 
            a time to grow stronger.
Liam: (thinking) That’s true. Winter feels hard, but it makes you think about yourself.
Anna: Yes, when everything is quiet, you can get to know yourself better.
Jake: (nodding) It sounds deep, but I think it’s true. 
           Winter slows everything down, and that’s not always bad.
Maya: (smiling) And even when it’s dark and cold, we keep walking. That’s the important part.
Liam: (looking at the group) And we stay together. That makes winter feel less scary.
(The group keeps walking, the snow sounding soft under their feet. Dongshan looks peaceful now, even in the fading light.)
