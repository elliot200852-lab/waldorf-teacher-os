Lingchi, also called "slow slicing" or "death by a thousand cuts," was a type of punishment used in China from the 10th century to the early 1900s. It was also used in Vietnam and Korea. In this punishment, a person’s body was slowly cut with a knife over a long time until they died. Lingchi was used for very serious crimes, like betraying the country. Even though it was banned later, the idea of Lingchi still appears in stories and media today.
