1. My little brother, Joe __________ his pajamas and brushed his teeth. He can go to bed now.
a) have worn       b) has worn
c) haven’t worn   d) hasn’t worn


2. Our neighbor ____________ men an invitation card for her wedding party.
have given b) haven’t given c) has given d) hasn’t given

3. Peter and Lisa __________ played tic-tac-toe recently.
a) have b) has
c) haven’t d) hasn’t

4. Samuel is on a Europe tour now. He ________ to thirteen different cities so far.
have gone b) have been c) has gone d) has been

5. My aunt, Liz has read twelve novels ______ the summer holiday started.
for b) since c) yet d) just

6. Oh god! I ______ lost my way. I have got a map, but I ______ understood it yet.
a) has/hasn’t     b) hasn’t/has
c) have/haven’t  d) haven’t/have

7. Mary and Bob ________ slept yet. They are awake.
a) hasn’t b) has
c) haven’t d) have

8. Gary: I ____ seen you for a long time? Where have you _____? 
   Ann: On holiday in Turkey.
have/been  b) haven’t/been c) have/gone d) haven’t/gone

9. My best friend, Sara has planted a lot of trees ___________ . She loves nature so much.
a) last week
b) yesterday
c) recently
d) an hour ago

10. Lawrence hasn’t eaten his lunch ____________ because he doesn’t like spinach at all.
a) just       b) yet
c) already  d) since
