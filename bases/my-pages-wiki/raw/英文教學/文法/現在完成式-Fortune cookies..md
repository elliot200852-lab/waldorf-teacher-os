(In the living room)
Mrs. Brown: Help yourself to some fortune cookies. 
                     Have you ever tried them before?

Linda: No, I haven't.

Peter: Really? They're very popular and have been in the USA since the early 
          1900s.

Mrs. Brown: That's right. They've been an important part of American food 
                      culture for a long time. Here, break one and try it.

Linda: What's this small piece of paper inside?

Peter: Oh, that's your fortune. Go on! Read it.

Linda: It says, "You've already gone through the worst part of your life." 
            Wow! How did the cookie know that?
Peter: My sentence says, "All roads lead to Rome." Why did the cookie say  
           that? I've never been to Rome.

Linda: Come on, Peter. This is fun. Are there any more fortunes?

Mrs. Brown: Sure, but you'll have to eat up all the cookies first.

Linda: That shouldn't be a problem because
           I haven't had lunch yet.








 Fortune cookies have been a popular dessert in the USA for a long time. You can often see them in many Chinese restaurants there. They are well-known not for their taste, but for their sayings.
Here are some common fortunes:

1. Knowlege is power.

2. The world belongs to hard-working people.

3.If you want to have a rainbow, you must put up with the rain.

4. When a blind man leads another, both fall down.

5. Dig your well before you are thirsty.

6. You have to kiss a lot of frogs to find your prince or prince.

Fortune cookies have been an interesting feature of Chinese restaurants in the USA since they became common in the early 1900s.
It is fun to get the fortune cookies and share the wise sayings with family and friends. It is a wonderful way to end a happy meal.
