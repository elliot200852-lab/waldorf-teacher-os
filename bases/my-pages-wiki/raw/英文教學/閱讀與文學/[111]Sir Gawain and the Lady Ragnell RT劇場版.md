Act one: Gawain’s brotherhood and courage
Scene one: King Arthur Seeks Adventure Alone
① Arthur :    
I wish I were not the king. I would like to ride off to seek adventure like my knights. Nobody would fight with me. Not as King Arthur.

Narrator1:

Narrator1&2:      There was nowhere to rest. So poor the King was.

Narrator2:
Suddenly, he saw a light shinning faintly in the distance. He rode toward it. There on a hill stood a black castle with seven towers. From each tower a black flag waved. And all around the castle was a moat full of dark water. Arthur blew his hunting horn, and then shouted.

Arthur: 
( Blow the horn first…) Let down your drawbridge!

Voice inside the castle:
Go away from this place.  JUST GO.

Arthur : 
 I am a knight of the Round Table. I am tired and cold. You cannot turn me away. 

Voice inside the castle:
All right,  YOU asked for it. I will tell my master. Just remember I have warned you.

Later, there was a clang and clatter.（sound effect…) The drawbridge was let down. A very old man led King Arthur to a large hall.

  A fire burned in a fireplace. On the wall above it hung the biggest axe Arthur had ever seen.
Arthur: Wow…mmm..
Narrator2:
A huge man sat by the fire staring into the flames. Then he turned. Arthur saw the huge man had a patch over one eye and a deep scar on his face.

Sir Malger: 
I am Sir Malger of the Deadly Axe. Long have I wished to meet a knight of the Round Table. For I have heard they are the bravest knights of all!  HO…HOOOO….

Arthur:  What you have heard is true. My knights are the greatest of all time.
Narrator1: Sir Malger stood and towered over King Arthur.
Sir Malger:
But no one of them is a match for me.

   A battle! I can kill you with one blow..… I challenge you to a true test of bravery. I will give you my axe. It is sharper and heavier than any in the world. I will let you strike me first. But then…….. you must let me strike you.

Narrator2: Arthur stared at the giant axe. Look…. and think….
Arthur: I will do it.

Narrator1:
Sir Malger took the axe from the wall and Arthur could hardly lift it. Sir Malger kneeled and bent his head. 

Sir Malger:
 Come. Strike your blow!

Arthur lifted the axe with ALL his strength… and brought it down. Instantly, Sir Malger’s head rolled about on the floor. But then ……the headless knight went to pick up his head and placed it back on his neck. He was WHOLE again. 

Sir Malger: (With an evil smile.)
 Now, it is your turn.

Arthur:
So be it. Strike!

Arthur: Oh….my life. My everything.
Narrator2: But it did not come. Arthur looked up. 


Sir Malger: 
       You are a brave man. Perhaps you are wise, too. I will make a bargain with you. I will ask you a riddle. You will have seven days to find the answer. If your answer is correct, you can live. If not,….. you will kneel again for my blow. And this time I will strike right on your neck! Do you swear to return?

Arthur: 
   Yes. I promise to return.
Narrator1: What else can he say?

Scene two: Gawain’s Devotion Saves Arthur
④
Narrator1:
The two men reached the edge of the forest. Arthur told Gawain to wait because he thought he had to go alone. When Arthur rode up to the hill, where the dark castle was. Sir Malger stood on the drawbridge sharpening his giant axe.

Sir Malger:
   You keep your promises. (Holding up his axe.) And I’ll keep mine. Now…. What is your answer?  What do women want most?

Arthur:
 Beauty? Or love? Or children? Or riches? Or adventure? Or truth?

Sir Malger: 
No, no, no…… Bent your head. It’s time.

Arthur:
   Wait! I have one more answer which I learned from an old woman in the forest. What women want most is to …HAVE THEIR OWN WAY.

Sir Malger: (roared with anger)
   My sister! Lady Ragnell! It is SHE that told you all this. She has cheated me…. But I will still keep my word. You may live. Do not pass this way again! Off you go.
Arthur: Whew….Thank God.

Arthur rode off. He was free. But he was not happy. There is still a big problem. Gawain was going to pay a terrible price for Arthur’s life.

   I am Lady Ragnell. This is my true self. My brother, Sir Malger, put me under an evil spell. Because you were so good and kind, I am partly free now, but only partly. For half of each day, I will look as I do now. But for the other half, I will look as horrible as before.

Narrator1:

Sir Gawain: (looked into her eyes, can’t help wanting to kiss her)
