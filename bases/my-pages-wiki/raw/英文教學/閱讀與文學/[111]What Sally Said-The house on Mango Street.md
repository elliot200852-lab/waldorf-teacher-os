What Sally Said-The house on Mango Street

1、What does the sentence mean “ You’re not my daughter, you’re not my daughter. And then he broke into his hands.”?

The last sentence of the story suggests that Sally's father became very upset and lost control of his emotions. He kept saying that Sally was not his daughter, which means he didn't want to have a relationship with her anymore. The phrase "broke into his hands" means that he was very upset and may have hit something or hurt himself. This last sentence shows that the situation between Sally and her father was very sad and difficult, and it suggests that the abuse had a negative impact not only on Sally, but also on her father.

2、What’s the main idea of the story?
The story wants to teach people about domestic violence and how it affects girls like Sally. It shows how victims of domestic violence feel scared, ashamed, and powerless, and how they can try to hide the abuse. The story also explains that people may not believe victims of domestic violence because of how they look or because they don't understand what is happening. It is important to support victims of domestic violence and to help them get the help they need. The story is telling us that we need to work together as a society to stop domestic violence from happening.


3、How does the author describe the character of Sally?
In the story, Sally is a young girl who is physically hurt by her father, and she has bruises and scars on her body. She tries to hide the abuse by lying about it. Sally is scared of her father and feels like she cannot leave or tell anyone about the abuse. She feels trapped and helpless. However, she finds comfort and support from her friends, like the narrator and her family. Sally is a sympathetic character who is going through a very hard time, and the story shows that it is important to help and understand people who are experiencing domestic violence.
