King Arthur and the Round Table Knights
Sir Gawain and the Lady Ragnell

Characters:
 King Arthur, Servant of Sir Malger, Sir Malger, Sir Gawain, Maiden 1, Maiden 2, Mother, Poor woman, Rich lady, Rich lady’s companion, Lady Ragnell, Sir Lancelot, Knight 1, Knight 2, Knight 3 

Act one: Gawain’s brotherhood and courage
Scene one: King Arthur Seeks Adventure Alone
 Arthur :    
I wish I were not the king. I would like to ride off to seek adventure like my knights. Nobody would fight with me. Not as King Arthur.

Narrator:
    On day, King Arthur took off his crown, dressed as an ordinary knight, and rode off his castle all alone. When night fell, he was deep in a strange forest. King Arthur was cold and tired. There was nowhere to rest. Suddenly, he saw a light shinning faintly in the distance. He rode toward it. There on a hill stood a black castle with seven towers. From each tower a black flag waved. And all around the castle was a moat full of dark water. Arthur blew his hunting horn, and then shouted.

Arthur: 
Let down your drawbridge!

Voice inside the castle:
Go away from this place.  

Arthur : 
 I am a knight of the Round Table. I am tired and cold. You cannot turn me away. 

Voice inside the castle:
I will tell my master. But remember. You heard my warning.

Narrator:
Later, there was a clang and clatter. The drawbridge was let down. A very old man led King Arthur to a large hall. A fire burned in a fireplace. On the wall above it hung the biggest axe Arthur had ever seen.
A huge man sat by the fire staring into the flames. Then he turned. Arthur saw the huge man had a patch over one eye and a deep scar on his face.

Sir Malger: 
I am Sir Malger of the Deadly Axe. Long have I wished to meet a knight of the Round Table. For I have heard they are the bravest knights of all! 

Arthur: 
   What you have heard is true.

Sir Malger stood and towered over King Arthur.
Sir Malger:
But no one of them is a match for me.

Arthur:
   We will see. I will do battle with sword or spear or any weapon you choose.

Sir Malger: (laughing)
   A battle! I can kill you with one blow. I challenge you to a true test of bravery. I will give you my axe. It is sharper and heavier than any in the world. I will let you strike me. But then…….. you must let me strike you.
Arthur stared at the giant axe.
Arthur:
 I will do it.

Sir Malger took the axe from the wall and Arthur could hardly lift it. Sir Malger kneeled and bent his head. 

Sir Malger:
 Come. Strike your blow!
 
Arthur lifted the axe with all his strength and brought it down. Instantly, Sir Malger’s head rolled about on the floor. But then the headless knight went to pick up his head and placed it back on his neck. He was whole again. 

Sir Malger: (With an evil smile.)
 Now, it is your turn.

Arthur kneeled and bent his head.
Arthur:
So be it. Strike!

Malger swung his axe. Arthur closed his eyes and waited for the blow to fall. But it did not come. Arthur looked up. 
Sir Malger: 
       You are a brave man. Perhaps you are wise, too. I will make a bargain with you. I will ask you a riddle. You will have seven days to find the answer. If your answer is right, you can live. If not, you will kneel again for my blow. And this time I will strike on your neck! Do you swear to return?

Arthur: 
   Yes. I promise to return.

Sir Malger: 
Then here is the riddle…. What is it that women most want?…. Now go!


Scene two: Arthur searches for the answer to Sir Malger’s Riddle

Narrator: 
Arthur left Sir Malger’s hall. His heart was beating hard and rode back into the forest. When he could ride no more, he slept on the cold ground. The next morning, he woke to find one of his bravest knights leaning over him. It was Sir Gawain. 

Sir Gawain: 
King Arthur! Why are you here?

Arthur:
   I am in trouble. 
Arthur told Gawain all that had happened.

Sir Gawain:
   I will go with you. Somehow we will find the answer to the riddle.

Narrator:
For seven days, Arthur and Gawain rode through the land and asked the riddle of every woman they met. Young maidens with flowers in their hair. Mothers carrying their babies. Poor women tending sheep by the road. Rich ladies with jewels. 

Sir Gawain: 
Young ladies! Could you tell us what women most want?
Maiden 1& 2:
     Oh! We want beauty.

Sir Gawain:
   Ma’am, could you tell us what women most want?
Mother: 
     I definitely love children. 

Sir Gawain:
 What do you think women in the world want the most, Madam?
Poor women:
  Money for sure.  Money is what we want the most.

Arthur:
  What do you think women in the world want the most, Madam?
Rich lady:
  Oh…Amour. Love is the only answer to every woman’s heart. 

Arthur:
Some said beauty. Some said love. Or children. Or riches. Or adventure. Or truth. But somehow I know none is right. 
 

Act two: Sir Gawain turns misfortune into a blessing
Scene one: Monster-like Woman:Lady Ragnell 

Narrator:
   On the seventh day Arthur and Gawain rode slowly toward the black castle of Sir Malger. Worried and silent.  Suddenly, they heard a strange cackle. An old woman was sitting on a wood with her back to them.

Lady Ragnell: (Cackle loudlier and loudlier)
  Wooo…Haha…

Narrator:
The old woman looked like a monster. Her skin was wrinkled and yellow. Her hands were like claws. She has only a few jagged teeth and a patch over one eye.  When she turned and cackled again, Arthur gasped. Sir Gawain turned pale and looked away.  

Lady Ragnell: 
   You will go to your death, King Arthur. Unless I help you.

The two men looked at each other.
Lady Ragnell: 
Oh, yes, I know the answer to the riddle. And I will tell you the only answer that will save your life, if you make me a promise.

Arthur:
 What is it that you wish, my lady?

Lady Ragnell:
   I am Lady Ragnell. And I want one of your knights as my husband.

Arthur:
   That I cannot promise.

Sir Gawain:( Looking at the horrible creature and gulp)
 If the answer can save my king’s life, I will marry you.

Arthur: (whispered)
   I cannot let you do that.

Sir Gawain: 
   You must. We cannot lose our king. 

Arthur:
 ( Looking at Gawain knowing Gawain is right and then turning to Ragnell)
   Then tell me the answer.

Narrator:
Lady Ragnell called him over and whispered in his ear. Arthur almost smiled at her words. Could this be the answer Sir Manger wanted? Perhaps it was what women wanted most. And men as well. King Arthur mounted his horse.

Lady Ragnell: 
   In two days’ time I will be at Camelot. FOR MY WEDDING. Woo…Haha…

Scene two: Gawain’s Devotion Saves Arthur

Narrator:
The two men reached the edge of the forest. Arthur told Gawain to wait because he thought he had to go alone. When Arthur rode up to the hill, where the dark castle was. Sir Malger stood on the drawbridge sharpening his giant axe.

Sir Malger:
   You keep your promises. (Holding up his axe.) And I keep mine. Now…. What is your answer?  What do women want most?

Arthur:
 Beauty? Or love? Or children? Or riches? Or adventure? Or truth?

Sir Malger: 
No, no, no…… Bent your head. It’s time.

Arthur:
   Wait! I have one more answer which I learned from an old woman in the forest. What women want most is to have their own way.

Sir Malger: (roared with anger)
   My sister! Lady Ragnell! She told you. She has cheated me. But I will keep my word. You may live. Do not pass this way again!

Narrator: 
Arthur rode off. He was free. But he was not happy. Gawain was going to pay a terrible price for Arthur’s life.
   
Scene three: Gawain breaks the Spell on Lady Ragnell

Narrator: 
High in a tower she hides away, Lost to the world full of dismay. In a castle dark, dreaming her dreams, She's been there forever so it seems.
Looking out from her prison high, Waiting for Sir Gawain to come riding by. To rescue her and bear her away, In this dark tower, she just can't stay.

Two days later, Sir Gawain and Lady Ragnell were married. No one was asked to the wedding. No one except King Arthur.  Gawain was loved by all. His friend didn’t understand why no one was there. So they prepared a wedding feast in the Great Hall to wish Gawain happiness.

Knight 1: 
   They are coming. 
Knight 2:
   Happy couple, happy forever.

Narrator: 
Then Gawain and his bride walked into the hall and the cheers died away. Lady Ragnell was dressed in a beautiful gown. But nothing could hide her terrible face. Gawain was pale. But he held his head high.
For a moment no sound was heard. Later…the kind Sir Lancelot..

Sir Lancelot: 
Welcome, my lady, Good wishes to you and our dear friend Gawain.
 
Narrator:   
Then everyone began to talk brightly and laugh loudly to pretended to be happy. This is a wedding after all! But they kept their eyes away from Lady Ragnell until the feast was over when Ragnell had gone off to their tower room. Sir Gawain sat all alone until everyone had left.

Sir Gawain:
   What can I do? How I wish I could saddle my horse and ride away. FOREVER! …..But NOT as a knight of the Round Table. All I can do is keep my promise and have to be a good husband.

Narrator:  
Gawain climbed the stairs to the tower and slowly opened the door. In the dark, he saw Lady Ragnell sitting in a chair by the fire.

Lady Ragnell:
   Greetings, husband! Come, give me a kiss!

Narrator:  
Gawain  thought of his promise and tried to smile at his wife. He closed his eyes and kissed her gently.  

Lady Ragnell: (sweet and soft voice)  
   Thank you, my Gawain.

Narrator:  
Gawain opened his eyes and stepped back. He was amazed to see a beautiful lady in front of him.

Sir Gawain: 
   Who are you? Where is Lady Ragnell?

Lady Ragnell: (held out her hands)
   I am Lady Ragnell. This is my true self. My brother, Sir Malger, put me under an evil spell. Because you were so good and kind, I am partly free now, but only partly. For half of each day, I will look as I do now. But for the other half, I will look as horrible as before.

Narrator:  
Lady Ragnell took Gawain’s hands.

Lady Ragnell:
 So you must choose. And it is a hard choice. I can take my own shape at night or during the day. Which shall it be? Think carefully. If I am a monster by day, you will be shamed before all the world. But if I am a monster at night, you will be not happy in your own home.

Sir Gawain: (looked into her eyes)
   You are the one who must bear this burden. You must choose. Not I.

Lady Ragnell:
   Oh…MY DEAR! Do you remember the riddle? You have let me choose. You have given me what all women want. My own way. Now you have broken the spell forever! I need never take that monster shape again. I can be my true self. Day, night, and always!
 
Narrator:
   Sir Gawain had never felt such joy. And the next day he gave another wedding feast for his bride. It was the happiest ever seen at Camelot.
