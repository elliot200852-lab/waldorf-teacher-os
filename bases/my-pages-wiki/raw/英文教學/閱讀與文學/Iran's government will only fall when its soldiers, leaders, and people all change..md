Iran’s government will only fall 
                              when its soldiers, leaders, and people all change.


1. The police and the army must stop helping the government.

If the police and the army still obey the government,
they can stop protests easily.

But if many soldiers and police officers stop following orders,
or even support the people,
the government becomes very weak.

So, a government usually falls
only when its own security forces no longer protect it.

⸻

2. The leaders inside the government must fight each other.

A government is strong when its leaders work together.

But if leaders start to argue,
split into different groups,
and do not trust each other,
the government becomes weak inside.

When leaders fight each other,
they cannot control the country well,
and people get more chances to change the system.

⸻

3. Many different people must protest for a long time.

If only students or only workers protest,
the government can stop them easily.

But if students, workers, shop owners, farmers, and families
all join together,
and keep protesting for many days or months,
the pressure becomes very strong.

Big change needs many people,
many places,
and a long time.

⸻

4. The people must have new leaders or a new plan.

After a government falls,
someone must lead the country.

If there is no new leader or clear plan,
the country can fall into chaos.

So people need leaders, groups, or plans
that can speak for them
and build a new system.

Without this,
even if the old government falls,
life may not become better.
