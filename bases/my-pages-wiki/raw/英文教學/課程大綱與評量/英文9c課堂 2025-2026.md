人生第一本原汁原味英文小說：《The House on Mango Street》
About the author… 
    Sandra Cisneros was born in Chicago in 1954, the only daughter in a Mexican-American family of seven children. She had many home spaces as her family traveled frequently between Chicago and Mexico, often settling in a different house upon each return. 
    Cisneros started writing at a young age: encouraged by a teacher, she began writing poetry in high school, worked on her school literary magazine, and then even deciding ago study English at University. She went on to work as a teacher, while still investing in her own writing in her spare time.  She published The House on Mango Street in 1984, which was a huge success and even won the American Book Award. 
    Drawing on the unsettled nature of her own childhood, as well as her identity as the only girl growing up with many brothers, the novel creates an vivid picture of one girl’s search for a sense of belonging and identity. 
Why write as a series of vignettes?
She thinks people who are busy working for a living deserve beautiful little stories, because they don’t have much time and are often tired. She has in mind a book that can be opened at any page and will still make sense to the reader who doesn’t know what came before or comes after.
Question:
１、Why did Cisneros decide to write The House on Mango Street as a series of vignettes？
２、If we like to write about your childhood, what comes to your mind first? Would you please tell a short story on one part of your childhood?


Mahatma Gandhi
	1. Mahatma Gandhi was an Indian leader.
	2.He led the movement for India’s independence through non-violence.
	3.His philosophy of peaceful resistance inspired many people around the world.
	4.He is remembered as the “Father of India.”
1. Introduction
Good morning, everyone.
Today, I want to introduce a very famous leader, Mahatma Gandhi.
He was born in India in 1869.
People call him the “Father of India.”
⸻
2. Main Achievements
Gandhi studied law in England and later returned to India.
He saw that his people were not free under British rule.
He started to fight for India’s independence, but he used non-violence and peaceful protests.
His famous way is called “ahimsa,” which means “no harm.”
Because of him, India became free in 1947.
⸻
3. Touching Story
One touching story is about the Salt March in 1930.
The British government forced Indian people to buy salt only from them, with a high tax.
Gandhi walked about 390 kilometers with many people to the sea.
They made their own salt peacefully, to show they wanted freedom.
This story shows Gandhi’s courage and his peaceful spirit.
⸻
4. Conclusion
Gandhi taught the world that we can fight for justice without violence.
His life still inspires people today to choose peace over war.
Thank you.

There Was an Old Woman She Had So Many Children She Didn’t Know What to Do
從前有個老太太，她的孩子多到讓她根本不知道該怎麼辦。

Rosa Vargas’ kids are too many and too much.
羅莎．瓦加斯的孩子又多，又難管。

It’s not her fault you know, except she is their mother and only one against so many.
說真的這也不能全怪她，一個媽媽要對付那麼多小孩，實在太吃力。

They are bad those Vargases, and how can they help it with only one mother who is tired all the time from buttoning and bottling and babying,
那些瓦加斯家的孩子很失控，可是他們只有一個媽媽，她整天在穿扣子、裝奶瓶、照顧小孩，累得要命，

and who cries every day for the man who left without even leaving a dollar for bologna or a note explaining how come.
而且那個男人一走了之，連一塊買火腿的錢或一張說明為什麼要離開的紙條都沒留下，讓她每天哭。

The kids bend trees and bounce between cars and dangle upside down from knees
那些小孩把樹壓彎、在車子中間亂竄，還倒掛在膝蓋上晃來晃去，

and almost break like fancy museum vases you can’t replace.
一不小心就好像會摔碎成為無法修補的博物館花瓶。

They think it’s funny.
他們覺得這樣很好玩。

They are without respect for all things living, including themselves.
他們對所有活著的東西都沒有尊重，包括他們自己。

But after a while you get tired of being worried about kids who aren’t even yours.
可是久了之後，你也會對那些根本不是自己小孩的孩子擔心到累了。

One day they are playing chicken on Mr. Benny’s roof.
有一天，他們在班尼先生的屋頂上玩「膽小鬼遊戲」（看誰先害怕）。

Mr. Benny says, Hey ain’t you kids know better than to be swinging up there?
班尼先生說：嘿，你們不知道不可以在那上面亂晃嗎？

Come down, you come down right now, and then they just spit.
「快下來，馬上給我下來。」結果他們只是不屑地吐口水。

See. That’s what I mean.
你看，這就是我說的。

No wonder everybody gave up.
難怪大家最後都放棄了。

Just stopped looking out when little Efren chipped his buck tooth on a parking meter
後來小艾弗連的門牙撞在停車收費器上崩了一角，大家也不再多看一眼；

and didn’t even stop Refugia from getting her head stuck between two slats in the back gate
也沒人去阻止芮芙琪亞，把頭卡在後門兩根木條的縫縫裡；

and nobody looked up not once the day Angel Vargas learned to fly and dropped from the sky like a sugar donut, just like a falling star,
甚至在那天，安赫爾．瓦加斯「學會飛」，像糖霜甜甜圈、像流星一樣，從天上掉下來時，也沒人抬頭看一眼。

and exploded down to earth without even an “Oh.”
他重重摔到地上，連一句「喔！」的驚呼都沒有。

Q1.Why are the Vargas children so wild and out of control?
為什麼瓦加斯家的孩子這麼失控？

A (要點)：Because they have only one mother who is always tired and alone, and their father left them with no money or explanation.
因為家裡只有一個累壞了的媽媽要照顧一大群小孩，爸爸又不告而別、沒留下錢也沒解釋，缺乏大人的照顧與支持。

Q2. How does the neighborhood change its attitude toward the Vargas children?
鄰居對瓦加斯家孩子的態度後來有什麼改變？

A (要點)：At first people worry and watch them, but after a while they get tired and give up watching or helping.
一開始大家還會擔心、留意他們，但過了一陣子，鄰居都累了，就不再管、不再幫忙了。

Q3.What happens to Angel Vargas, and what does “without even an ‘Oh’” show?
安赫爾．瓦加斯發生了什麼事？最後那句「連一句『喔』都沒有」表達了什麼？

A (要點)：Angel Vargas falls from the sky and dies (or is badly hurt), but nobody reacts or is shocked. The phrase shows that people have become numb and don’t value the children’s lives anymore.
安赫爾從高處掉下來，死亡（或重傷），卻沒有人驚呼或在意。這句話顯示大家已經麻木，不再真正重視這些孩子的生命。
