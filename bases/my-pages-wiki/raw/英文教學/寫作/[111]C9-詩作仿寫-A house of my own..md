A House of My own

    Not a flat. Not an apartment in back. Not a man's house. Not a daddy's. A house all my own. With my porch and my pillow, my pretty purple petunias. My books and my stories. My two shoes waiting beside the bed. Nobody to shake a stick at. Nobody's garbage to pick up after.
    Only a house quiet as snow, a space for myself to go, clean as paper before the poem.

A Room of My own

    Not a desk. Not a table in the corner. Not a man's chair. Not a daddy's. A room all my own. With my lamp and my notebook, my favorite pens. My books and my journals. My two slippers waiting beside the bed. Nobody to disturb me.    Nobody's mess to clean up after.
    Only a room quiet as a library, a space for myself to write, clear as the page before the words.

A School of My own 

Not a clone. Not a copy of someone else. Not a man's school. Not a daddy's. A school of my own. With my own curriculum, my own style of teaching. My passions and my knowledge. My own two feet leading me where I want to go. Nobody to decide what to learn. Nobody's standards to meet.
Only a school big as the ocean, a space for my ideas to grow, unique as a fingerprint.
