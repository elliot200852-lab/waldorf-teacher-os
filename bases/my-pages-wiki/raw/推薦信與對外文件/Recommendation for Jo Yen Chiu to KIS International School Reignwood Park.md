Dear Admissions Team,  
KIS International School Reignwood Park,

I am honored to write this letter in strong support of my student, Jo Yen Chiu (Olivia Chiu), whom I have known closely as her homeroom teacher from Grade 6 through Grade 9. I am also her English teacher and Humanities and Social Studies teacher, working with her across both language and humanities learning for many years.

Over these four years, I have witnessed Olivia’s growth from early adolescence into a confident, independent learner. She is naturally sunny, open-hearted, and generous in spirit. Her presence is warm and steady, and she carries herself with what we often call “a general’s grace”—not loud authority, but calm confidence grounded in real ability.

Academically, Olivia is intelligent and strategic in her learning. She quickly grasps the core of a problem, understands how to read questions accurately, and knows how to approach tasks efficiently.

As her English teacher, I can confidently say that Olivia has strong language talent. Her expressive personality blends beautifully with her linguistic ability—she is articulate, emotionally aware, and intuitive with tone and rhythm. Her English performance has consistently been impressive, especially in speaking, writing, and creative expression.

What truly sets Olivia apart is her artistic sensitivity. Whether in creative thinking, visual design, or painting, she consistently produces work that surprises and moves others.

Olivia actively seeks meaningful roles. She volunteered as director and stage manager in our graduation drama, and often initiates class activities by blending organization with creativity.

Like all adolescents, she sometimes shows moments of distraction. Yet when guided, she reflects quickly and corrects herself gracefully.

Socially, Olivia is comfortable with both adults and peers. She has strong opinions and is not easily influenced by peer pressure, often becoming an opinion leader while remaining sensitive to others.

Because of her language ability, artistic mind, and international outlook, I believe Olivia is especially suited to studying in a global environment.I recommend Olivia to your school with my fullest confidence. If you need any further information, please feel free to contact me.

Sincerely,

Cixin Waldorf High School, Taiwan.
