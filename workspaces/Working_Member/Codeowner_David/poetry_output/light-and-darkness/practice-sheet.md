# Practice Sheet

**Topic:** light and darkness

## Part 1: Vocabulary Matching
Match each word with its definition.

| # | Word | Definition |
|---|------|------------|
| 1 | light | Visible electromagnetic radiation. The human eye can typically detect radiation (light) in the wavelength range of about 400 to 750 nanometers. Nearby shorter and longer wavelength ranges, although not visible, are commonly called ultraviolet and infrared light. |
| 2 | darkness | The state of being dark; lack of light. |
| 3 | shade | Darkness where light, particularly sunlight, is blocked. |
| 4 | dark | Having an absolute or (more often) relative lack of light. |
| 5 | dusk | A period of time at the end of day when the sun is below the horizon but before the full onset of night, especially the darker part of twilight. |
| 6 | shades | Darkness where light, particularly sunlight, is blocked. |
| 7 | black | The colour/color perceived in the absence of light, but also when no light is reflected, but rather absorbed. |
| 8 | daylight | The light from the Sun, as opposed to that from any other source. |
