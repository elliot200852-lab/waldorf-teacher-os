# Practice Sheet

**Topic:** the road

## Part 1: Vocabulary Matching
Match each word with its definition.

| # | Word | Definition |
|---|------|------------|
| 1 | road | A way used for travelling between places, originally one wide enough to allow foot passengers and horses to travel, now (US) usually one surfaced with asphalt or concrete and designed to accommodate many vehicles travelling in both directions. In the UK both senses are heard: a country road is the same as a country lane. |
| 2 | route | A course or way which is traveled or passed. |
| 3 | moving | To change place or posture; to go, in any manner, from one place or position to another. |
| 4 | traveling | To be on a journey, often for pleasure or business and with luggage; to go from one place to another. |
| 5 | highway | A motor vehicle for transporting large numbers of people along roads. |
| 6 | via | A main road or highway, especially in ancient Rome. (Mainly used in set phrases, below.) |
| 7 | traffic | Pedestrians or vehicles on roads, or the flux or passage thereof. |
| 8 | transportation | The act of transporting, or the state of being transported; conveyance, often of people, goods etc. |

## Part 2: Sound & Rhyme
These words rhyme with **road**: goad, abode, load, bestowed, mode, ode

**Task:** Choose 3 rhyming words and write a short verse (2-4 lines).

## Part 3: Poetry Response

Answer ONE of the following:
1. What image stayed with you after reading? Describe it.
2. Choose one line that speaks to you. Why?
3. If this poem were a colour, what would it be? Explain.

## Part 4: Word Associations (Creative Writing)
When people hear **road**, they think of: roundabout, motorway, connects, intersection, turnpike, interchange

**Task:** Pick 2-3 words from the list above and write a short paragraph (5-8 sentences) that connects them.
