# 1. The Crusades
# 1. 十字軍東征

Think how many times there have been invasions of Britain: the Romans,
the Anglo-Saxons, the Danes, the Normans. Each one of these invaders
has left their mark on the English language. For example, from Roman
times comes the word 'master', from the Latin magister, meaning a superior person. From the Anglo-Saxons come nearly all words about farming: wheat, rye, oats, horse, cow, house. From the Danes come most of
the words beginning with 'sk': sky, skin,
skull, skill. From the Normans
come French words like mutton (from mouton, sheep), pork (from porc,
pig), and court. Our language is really a mixture of many languages, and
this is also the reason for the difficult spelling, for it is also a mixture of
different spellings. In Anglo-Saxon words 'ou' is pronounced as in 'house',
but in Norman words the pronunciation is as in 'court'.

想想不列顛曾經遭受過多少次入侵：羅馬人、盎格魯撒克遜人、丹麥人、諾曼人。每一批入侵者都在英語中留下了印記。例如，「master」這個字來自羅馬時代，源於拉丁文 magister，意指上位者。盎格魯撒克遜人帶來了幾乎所有與農業相關的詞彙：wheat（小麥）、rye（黑麥）、oats（燕麥）、horse（馬）、cow（牛）、house（房屋）。丹麥人帶來了大多數以「sk」開頭的字：sky（天空）、skin（皮膚）、skull（頭骨）、skill（技能）。諾曼人帶來了法語詞彙，如 mutton（羊肉，來自法語 mouton）、pork（豬肉，來自法語 porc）、以及 court（宮廷）。我們的語言實際上是多種語言的混合體，這也是拼寫困難的原因，因為它同時也是不同拼寫方式的混合體。在盎格魯撒克遜語的字中，「ou」的發音如同「house」裡的發音，但在諾曼語的字中，發音則如同「court」。

But there are also words in our language that come from a people
who never invaded Britain, and these are the Arabs. We have Arabic
words in our language such as alcohol, sugar and sofa. These words
came into our language through the Crusades, and we shall look at how
the Crusades began.

然而，我們的語言中也有一些字來自從未入侵過不列顛的民族，那就是阿拉伯人。我們語言中有阿拉伯語詞彙，如 alcohol（酒精）、sugar（糖）和 sofa（沙發）。這些字透過十字軍東征進入了我們的語言，接下來我們就來看看十字軍東征是如何開始的。

The Christian religion meant much to people in Western Europe. Poor
farmers willingly gave one tenth (the tithe) of their crop to the monks;
knights and kings made great gifts of land to monasteries and churches,
and people spared no money or effort to build their churches as beautifully as they could. But this was not all. In every Christian land there were
people who felt the most wonderful thing in life would be to see with
their own eyes the place where Jesus Christ had walked: the Holy Land
and Jerusalem, the Holy City. A person who had this great longing to see
the Holy Land had to make a very long and arduous journey in those
days. It took years before they saw their homeland again. Such a person
was called a pilgrim, and the journey was called a pilgrimage. A pilgrim
often wore special clothes that told everyone that this was a pilgrim who
must be helped with food, shelter or money, for it was a Christian's duty
to assist a pilgrim. The pilgrims wore a hat with shells on it and a garment that reached down to their ankles. They carried a little satchel,
called a scrip, on a belt and a long staff with a cross on top. In those days,
when people felt that their religion was the most important thing in life,
there were always hundreds of people coming or going on that great
journey.

基督教信仰對西歐人民意義重大。貧窮的農民心甘情願地將收成的十分之一（什一稅）獻給修士；騎士和國王將大片土地贈予修道院和教堂，人們不惜花費金錢和心力，將教堂建得盡可能美麗。但這還不是全部。在每一個基督教國度裡，都有人覺得一生中最美好的事，就是親眼看看耶穌基督曾經走過的地方：聖地和聖城 Jerusalem。在那個年代，一個懷抱強烈渴望想要前往聖地的人，必須踏上一段漫長而艱辛的旅程。要等好多年才能再見到自己的故鄉。這樣的人被稱為朝聖者（pilgrim），而這段旅程被稱為朝聖之旅（pilgrimage）。朝聖者通常穿著特殊的衣服，讓每個人都知道這是一位朝聖者，必須提供食物、住所或金錢來幫助他，因為幫助朝聖者是每個基督徒的責任。朝聖者戴著裝飾貝殼的帽子，穿著長及腳踝的衣袍。他們腰帶上掛著一個小袋子，稱為 scrip，手持一根頂端有十字架的長杖。在那個人們認為信仰是生命中最重要之事的年代，總是有成百上千的人正在踏上或正在返回那偉大的旅途。

The Arabs, who now controlled the Holy Land and Jerusalem, were
Muslims, but they allowed these Christian pilgrims to come to Jerusalem,
to say their prayers at the holy places and return again to their homelands. But around the time of the Battle of Hastings, when William the
Conqueror and his Normans invaded England, something happened in
the faraway lands of the East.

當時控制聖地和 Jerusalem 的阿拉伯人是穆斯林，但他們允許這些基督徒朝聖者來到 Jerusalem，在聖地祈禱，然後返回家鄉。但大約在 Hastings 戰役、征服者 William 和他的諾曼人入侵英格蘭的同一時期，遙遠的東方發生了一件事。

From the East, from Asia, came the Seljuk Turks, who had long ago
embraced Islam, so they did not attack the Arab kingdom for religious
reasons, but for reasons of conquest. When Jerusalem fell into their
hands in 1071, the Seljuks stabled their horses in the places holy to
Christians and no longer allowed pilgrims to come.

來自東方、來自亞洲的塞爾柱突厥人（Seljuk Turks）很早就皈依了伊斯蘭教，因此他們攻打阿拉伯王國並非出於宗教原因，而是為了征服。當 Jerusalem 在 1071 年落入他們手中時，塞爾柱人在基督徒的聖地裡養馬，不再允許朝聖者前來。

When news of this reached the Christians in Europe, it came as a terrible shock. In 1095, Pope Urban II called a great council in the city of
Clermont in France. It was a mighty gathering. Lords and noblemen who
commanded thousands of men, knights who had only a handful of vassals, kings and bishops, monks and peasants, people from every country.
Pope Urban held a sermon and called upon them to take up arms and
drive the Seljuks from the Holy Land.

當這個消息傳到歐洲的基督徒耳中時，帶來了巨大的震動。1095 年，教宗 Urban 二世在法國 Clermont 城召開了一場盛大的宗教會議。那是一場聲勢浩大的聚會。統領數千人的領主和貴族、只有少數封臣的騎士、國王和主教、修士和農民，來自各個國家的人們齊聚一堂。教宗 Urban 發表了一篇講道，號召他們拿起武器，將塞爾柱人從聖地驅逐出去。

'Consider,
therefore,' he said, 'that the Almighty has created you for
this purpose, that through you he may restore Jerusalem.'

他說：「因此，你們要明白，全能的上帝創造你們正是為了這個目的，好讓祂能透過你們收復 Jerusalem。」

And like a roar of thunder there came from all these thousands of
men the cry: 'Dieu le volt (God wills it).'* As a sign that they devoted their
lives and all their possessions to the task of fighting for the Holy Land,
they sewed red strips of cloth in the form of a cross on their breast and
shoulders. It was the sign of the War of the Cross, or the Crusade as they
called it.

如同雷鳴般的吶喊從這數千人中爆發而出：「Dieu le volt（上帝的旨意）！」*作為將生命和所有財產奉獻給為聖地而戰的標誌，他們在胸前和肩上縫上了紅色十字形的布條。這就是十字架之戰的標誌，也就是人們所稱的十字軍東征（Crusade）。

From the Council of Clermont the knights hastened home and prepared themselves for the long journey. Many of them sold their land and
their castles in order to have money for the expedition. Not only knights,
but also villagers and serfs who worked for the knights, left their ploughs
to become Crusaders, and no master dared to hold them back. Merchants left their shops, shepherds their flocks, men with wives and children left their families to the care of God and set out. Monks went to
every village to tell people about the Crusade. Because this call to arms
was met with such an enthusiastic response from all levels of society, the

從 Clermont 會議結束後，騎士們匆忙趕回家，為漫長的旅程做準備。他們中的許多人賣掉了土地和城堡，以籌措遠征的費用。不僅是騎士，為騎士工作的村民和農奴也放下了犁，成為十字軍戰士，沒有任何主人敢阻攔他們。商人離開了店鋪，牧羊人拋下了羊群，有妻兒的男人把家人託付給上帝便啟程出發。修士們走遍每一個村莊，向人們宣傳十字軍東征。由於這場武裝號召得到了社會各階層如此熱烈的回應，

# 2. Godfrey de Bouillon

Before we look further at the so-called Prince's Crusade, we must try to
understand why hundreds of thousands of people in Britain and France,
in Germany and Italy, were willing to leave their homes, their families
and their possessions, to set out on a long and terrible journey that
would bring them great hardship and, perhaps, death in battle.

在我們進一步了解所謂的諸侯十字軍之前，我們必須試著理解，為什麼不列顛和法蘭西、德意志和義大利的數十萬人願意離開家園、家人和財產，踏上一段漫長而可怕的旅程——一段會帶來巨大苦難、甚至可能在戰場上喪命的旅程。

How was it that hundreds of thousands of men from every walk of life
- knights and villagers, merchants and tradesmen - willingly and joyfully,
pinned the red Crusader's cross on their breasts and set out on a journey
from which, as they knew, many would not return?

為什麼來自各行各業的數十萬人——騎士和村民、商人和工匠——心甘情願、甚至滿懷喜悅地在胸前別上紅色的十字軍十字架，踏上一段他們明知許多人將無法歸來的旅途？

First, there was a feeling of anger that the most holy place for any
Christian - the tomb where Christ had risen from the dead - should be in
the hands of the people who desecrated it by stabling horses there, and
who would not allow any Christian to come to Jerusalem. To them it was
shameful to let such a thing happen, and they considered it a holy duty
to wrest holy places from the grip of the Seljuk Turks. For most Crusaders this was the main reason. They believed that they were doing the
will of God by joining the Crusade.

首先，有一股憤怒的情緒——對任何基督徒而言最神聖的地方——基督從死裡復活的墓穴——竟然落入褻瀆聖地、在那裡養馬、且不允許任何基督徒來到 Jerusalem 的人手中。對他們來說，坐視這種事情發生是一種恥辱，他們認為從塞爾柱突厥人手中奪回聖地是一項神聖的責任。對大多數十字軍戰士來說，這是主要的原因。他們相信參加十字軍東征是在執行上帝的旨意。

But for some Crusaders there were also other reasons. Let us take
Fred, a villager. Fred certainly wanted to do the will of God and help take
back the Holy Land from the Seljuks. But he also thought that by joining
the Crusaders he could get away from the endless hard work he did for
his master and lord. Perhaps he would even gain a knighthood for himself by brave deeds in battle. And who knew what riches could be taken
from the enemy? In the Christian lands there was very little gold, but in
the East there was a great deal of it. So for Fred, there was not only the
holy duty of fighting the Seljuks, there was also the hope of freedom -
that he might come back a knight, a free man, with a lot of gold. There
were hundreds of thousands of villagers and serfs like Fred.

但對某些十字軍戰士來說，還有其他原因。讓我們以一個名叫 Fred 的村民為例。Fred 當然想要執行上帝的旨意，幫助從塞爾柱人手中奪回聖地。但他也想到，加入十字軍可以讓他擺脫為主人和領主做的無止盡的苦工。也許他甚至能透過戰場上的英勇事蹟為自己贏得騎士頭銜。而且誰知道能從敵人那裡奪取什麼財富呢？在基督教國度裡黃金很少，但在東方卻有大量黃金。所以對 Fred 來說，不僅有與塞爾柱人作戰的神聖責任，還有對自由的渴望——他可能帶著大量黃金、以騎士和自由人的身分回來。像 Fred 這樣的村民和農奴有數十萬人。

A knight like Godfrey de Bouillon, however, had no wish to enrich himself by going on a crusade. Yet for Godfrey, too, there was not only the
holy duty to take back the Holy Land, there was also another reason. We
could have heard this reason if we had been present at a meeting Godfrey had with his closest friends. At this meeting Godfrey said: 'We are all
friends here and so I can speak freely without fear that what I say will be
betrayed to monks and priests, and what I want to say is this: I am sure
you all feel
as I do that our Christian Church, which we love wholeheartedly, is not what it should be, for it is under the absolute authority
of the Pope in Rome. No one is allowed to think for themselves, no one is
allowed to ask questions about religion or the Church; if anyone does so,
he is called a bad Christian and threatened with punishment. Kings and
lords have to obey the wishes of the Pope in worldly matters as well as in
religion. Surely, this is not what Christ wanted. But in the East, in Greece,
in Constantinople there are Christians who do not recognise the Pope.
Perhaps we can learn from them. Perhaps, when we have taken
Jerusalem back from the Seljuks, we shall be able to make the Holy City
the centre of a new Christian Church, a Church independent of Rome
and independent of the Pope. My friends, let us hope that, in time, the
new free Church of Jerusalem will take the place of the unfree Church of
Rome where everyone is under authority of the Pope.'

然而，像 Godfrey de Bouillon 這樣的騎士，並不想藉十字軍東征來發財致富。但對 Godfrey 來說，同樣不只有奪回聖地的神聖責任，還有另一個原因。如果我們曾經在場參加 Godfrey 與他最親密朋友的一次會面，我們就能聽到這個原因。在那次會面中，Godfrey 說：「我們在這裡都是朋友，所以我可以暢所欲言，不用擔心我說的話會被洩露給修士和神父。我想說的是：我相信你們都和我有同樣的感受——我們全心熱愛的基督教會，並不是它應該有的樣子，因為它處於羅馬教宗的絕對權威之下。沒有人被允許獨立思考，沒有人被允許對宗教或教會提出疑問；如果有人這樣做，就會被稱為壞基督徒，並受到懲罰的威脅。國王和領主在世俗事務和宗教事務上都必須服從教宗的意願。這肯定不是基督所願的。但在東方、在希臘、在 Constantinople，有不承認教宗的基督徒。也許我們可以向他們學習。也許，當我們從塞爾柱人手中奪回 Jerusalem 之後，我們能讓聖城成為一個新基督教會的中心——一個獨立於羅馬、獨立於教宗的教會。朋友們，讓我們期望，終有一天，Jerusalem 的新自由教會將取代羅馬那個人人受教宗權威管轄的不自由教會。」

This is what Godfrey said to his closest friends who all thought as he
did. For them this hope of a new Church was another reason to join a
Crusade. As we will see later on, this hope was not fulfilled, but it was in
the hearts of some knights when they set out.

這就是 Godfrey 對他最親密的朋友們說的話，他們都與他有同樣的想法。對他們來說，建立新教會的希望是加入十字軍東征的另一個原因。正如我們後來將會看到的，這個希望並未實現，但在某些騎士出發時，這個希望確實存在於他們心中。

So among both villagers and knights there were all kinds of reasons to
join the Crusades besides the religious fervour to take back the Holy
Land.

因此，無論是村民還是騎士，除了奪回聖地的宗教熱情之外，加入十字軍東征還有各式各樣的原因。

Now Godfrey de Bouillon was in command of the second wave of Crusaders, a great army of a hundred thousand men, mainly French and
German. The knights of this army had sold their land and castles to have
money to pay for their journey. They marched through France, Germany,
Hungary, through the Balkan peninsula and Greece, then they passed
through Constantinople. Unfortunately, the Crusaders did not remain
friendly with the Christian people of Constantinople. When they saw all
the gold and wealth there many soldiers were driven to rob and plunder.

Godfrey de Bouillon 指揮第二波十字軍——一支十萬人的大軍，主要由法蘭西人和德意志人組成。這支軍隊中的騎士們賣掉了土地和城堡，以籌措旅費。他們行軍穿過法蘭西、德意志、匈牙利，穿過巴爾幹半島和希臘，然後通過 Constantinople。不幸的是，十字軍並未與 Constantinople 的基督徒人民保持友好。當他們看到那裡所有的黃金和財富時，許多士兵禁不住誘惑去搶劫掠奪。

When the Crusaders came to Syria, the country north of the Holy
Land, they had their first battles. But they also encountered other enemies more fearsome than the Turks. The worst of these enemies was the
hot climate. The heavy, iron armour that the Crusaders wore became a
ghastly burden in the scorching sun. On top of that, the Crusaders were
not used to washing themselves properly, and soon infectious diseases
broke out that killed thousands of Crusaders as they had no doctors to
treat them.

當十字軍來到聖地北方的 Syria 時，他們經歷了第一場戰鬥。但他們也遭遇了比突厥人更可怕的敵人。這些敵人中最糟糕的是炎熱的氣候。十字軍穿戴的沉重鐵甲在灼熱的烈日下成了可怕的負擔。雪上加霜的是，十字軍不習慣適當地清洗身體，很快就爆發了傳染病，由於沒有醫生治療，數千名十字軍因此喪命。

For three years the Crusaders battled through Syria; they lost more
men through epidemics than through fighting. When at long last they
had fought their way to Palestine and came before Jerusalem, there
were only twenty thousand Crusaders left - one hundred thousand had
set out. But for these twenty thousand it was a great day. When they saw
the Holy City of Jerusalem before them, knights and common soldiers
cried out with joy, some sank to their knees and kissed the ground to
thank God for being allowed to see the Holy City.

三年來，十字軍在 Syria 征戰；死於瘟疫的人比死於戰鬥的還多。當他們終於打到了 Palestine，來到 Jerusalem 城前時，只剩下兩萬名十字軍——出發時有十萬人。但對這兩萬人來說，這是偉大的一天。當他們看見聖城 Jerusalem 出現在眼前時，騎士和普通士兵都歡呼雀躍，有些人跪下親吻大地，感謝上帝讓他們得以親眼看見聖城。

But Godfrey de Bouillon was deeply worried. He had only twenty
thousand men and Jerusalem was not only surrounded by high, thick
walls, but also defended by sixty thousand Turks. However, the Crusaders readied themselves to assault the city.

但 Godfrey de Bouillon 深感憂慮。他只有兩萬人，而 Jerusalem 不僅被高聳厚實的城牆環繞，還有六萬突厥人守衛。然而，十字軍仍然準備好了進攻這座城市。

After five days of preparation the first assault was made. The Crusaders carried long rope-ladders with grappling-hooks. They threw the
ladders high up and the hooks gripped the top of the wall, allowing the
Crusaders to climb up. But the Seljuks knew how to deal with them. They
cut the rope of the ladders with their swords and the Crusaders crashed
to the ground. After a day in which they lost many men, the Crusaders
returned to their camp, sadder but also wiser. They realised now that
they had to build siege-towers to take the city. They set to work, knights
working side by side with villagers and serfs as they cut down trees in a
nearby forest. From the wood they built towers on wheels, towers as
high as the walls.

經過五天的準備，第一次攻城開始了。十字軍攜帶著帶有鉤爪的長繩梯。他們將繩梯高高拋起，鉤爪抓住城牆頂端，讓十字軍得以攀爬而上。但塞爾柱人知道如何對付他們。他們用劍砍斷繩梯的繩索，十字軍便摔落地面。經過損失慘重的一天後，十字軍回到營地，更加悲傷但也更加明智了。他們現在意識到必須建造攻城塔才能攻下這座城市。他們開始動工，騎士與村民和農奴並肩作業，在附近的森林砍伐樹木。用這些木材，他們建造了裝有輪子的塔樓，高度與城牆一般高。

And so, at long last, came the day of the assault. But before the assault
was made something else took place. The whole Crusader army formed
a great procession and, well out of reach of Seljuk arrows, they walked
around the walls of Jerusalem, singing hymns and saying prayers. When
they had completed the round, trumpets sounded and the assault
began. The creaking towers with knights on the top were pushed to the
walls. The Seljuks sent hails of arrows against the approaching towers.
They also shot flaming arrows at the towers, and here and there these
flaming arrows set a siege-tower on fire, but other towers reached the
walls. The Seljuks poured buckets of boiling oil onto the Crusaders, but
still they came.

終於，攻城的日子到來了。但在進攻之前，發生了另一件事。整支十字軍組成了一支盛大的隊伍，在塞爾柱人箭矢射不到的安全距離外，繞著 Jerusalem 的城牆行走，一邊唱著聖歌，一邊祈禱。當他們繞城一圈後，號角響起，進攻開始了。搭載著騎士的嘎吱作響的攻城塔被推向城牆。塞爾柱人向逼近的塔樓射出如雨的箭矢。他們還向塔樓射出火箭，這裡那裡有些攻城塔被點燃了，但其他塔樓仍然到達了城牆。塞爾柱人將一桶桶滾燙的油潑向十字軍，但他們仍然前進不止。

Then the bridge of one tower touched the top of the wall, a knight
rushed across - the first to set foot on the wall - it was Godfrey de BouilIon. A great shout: 'Dieu le volt, God wills it,' rose from the Crusaders
when they saw Godfrey defending his foothold against the Seljuks. Then
another knight joined him and another. Now Crusaders from other towers gained the walls. After fierce fighting the Seljuks were driven from
the walls, but now the battle continued in the streets of Jerusalem and
the Crusaders fought for every house.

然後，一座塔樓的吊橋觸及了城牆頂端，一名騎士衝了過去——第一個踏上城牆的人——正是 Godfrey de Bouillon。當十字軍看見 Godfrey 在城牆上抵禦塞爾柱人時，一聲巨大的呼喊響起：「Dieu le volt，上帝的旨意！」接著又一名騎士加入了他，然後又一名。現在，其他塔樓上的十字軍也攻上了城牆。經過激烈的戰鬥，塞爾柱人被趕下了城牆，但戰鬥隨後在 Jerusalem 的街道上繼續，十字軍為每一棟房屋而戰。

It is sad to say that in this furious battle inside the city, women and
children too were killed by the Crusaders. When the fighting was over
and Jerusalem was in the hands of the Crusaders, Godfrey, the commander, took off his armour and his weapons and, dressed only in the
coarse long shirt of a pilgrim, he walked barefoot to the tomb where
Christ rose from the dead, and remained there in silent prayer.*

令人遺憾的是，在這場城內的激烈戰鬥中，婦女和兒童也被十字軍殺害了。當戰鬥結束、Jerusalem 落入十字軍手中後，統帥 Godfrey 脫下盔甲和武器，僅穿著朝聖者的粗布長衫，赤腳走向基督從死裡復活的墓穴，在那裡靜默禱告。*

The year Jerusalem was taken by the Crusaders was 1099. Most of
those who had set out had died. Sometimes in old churches you find the
tomb of a Crusader knight. On such tombs there is a carving of the
knight with his arms crossed over his chest. If there is a lion under his
feet it means that he died on a Crusade.

十字軍攻下 Jerusalem 的那一年是 1099 年。出發的人中大多數已經死去。有時在古老的教堂裡，你會發現十字軍騎士的墓碑。在這些墓碑上刻著雙臂交叉於胸前的騎士像。如果他腳下有一頭獅子，表示他死於十字軍東征。

Now the Holy Land had become a new Christian country, and the Crusader knights offered Godfrey the crown of this new country, called the
Kingdom of Jerusalem. But Godfrey was too modest, and he refused. And
so his brother, Baldwin, became the first king of Jerusalem. This Kingdom
lasted for eighty-eight years, and in that time Christian pilgrims could
safely come and visit the holy places.

現在聖地成了一個新的基督教國家，十字軍騎士們將這個新國家的王冠獻給 Godfrey，這個國家被稱為 Jerusalem 王國。但 Godfrey 太過謙遜，拒絕了。於是他的兄弟 Baldwin 成為了第一任 Jerusalem 國王。這個王國持續了八十八年，在那段時間裡，基督徒朝聖者可以安全地來訪聖地。

* To this day one can see the hundreds of crosses the Crusader knights
carved in the wall of the church at the tomb.

*時至今日，人們仍然可以在墓穴教堂的牆壁上看到十字軍騎士刻下的數百個十字架。

# 3. Saladin and Richard the Lionheart
# 3. Saladin 與獅心王 Richard

The Crusaders' Kingdom, the Kingdom of Jerusalem, was also called the
Frankish Kingdom, as most of the knights came from France. Many of the
knights had sold their lands and their castles to have money for their
long journey, and when the Crusade was finished they had no home to
go back to. So many knights stayed and built castles for themselves. One
can still see some of these Crusader castles there today.

十字軍的王國——Jerusalem 王國——也被稱為法蘭克王國，因為大多數騎士來自法蘭西。許多騎士為了籌措漫長旅途的費用而賣掉了土地和城堡，當十字軍東征結束後，他們已無家可歸。所以許多騎士留了下來，為自己建造城堡。至今仍能在那裡看到一些十字軍時代的城堡。

Over time they saw how the Muslim Turks and Arabs lived, and they
saw that there were many good things to learn from them. There was
the use of sugar to make things sweet; in Europe people had only known
honey. They found the sugar made from sugarcane much more useful
for cooking. That was how the Arabic word sukkar and the use of sugar
came to us. They also saw that the Turks and Arabs used many spices
with their food: pepper, but also ginger, mustard, cinnamon and many
others. But it was not only the knights in the Holy Land who came to like
spices in their food. People in Europe had to eat meat that was kept in
salt all through the winter to preserve it, which tasted and smelled horrible. But when they heard of spices, they added them too, and the salty
taste and nasty smell disappeared. Soon many people in Europe wanted
to eat heavily spiced food. And so a great trade in spices from the East
began.

隨著時間推移，他們看到了穆斯林突厥人和阿拉伯人的生活方式，發現有許多值得學習的好東西。其中之一是用糖來使食物變甜；在歐洲，人們以前只知道蜂蜜。他們發現從甘蔗製成的糖在烹飪上更為實用。這就是阿拉伯語 sukkar 這個字和糖的使用傳入我們語言的經過。他們還看到突厥人和阿拉伯人在食物中使用許多香料：胡椒，還有薑、芥末、肉桂和許多其他香料。但喜歡在食物中加香料的不只是聖地的騎士。歐洲的人們整個冬天都得吃用鹽醃製保存的肉，味道和氣味都很糟糕。但當他們聽說了香料後，也開始添加使用，鹹味和難聯的氣味就消失了。很快，歐洲許多人都想吃重香料的食物。於是，一場從東方進口香料的龐大貿易就此展開。

But in the long run, the Frankish knights could not keep the Holy
Land. For about eighty-eight years, less than three generations,
they
held the land, but then there arose a Muslim leader who was not only a
great general and warrior, but a man of noble character. This man's
name was Saladin, and he united the Muslims of Egypt, Syria, Yemen and
Palestine,
and became their leader. Under Sultan Saladin, the Muslim
army stormed into the Holy Land and reconquered Jerusalem. But Saladin was a generous man. None of the women or children in Jerusalem
were harmed and they were allowed to leave Jerusalem and to go to
Tyre in the north of the Holy Land, which was still held by Christian
knights. Even the men who were taken prisoner were well treated and if
their families paid a ransom for them, they were released. The Christian
Crusaders had never treated the Muslims so generously.

然而從長遠來看，法蘭克騎士無法守住聖地。大約八十八年——不到三代人的時間——他們統治著這片土地，但隨後出現了一位穆斯林領袖，他不僅是偉大的將軍和戰士，更是一個品格高尚的人。此人名叫 Saladin，他統一了 Egypt、Syria、Yemen 和 Palestine 的穆斯林，成為他們的領袖。在 Sultan Saladin 的率領下，穆斯林軍隊攻入聖地，收復了 Jerusalem。但 Saladin 是一個寬宏大量的人。Jerusalem 城內的婦女和兒童無一受害，他們被允許離開 Jerusalem，前往聖地北部仍由基督教騎士據守的 Tyre。甚至被俘的男子也受到善待，如果他們的家人支付了贖金，便會被釋放。基督教十字軍從未如此寬待穆斯林。

The fall of Jerusalem in 1187 came as bitter news to the people in the
Christian lands of Europe. Again, the call for a Crusade went out and
three powerful rulers agreed to join forces and lead their armies against
Saladin. One was the king of England, Richard the Lionheart,
the other
was Philip, king of France, and the third was Duke Leopold of Austria.

1187 年 Jerusalem 的陷落，對歐洲基督教國度的人民來說是一個痛苦的消息。十字軍東征的號召再次發出，三位強大的統治者同意聯合起來，率領軍隊對抗 Saladin。一位是英格蘭國王獅心王 Richard，另一位是法蘭西國王 Philip，第三位是奧地利公爵 Leopold。

From the beginning, the pride and jealousy between these three
rulers was so strong that they could not work together. They would not
even attack an enemy fortress together. Instead it was arranged that
their forces would attack on alternate days. In the end the fortress surrendered and Saladin paid a great ransom for the safe return of his soldiers. But Philip and Richard the Lionheart quarrelled so fiercely over the
division of the gold, that the French king decided he had had enough of
the Crusade and sailed home with his army.

從一開始，這三位統治者之間的驕傲和嫉妒就強烈到他們無法合作。他們甚至不願一起攻打敵人的堡壘。取而代之的安排是他們的部隊輪流在不同天攻擊。最終堡壘投降了，Saladin 支付了巨額贖金以換回他的士兵安全歸來。但 Philip 和獅心王 Richard 為了分配黃金而激烈爭吵，法蘭西國王決定他受夠了十字軍東征，帶著他的軍隊乘船回國了。

Soon after that Richard the Lionheart had another quarrel with the
duke of Austria. The Austrians had stormed the walls of another enemy
fortress and raised their flag, but Richard came and tore it down. And so
the Austrians, too, left Richard, swearing they would revenge the insult.

不久之後，獅心王 Richard 又與奧地利公爵發生了另一場爭執。奧地利人攻上了另一座敵方堡壘的城牆並插上了他們的旗幟，但 Richard 過來把旗幟扯了下來。於是奧地利人也離開了 Richard，發誓要為這個侮辱復仇。

The English now had to fight Saladin and his armies alone. But Richard
the Lionheart, although he was proud and conceited, was a fearless leader and his knights loved him. There is a little story that shows how
faithful the English knights were to their king as well as showing what a
great and noble person Saladin was.

英格蘭人現在不得不獨自對抗 Saladin 和他的軍隊。但獅心王 Richard，儘管驕傲自負，卻是一位無畏的領袖，他的騎士們深愛著他。有一個小故事既展現了英格蘭騎士對國王的忠誠，也展現了 Saladin 是多麼偉大而高貴的人。

On one occasion Richard the Lionheart and a few knights left the
camp of the English army and went hunting. The hunt took them further
and further away until they were many miles from their soldiers. Suddenly a band of Saladin's horsemen appeared and attacked the group of
hunters. The Crusaders fought valiantly, but they were outnumbered.
Suddenly, one of the English knights called out: 'I am Richard, the King.
Come and fight me, you cowards!' Immediately the horsemen threw
ropes over him to bring this precious prisoner alive to Saladin. While
they were busy with this prisoner, the real Richard and the other knights
galloped away and escaped. The brave knight was brought before Saladin who immediately recognised that the prisoner was
not King
Richard. The knight told Saladin what he had done. Saladin praised him
and sent a message saying that in exchange for ten Muslim prisoners the
king could have his faithful knight back. Richard agreed gladly.

有一次，獅心王 Richard 和幾名騎士離開英軍營地去打獵。狩獵讓他們越走越遠，直到離部隊好幾哩。突然，一隊 Saladin 的騎兵出現並襲擊了這群獵人。十字軍英勇戰鬥，但寡不敵眾。突然間，一名英格蘭騎士大喊：「我是 Richard 國王。來跟我戰鬥吧，你們這群懦夫！」騎兵們立刻用繩索套住他，要把這個珍貴的俘虜活著帶到 Saladin 面前。趁他們忙著處理這名俘虜時，真正的 Richard 和其他騎士策馬逃脫了。這位勇敢的騎士被帶到 Saladin 面前，Saladin 立刻認出這名俘虜並非 Richard 國王。騎士告訴了 Saladin 他所做的事。Saladin 讚揚了他，並傳話說只要交換十名穆斯林俘虜，國王就可以換回他忠誠的騎士。Richard 欣然同意了。

For a whole year the English Crusaders battled against Saladin. But despite their courage, this small army could not regain Jerusalem. They
came so near that they could see the Holy City in the distance, but they
could not come any closer, they could not defeat Saladin.

整整一年，英格蘭十字軍與 Saladin 戰鬥。但儘管他們英勇無畏，這支小軍隊仍無法奪回 Jerusalem。他們離得那麼近，已能在遠方看見聖城，但卻無法再靠近一步，無法擊敗 Saladin。

In that year of battles Richard and Saladin had learned not only to respect each other but even to think very highly of each other. And so they
came together to make peace. Saladin, of course, would
not give up
Jerusalem, it remained in the hands of the Muslims, but he agreed that
Christian pilgrims could come to Jerusalem unhindered and could worship in the holy places. That was all Richard could achieve, and more than
he could hope for because Saladin might just as easily have kept the pilgrims out.

在那一年的戰鬥中，Richard 和 Saladin 不僅學會了彼此尊重，甚至對彼此懷有崇高的敬意。於是他們會面議和。Saladin 當然不會放棄 Jerusalem，它仍留在穆斯林手中，但他同意基督徒朝聖者可以暢通無阻地來到 Jerusalem，在聖地禮拜。這是 Richard 所能取得的一切成果，甚至已超出他所能期望的——因為 Saladin 大可以輕易地將朝聖者拒於門外。

So Richard set out for England and for home. But his troubles were
not yet over. The ship in which he sailed was wrecked in a storm and he
barely escaped with his life. Disguised
as a pilgrim he made his way
through Greece, the Balkans and up the River Danube through Austria.
But there he was recognised by a man who had been in the Crusade,
and the duke of Austria had
not forgotten the insult done to him.
Richard the Lionheart was taken
and imprisoned in a castle on the
Danube.

於是 Richard 啟程返回英格蘭和家鄉。但他的麻煩還沒有結束。他乘坐的船在暴風雨中失事，他險些喪命。他偽裝成朝聖者，穿過希臘、巴爾幹半島，沿 Danube 河而上穿過奧地利。但在那裡，他被一個曾參加過十字軍東征的人認出來了，而奧地利公爵也沒有忘記對他的侮辱。獅心王 Richard 被捕，囚禁在 Danube 河畔的一座城堡裡。

His people in England had
no news from him. They did not know
whether he was alive or dead. But Richard had a faithful friend, a minstrel called Blondin who went to search for his king. He went from castle
to castle singing a tune he and King Richard had often sung together.
After many months he came to Castle Dürnstein where Richard was kept,
and when Blondin sang the first bars of the tune he heard a voice inside
the castle sing the next bars. Now he knew where King Richard was. He
hastened back to England and the English lords offered such a great ransom to the duke of Austria that he agreed to let Richard return.

英格蘭的人民沒有他的任何消息。他們不知道他是生是死。但 Richard 有一位忠實的朋友，一位名叫 Blondin 的吟遊詩人，他出發去尋找他的國王。他從一座城堡走到另一座城堡，唱著他和 Richard 國王經常一起唱的曲調。經過好幾個月，他來到了關押 Richard 的 Dürnstein 城堡，當 Blondin 唱出曲調的前幾小節時，他聽到城堡裡傳來一個聲音接著唱出了後面的小節。現在他知道 Richard 國王在哪裡了。他急忙趕回英格蘭，英格蘭的貴族們向奧地利公爵提出了一筆巨額贖金，公爵同意讓 Richard 回國。

# 4. The Changes in Europe
# 4. 歐洲的變遷

The Crusades started with enthusiasm. Noblemen sold their lands, fathers left their families, knights and villagers went willingly to give their
life in order to rescue the Holy Land from the Muslims. But what happened later? After they had taken Jerusalem the Crusaders who had settled in the Holy Land lived more like the people in the East, and the Crusaders from Europe quarrelled so much amongst themselves that they
could not defeat the Muslims.

十字軍東征在一片熱情中開始。貴族們賣掉土地，父親們離開家庭，騎士和村民心甘情願地獻出生命，為了從穆斯林手中拯救聖地。但後來發生了什麼？在攻下 Jerusalem 之後，定居聖地的十字軍越來越像東方的人一樣生活，而來自歐洲的十字軍之間爭吵不休，以至於無法擊敗穆斯林。

In the end the Holy Land was retaken by the Muslims, and of the Crusaders' Kingdom, the Kingdom of Jerusalem, nothing remained except a
few ruined castles. Although the Crusades were a terrible waste of
human lives - a great effort and a great suffering - some good did eventually come from them. While the people in Europe had hoped in vain to
gain the Holy Land, Europe gained a great deal in the end - it gained
things no one had thought of when the Crusades started.

最終聖地被穆斯林奪回，十字軍的王國——Jerusalem 王國——除了幾座殘破的城堡外什麼也沒留下。雖然十字軍東征是對人命的可怕浪費——巨大的努力和巨大的苦難——但最終還是帶來了一些好處。雖然歐洲人民白白期望獲得聖地，但歐洲最終收穫了很多——收穫了十字軍東征開始時誰也沒有想到的東西。

Before the Crusades, a peasant from France or from Britain had only
ever seen his little piece of land in his homeland and the limited ways of
farming of his father and grandfather. He had lived in a crude cottage
and did not know that life could be different. But then this man 'took the
Cross' and went east and saw how people lived there, and when he came
back again he surprised the people of his village when he opened a little
bag and showed a few seeds, saying: 'We and our fathers before us have
never known any other wheat than one with short stalks and about six
grains on each ear. But in the East I have seen wheat with proud long
stalks and twenty grains or more on each ear. I have brought the seed
grains with me. In a few years' time we shall all have three or four times
more golden yield in our fields - three or four times more food for ourselves!

在十字軍東征之前，來自法蘭西或不列顛的農民只見過家鄉自己那一小塊土地，以及父親和祖父有限的耕作方式。他住在簡陋的茅舍裡，不知道生活可以有所不同。但後來這個人「領了十字架」東征，看到了那裡的人們如何生活。當他回來時，他打開一個小袋子，拿出幾粒種子，讓村裡的人大吃一驚。他說：「我們和我們的祖先從來只知道一種矮稈的小麥，每穗大約六粒穀粒。但在東方，我看到了驕傲的長稈小麥，每穗有二十粒甚至更多的穀粒。我把種子帶回來了。幾年之後，我們田裡的金色收成將是現在的三、四倍——我們的食物將增加三、四倍！」

He also said: 'Another thing I saw was that people in the East use cows'
manure to keep their fields fertile. That's another thing to give us more
and much better food. I have also brought seeds of plants you have
never seen before: cabbage, carrots, spinach, cauliflower. And also fruit
you have never heard of before: apricots, peaches, plums.'

他還說：「我看到的另一件事是，東方的人用牛糞來保持田地肥沃。這也能讓我們有更多、更好的食物。我還帶來了你們從未見過的植物種子：高麗菜、紅蘿蔔、菠菜、花椰菜。還有你們從未聽過的水果：杏桃、桃子、李子。」

The villagers could hardly believe him, but a few years later they saw
his rich yield of corn, his tasty vegetables, his juicy fruit and asked the
Crusader for the seeds of these wonderful plants. So the Crusades
brought better ways of farming, as well as new food to Europe.

村民們簡直不敢相信他，但幾年後他們看到了他豐收的穀物、美味的蔬菜和多汁的水果，紛紛向這位十字軍索要這些奇妙植物的種子。就這樣，十字軍東征為歐洲帶來了更好的耕種方式和新的食物。

Another thing the Crusaders had never seen before was a beautiful
flower-garden. They liked the gardens so much that they brought the
seeds and the art of gardening back to Europe. Certain flowers - tulips,
lilies and carnations - and the love of flowers, the knowledge of when to
plant
each kind, were brought to Europe by the Crusaders. They also
brought home the art of making perfume from flowers. The words sugar,
candy and syrup are Arab words, and the knowledge to make candy and
syrup was brought to Europe by the Crusaders.

另一樣十字軍從未見過的東西是美麗的花園。他們非常喜歡這些花園，於是把種子和園藝技術帶回了歐洲。某些花卉——鬱金香、百合和康乃馨——以及對花卉的熱愛、何時種植各種花卉的知識，都是十字軍帶到歐洲的。他們還帶回了從花卉中提煉香水的技術。Sugar（糖）、candy（糖果）和 syrup（糖漿）都是阿拉伯語詞彙，製作糖果和糖漿的知識也是十字軍帶到歐洲的。

Before the Crusades, people in Europe knew no other seating than
hard chairs or stools. But in the East they saw people recline comfortably
on cushioned seats, called sofas and divans in Arabic. They also saw that
the Arabs did not sleep on the wooden planks of a bed, but put something they called a mattress on the boards. These words as well as these
objects came to Europe through the Crusades.

在十字軍東征之前，歐洲人只知道硬椅子或板凳。但在東方，他們看到人們舒適地斜躺在有軟墊的座椅上，阿拉伯語稱之為 sofa（沙發）和 divan（長沙發）。他們還看到阿拉伯人不是睡在床的木板上，而是在木板上放一種他們稱之為 mattress（床墊）的東西。這些詞彙和這些物品都是透過十字軍東征傳入歐洲的。

The story of paper is also interesting. It was the Chinese who first invented a way to use sawdust and old rags, adding water and acid to
make a soggy mess called 'pulp', which they then spread in thin layers on
a board. When it dried it became a sheet of paper. The Chinese were the
first to have paper. The Arabs, in one of their expeditions to the Far East,
captured some Chinese people and from them they learned how to
make paper. The Crusaders learnt it from the Arabs and brought the
knowledge to Europe.

造紙的故事也很有趣。最早發明用鋸末和舊布加上水和酸液製成糊狀物（稱為「紙漿」）的是中國人，然後他們將紙漿薄薄地鋪在木板上。乾燥後就成了一張紙。中國人是最早擁有紙張的。阿拉伯人在一次遠東遠征中俘虜了一些中國人，並從他們那裡學會了造紙術。十字軍又從阿拉伯人那裡學會了造紙，並將這項知識帶到了歐洲。

But it was a smith in the city of Damascus who discovered that if you
take an iron sword, heat it until it is red hot, and then plunge it into cold
water, the iron becomes not only harder but also more flexible - it becomes steel. The making of steel was another thing brought back by the
Crusaders.

但正是 Damascus 城的一位鐵匠發現，如果把一把鐵劍加熱到通紅，然後浸入冷水中，鐵不僅變得更硬，而且更有彈性——它變成了鋼。煉鋼術是十字軍帶回的另一項技術。

To their surprise, the Crusaders saw Turkish and Arab sailors on their
sea journeys use a little piece of magnetic iron which always pointed
north - a compass. But this too was originally a Chinese invention. From
the Turks the Crusaders learned the use of drums for marching; they
found it was much easier to march to the steady beat of the drum.

令十字軍驚訝的是，他們看到突厥和阿拉伯水手在航海中使用一小塊始終指向北方的磁鐵——指南針。但這原本也是中國人的發明。十字軍從突厥人那裡學會了用鼓來行軍；他們發現跟著穩定的鼓點行進要容易得多。

In the workshops of the East the Crusaders also saw incredible leather
work, beautifully carved and varnished furniture, and cloth dyed in many
colours. All this was unknown in Europe and now became known
through the Crusaders. New crafts grew up in Europe, and soon the people there could make things that the Arabs and Turks liked and were willing to pay for. And so, through trade, money began to come back to Europe.

在東方的工坊裡，十字軍還看到了令人驚嘆的皮革工藝、精美雕刻和上漆的家具，以及染成多種顏色的布料。這一切在歐洲都是聞所未聞的，現在透過十字軍而為人所知。歐洲興起了新的手工藝，不久那裡的人們就能製造出阿拉伯人和突厥人喜歡且願意購買的東西。就這樣，透過貿易，金錢開始流回歐洲。

Through the Crusades great improvements came about in all people's
lives: food, flowers, comforts. But there was also something
else - a
great change in people's minds. The people who went on the first Crusades considered anyone who was not a Christian like themselves to be
an evil person. They could not imagine that anyone who was not a Christian by religion could be kind, generous or in any way a good person.
And because they had this idea in their heads they were merciless in
their battles. But then they came to know such people as Sultan Saladin,
a man who was not a Christian but who had more fairness, nobility and
courage than many a Christian knight. Even Richard the Lionheart - who
did not respect other people easily - felt a deep respect and admiration
for Saladin. People in Europe began to realise that a person is good
through their heart, not because they are a Christian or a Jew or a Muslim, and that one must respect a person for what they are in themselves
and not judge them by their religion.

透過十字軍東征，所有人的生活都獲得了巨大的改善：食物、花卉、舒適的生活。但還有另一件事——人們的心靈發生了巨大的變化。參加第一次十字軍東征的人認為任何不像他們一樣是基督徒的人都是邪惡的。他們無法想像一個非基督徒在宗教上可以是善良、慷慨或在任何方面是好人。因為他們腦中有這種想法，所以在戰鬥中毫不留情。但後來他們認識了像 Sultan Saladin 這樣的人——一個不是基督徒，但比許多基督教騎士更公正、更高貴、更有勇氣的人。甚至不輕易尊重他人的獅心王 Richard 也對 Saladin 懷有深深的敬意和欽佩。歐洲人開始認識到，一個人的善良來自他的心靈，而不是因為他是基督徒、猶太人或穆斯林，人必須尊重一個人的本身，而不是以他的宗教來評判他。

In the East, the Crusaders also met people who were Christians but
did not recognise the authority of the Pope, like the people of Constantinople. Some of them began to wonder if there could be a Church
that was not ruled over by the Pope in Rome.

在東方，十字軍還遇到了像 Constantinople 人那樣身為基督徒但不承認教宗權威的人。其中一些人開始思考，是否可以有一個不受羅馬教宗統治的教會。

Not only material things, but also ideas were exchanged through the
Crusades. The Arabs' knowledge of science, like astronomy or medicine,
stirred the minds of the Europeans. The people of Europe had not
gained the Holy Land, but they had become more awake, more open to
the world. This was the real gain of the Crusades.

透過十字軍東征交流的不僅是物質，還有思想。阿拉伯人的科學知識——如天文學或醫學——激發了歐洲人的思維。歐洲人民雖然沒有獲得聖地，但他們變得更加清醒、更加開放地面對世界。這才是十字軍東征真正的收穫。

Europe had become richer in material things as well as in ideas. Without the Crusades the Christian peoples of Europe might have carried on
in their simple ways without even knowing that life could be different.

歐洲在物質和思想上都變得更加富有。如果沒有十字軍東征，歐洲的基督教人民可能會繼續他們簡樸的生活方式，甚至不知道生活可以有所不同。

# 5. The Growth of Cities
# 5. 城市的興起

Although the Crusades ultimately failed to take control of the Holy Land,
Europe nevertheless gained new knowledge from the cultural exchange
that resulted. The pilgrims and the Crusaders brought back better grain,
new plants to eat (cabbage, cauliflower), new fruit (apricots), new crafts
(glassmaking, leather work), better ways of building, and many other
changes. But the most important of all the changes that came with the
Crusades was the growth of cities.

儘管十字軍東征最終未能控制聖地，歐洲還是從隨之而來的文化交流中獲得了新知識。朝聖者和十字軍帶回了更好的穀物、新的食用植物（高麗菜、花椰菜）、新水果（杏桃）、新工藝（玻璃製造、皮革工藝）、更好的建築方式，以及許多其他變革。但十字軍東征帶來的所有變化中，最重要的是城市的興起。

Roman life had been concentrated in cities, but when the Germanic
tribes destroyed the Roman Empire they also destroyed the cities. The
Germanic tribes did not like to be crowded together, they preferred the
free, open countryside, and so many cities declined or disappeared. People lived in villages, huddling under the protection of a castle, and few
people ventured far from their home.

羅馬的生活集中在城市裡，但當日耳曼部落摧毀羅馬帝國時，他們也摧毀了城市。日耳曼部落不喜歡擁擠在一起，他們偏好自由開闊的鄉間，因此許多城市衰落或消失了。人們住在村莊裡，蜷縮在城堡的保護之下，很少有人敢遠離家鄉。

But when the Crusades came, the little fishermen's village of Venice on
the coast of Italy, for example, suddenly grew. Crusaders and pilgrims
wanted to go by sea to the Holy Land and paid the fishermen to take
them over in their boats. These fares paid by thousands of travellers
made the fishermen rich. Venice grew into a city of wealthy merchants.
And when the people of Europe grew fond of spices in their food, it was
again the merchants of Venice who carried the spices from the East to
Europe and made huge profits. Venice became richer and grew larger, it
could afford its
own army of mercenaries who were paid to fight for
Venice. It had become a powerful city built on business and trade.

但十字軍東征來臨時，例如義大利海岸上的小漁村 Venice 突然發展起來。十字軍和朝聖者想從海路前往聖地，付錢給漁民讓他們用船載運。成千上萬旅客支付的船費使漁民們富裕起來。Venice 發展成為一座富商雲集的城市。當歐洲人開始喜歡在食物中加香料時，又是 Venice 的商人將香料從東方運到歐洲，賺取了巨額利潤。Venice 變得更加富有、規模更大，它有能力養自己的僱傭軍為 Venice 而戰。它已成為一座建立在商業和貿易之上的強大城市。

Similar things happened all over Europe. Pilgrims and Crusaders travelled on rivers, which were quicker and safer than the poor roads of that
time, and where these travellers stopped for a rest or for food, cities
began to grow.

類似的事情在歐洲各地都在發生。朝聖者和十字軍沿河流旅行，河流比當時糟糕的道路更快捷、更安全，而在這些旅客停下來休息或用餐的地方，城市開始成長。

Business and trade were what made new cities rise in Europe. But
there was something else. Before the Crusades, villagers and serfs were
hardly better off than slaves, but a villager or serf who came back from
the Crusades had seen the world. He had perhaps learned a new craft
and had no wish to go back to a life of serfdom to a knight. A serf could
do two things to gain freedom: he could buy his freedom by paying his
master a sum of money (but not many serfs had enough to buy their
freedom), or he could simply run away. Just running away was not much
use, for fleeing from one's lord was a crime punishable by death.
But
there was a special law for the new cities: if a serf or a villager ran away
from his lord and came into a city, and if he lived in the city without
being caught for a year and a day, he was free. His master could neither
claim him nor punish him. The serf had become a free man.

商業和貿易是歐洲新城市興起的動力。但還有別的因素。在十字軍東征之前，村民和農奴的處境幾乎與奴隸無異，但從十字軍東征回來的村民或農奴已經見過了世面。他也許學會了一門新手藝，不想再回到為騎士做農奴的生活。農奴要獲得自由可以做兩件事：他可以付給主人一筆錢來贖身（但沒多少農奴有足夠的錢贖身），或者他可以直接逃跑。但單純逃跑沒多大用處，因為逃離領主是可處以死刑的罪行。然而新城市有一條特殊法律：如果一個農奴或村民從領主那裡逃跑進入城市，並且在城市裡居住一年零一天而未被抓獲，他就自由了。他的主人既不能追討他，也不能懲罰他。農奴變成了自由人。

A good many serfs and villagers escaped to the new cities to live as
free men. In a city, no one could 'own' a fellow man - every member of
the city was a free person, a citizen or burgher, and very proud of it. The
free citizens chose or elected their own city government, the town council. No knight, no lord, could rule them, and even knights had to respect
the freedom of the cities.

相當多的農奴和村民逃到新城市去過自由人的生活。在城市裡，沒有人可以「擁有」另一個人——城市的每一個成員都是自由人、公民或市民，對此非常自豪。自由公民選擇或選舉自己的市政府——市議會。沒有騎士、沒有領主可以統治他們，甚至騎士也必須尊重城市的自由。

The cities became places where freedom could grow while the castles
and villages were still places of serfdom. So the Crusades started business and trade in the cities, and as the cities grew they also became the
birth place of a new freedom in Europe.

城市成為自由可以生長的地方，而城堡和村莊仍然是農奴制的場所。十字軍東征在城市中開啟了商業和貿易，隨著城市的成長，它們也成為歐洲新自由的誕生地。

What did a medieval city look like?

中世紀的城市是什麼樣子的？

Approaching from a distance we would see a great wall surrounding
the city. On the wall were watchtowers, and there were gates and
drawbridges over the moat below the wall. Every city was like a fortress,
ready to defend itself. All citizens were trained in the use of arms, especially in the use of the crossbow, which was also an invention that had
come from the East.

從遠處接近時，我們會看到一道環繞城市的巨大城牆。城牆上有瞭望塔，城牆下方的護城河上有城門和吊橋。每座城市都像一座堡壘，隨時準備自衛。所有市民都受過武器使用的訓練，尤其是弩的使用——弩也是從東方傳來的發明。

In the centre of the city there was a high tower, called the belfry. High
up in this tower there was always a watchman on duty ready to ring a
great bell if he saw any armed force appear.

在城市的中心有一座高塔，稱為鐘樓（belfry）。高塔上方總有一名值班的守望者，一旦看到任何武裝力量出現，就準備敲響大鐘。

Passing through one of the gates into the city, we would immediately
have realised that the great wall that protected the city also had disadvantages. Once the wall was built, the city could no longer grow and
spread; new people came, new houses were built, but all in the space inside the walls. And so a medieval city was terribly crammed and crowded:
the houses were huddled together, the streets were incredibly narrow -
barely wide enough for an ox-cart - and they twisted and turned in every
direction.

穿過城門進入城市，我們會立刻意識到保護城市的這道大牆也有缺點。城牆一旦建成，城市就無法再擴張蔓延；新的人來了，新的房屋建起來了，但都擠在城牆裡面的空間裡。因此中世紀的城市擁擠不堪：房屋緊緊相挨，街道窄得不可思議——勉強能讓一輛牛車通過——而且彎彎曲曲、四處蜿蜒。

But the worst thing about these winding streets was that everyone
treated them as rubbish-heaps. There was no sanitation or lavatories in
the houses. Buckets full of refuse were emptied through the windows
into the street below, and if a dog or a cat died it was also thrown out
into the street. In and out of this rubbish and dirt walked pigs and chickens and rats looking for food

但這些蜿蜒街道最糟糕的是，每個人都把它們當作垃圾堆。房屋裡沒有衛生設施或廁所。裝滿廢物的桶從窗戶傾倒到下方的街道上，如果一隻狗或貓死了，也會被扔到街上。在這些垃圾和污穢之間，豬、雞和老鼠進進出出地覓食。

At night there was no street lighting. At nine o'clock the curfew bell
tolled from a tower. Curfew comes from couvre feu, which is French for
'cover the fire', and when this bell tolled all lights - all candles and burning torches for there were no other lights - had to be extinguished. This
was to prevent fires at night. In the dark, the nightwatchmen patrolled
the streets to prevent burglary and theft, and every hour they sang the
time.

夜間沒有路燈。九點鐘時，塔樓上響起宵禁鐘聲。Curfew（宵禁）這個字來自法語 couvre feu，意思是「蓋住火」，當這口鐘敲響時，所有的光源——所有的蠟燭和燃燒的火把（因為沒有其他照明）——都必須熄滅。這是為了防止夜間火災。在黑暗中，巡夜人巡邏街道以防止盜竊，每個小時他們都會報時歌唱。

There was only one large open space in the city, the marketplace. On
a weekday morning the peasants from the surrounding countryside
came in to sell milk, butter and vegetables. In the marketplace you might
see an important-looking man swinging a hand-bell - this was the towncrier. When a crowd of people had been called together by his bell, he
told them the important news or proclamations from the town council.
Today we have media like newspapers, radio and television. In those
days the town-crier was all these rolled into one.

城市裡只有一片大的開放空間，就是市場。在工作日的早晨，周圍鄉下的農民會進城來賣牛奶、奶油和蔬菜。在市場上，你可能會看到一個看起來很重要的人搖著手鈴——這就是市鎮公告員（town-crier）。當他的鈴聲召集了一群人之後，他會告訴他們來自市議會的重要新聞或公告。今天我們有報紙、廣播和電視等媒體。在那個年代，市鎮公告員就是這些媒體的綜合體。

In another corner of the marketplace there were the stocks and the
pillory, to punish butchers, bakers or other tradesmen who cheated
their customers. They had to sit or stand in these wooden frameworks
which had holes for the head and limbs, and their fellow citizens could
heap abuse and scorn on them.

在市場的另一個角落，有枷鎖和頸手枷，用來懲罰欺騙顧客的肉販、麵包師或其他商人。他們必須坐或站在這些有頭和四肢孔洞的木框裡，其他市民可以對他們百般辱罵和嘲弄。

As in the East, all shops of the same trade were in the same street:
cobbler's lane, tailor's street, and so on. These shops had only picture
signs - a shoe, a pair of scissors, a barber's pole - because most people
could not read.

和東方一樣，同行業的所有店鋪都在同一條街上：鞋匠巷、裁縫街，等等。這些店鋪只有圖畫招牌——一隻鞋、一把剪刀、一根理髮桿——因為大多數人不識字。

You might think there was no beauty in such a city of the Middle Ages.
But there was. Coming out of a narrow lane you might see a great
church with high, lofty spires. These churches had stained-glass windows
and from the inside they glowed in wonderful colours as the light shone
through them. The pointed arches above were like hands in prayer. This
style of building is known as Gothic, and it was a style that the Crusaders
brought back from the East.

你可能會認為中世紀的城市裡毫無美感。但美確實存在。從一條狹窄的小巷走出來，你可能會看見一座有著高聳尖塔的宏偉教堂。這些教堂有彩色玻璃窗，從裡面看，當光線穿透時，它們閃耀著奇妙的色彩。上方的尖拱就像雙手合十祈禱。這種建築風格被稱為哥德式（Gothic），它是十字軍從東方帶回的一種風格。

# 6. King John and the Magna Carta
# 6. John 國王與大憲章

The cities where all citizens were equal were the cradles of freedom in
Europe. Under the old feudal system there were serfs, above them villagers, above them knights and above them the king. In this feudal system everyone (except the king) had an overlord who had to be obeyed.
But with the cities something new came about: men of the city only
obeyed the town council, which they themselves had elected. The citizens did not belong to the feudal system.

所有公民一律平等的城市，是歐洲自由的搖籃。在舊的封建制度下，有農奴，農奴之上是村民，村民之上是騎士，騎士之上是國王。在這個封建制度中，每個人（除了國王）都有一個必須服從的上級領主。但隨著城市的出現，新的事物誕生了：城市裡的人只服從他們自己選舉的市議會。市民不屬於封建制度。

At first it was only the city people, the burghers, who had this kind of
freedom, but in time the whole feudal system came to an end. One of
the most important changes in British history came in the year 1215.

起初只有城市裡的人——市民——擁有這種自由，但隨著時間推移，整個封建制度最終走向了終結。英國歷史上最重要的變革之一發生在 1215 年。

Richard the Lionheart, the valiant Crusader, was away from England
for many years, first on his Crusade, then as a prisoner in Austria. He
could only give a very little time to the task of ruling England. When he
returned he did not live long and he died without leaving any children.
Now Richard had two brothers. Geoffrey had died before Richard and so
his son, Arthur, was to inherit the throne. But Arthur was only a young
boy, and so Richard's other brother, John, became king.

英勇的十字軍戰士獅心王 Richard 離開英格蘭多年，先是參加十字軍東征，後來又在奧地利被囚禁。他只能投入極少的時間來治理英格蘭。當他回來後不久便去世了，沒有留下子嗣。Richard 有兩個兄弟。Geoffrey 在 Richard 之前就已去世，因此他的兒子 Arthur 本應繼承王位。但 Arthur 只是一個小男孩，所以 Richard 的另一個兄弟 John 成了國王。

King John was as wicked and evil a man as ever ruled a country. The
story of his misdeeds and cruelty would fill books. One of his first misdeeds was against his nephew, the boy Arthur. John wanted him out of
the way so that when Arthur grew up he could not claim the throne.

John 國王是有史以來統治國家的人中最邪惡殘暴的。他的惡行和殘忍足以寫成數本書。他最早的惡行之一就是針對他的侄子、少年 Arthur。John 想除掉他，這樣 Arthur 長大後就不能主張王位了。

When William the Conqueror had conquered England, he remained
master of Normandy on the other side of the Channel, and the kings
who followed him also ruled Normandy as well as England. The north of
France was at that time under the rule of the kings of England.

當征服者 William 征服了英格蘭後，他仍然是海峽對岸 Normandy 的主人，繼承他的國王們也同時統治 Normandy 和英格蘭。當時法蘭西北部處於英格蘭國王的統治之下。

King John sent his little nephew to a lonely castle in Normandy where
he was kept prisoner. One day King John came to visit the castle and
spent the night there. We shall never know what happened that night,
but the little Prince Arthur was never seen again.

John 國王把他年幼的侄子送到 Normandy 一座偏僻的城堡裡囚禁。有一天 John 國王來城堡探視，在那裡過了一夜。我們永遠不會知道那一夜發生了什麼，但小王子 Arthur 從此再也沒有出現過。

Having made sure that there was no competitor for the throne, John
now showed his noblemen, the knights of England, that, as their overlord, he stood above the law. He could do as he liked.

在確保沒有王位競爭者之後，John 向他的貴族——英格蘭的騎士們——表明，作為他們的領主，他凌駕於法律之上。他可以為所欲為。

One of the English noblemen was engaged to a very beautiful lady.
King John saw her, and decided he wanted her for himself. So he sent
armed men to kidnap her when she went for a walk and then she was
held captive in a castle until, in the end, she agreed to marry King John.

一位英格蘭貴族與一位非常美麗的女士訂了婚。John 國王看見了她，決定要據為己有。於是他派武裝士兵在她外出散步時綁架了她，然後把她囚禁在城堡裡，直到她最終同意嫁給 John 國王。

And so it was in many other things. King John showed that no one had
any rights in his land: not his noblemen, not the priests, not the common
people. He took what he wanted and used his soldiers against anybody
who resisted. This brother of the noble Richard the Lionheart became
one of the worst tyrants in British history.

其他許多事情也是如此。John 國王表明在他的國土上沒有人擁有任何權利：無論是他的貴族、神職人員、還是普通百姓。他想拿什麼就拿什麼，對任何反抗的人都動用士兵。這位高貴的獅心王 Richard 的兄弟，成了英國歷史上最糟糕的暴君之一。

By this time, people were no longer willing to put up with such treatment. A number of English lords and noblemen met secretly and swore
an oath that they would help each other to force this wicked king to respect the rights of his people.

此時，人們不再願意忍受這樣的對待。一些英格蘭領主和貴族秘密會面，宣誓互相幫助，迫使這個邪惡的國王尊重人民的權利。

They made a list of all the things a king should not be allowed to do,
and they wrote down the rights that everyone in the land should have
and which no one could take from them. A list was called a charter in
those days, and this long list was called the Great Charter or, in Latin,
Magna Carta.

他們列出了國王不應該被允許做的所有事情，並寫下了這塊土地上每個人都應該擁有的、任何人都不能剝奪的權利。在那個年代，這樣的清單被稱為 charter（憲章），而這份長長的清單被稱為大憲章，或用拉丁語說就是 Magna Carta。

The Magna Carta, the great list of rights, is one of the most famous
documents in history. One of the original copies is in the British Library
in London. Today we take it for granted that the king or queen or the
government cannot simply come and take our property away. We take it
for granted that no person can be thrown into prison just because some
powerful person does not like them. But all this was quite possible until
these English knights wrote in the Magna Carta that it should not be allowed. That is why the Magna Carta is so important. For the first time the
peoples of a country laid down rules for their kings and wrote down
their rights, which no king could take from them.

大憲章——這份偉大的權利清單——是歷史上最著名的文件之一。其中一份原件收藏在倫敦的大英圖書館。今天，我們視為理所當然的是，國王、女王或政府不能隨便來奪走我們的財產。我們視為理所當然的是，不能僅僅因為某個有權勢的人不喜歡某人，就把那人投進監獄。但在這些英格蘭騎士在大憲章中寫明這些事情不該被允許之前，這些都是完全可能發生的。這就是大憲章如此重要的原因。一個國家的人民第一次為他們的國王制定了規則，並寫下了他們的權利——任何國王都不能剝奪這些權利。

When the knights had made this list, the Magna Carta, they took it to
King John and asked that he should sign it with his name, to show that
he would from now on respect the rules set out in it. But King John refused - no one was to have any rights but the king himself.

當騎士們製作好這份清單——大憲章——之後，他們把它帶到 John 國王面前，要求他簽上自己的名字，以表明他從今以後會尊重其中規定的規則。但 John 國王拒絕了——除了國王本人之外，不允許任何人擁有任何權利。

Now it was not always the case that the people of England were united amongst themselves. The common people were pleased when the
noblemen were in trouble. The knights and noblemen often had quarrels and fights with the cities, and the priests often sided with the king.
But this time priests, knights, peasants and citizens were all united
against King John.

英格蘭的人民並不總是團結一致的。當貴族遇到麻煩時，平民百姓往往幸災樂禍。騎士和貴族常常與城市發生爭吵和衝突，而神職人員通常站在國王一邊。但這一次，神職人員、騎士、農民和市民全都團結起來反對 John 國王。

A great army was assembled and marched towards London to force
King John to put his name to the Magna Carta. When King John tried to
get his army together, he found that, in the whole of England, only seven
knights were ready to fight for him.

一支大軍集結起來，向倫敦進軍，迫使 John 國王在大憲章上簽名。當 John 國王試圖集結軍隊時，他發現在整個英格蘭，只有七名騎士願意為他而戰。

So King John could do nothing but meet the rebels. This meeting took
place at Runnymede, near London, in the year 1215.

所以 John 國王除了去見叛軍之外別無選擇。這次會面發生在 1215 年，地點是倫敦附近的 Runnymede。

Once again the noblemen put the Magna Carta before King John for
his signature. King John cursed and swore, he shouted: 'Why don't you
ask me to give my whole kingdom away?' But when he looked at the
stern faces around him he knew that these men were prepared to kill
him unless he put his signature to the Magna Carta. So he signed. When
he returned to his palace he was in such a fit of rage that he threw himself on the floor, screaming and
cursing. But the Magna Carta was
signed, and the power of kings was no longer what it had been before.
The king was no longer all-powerful and had to respect the rights of his
people.

貴族們再次將大憲章擺在 John 國王面前要求簽名。John 國王咒罵不止，他喊道：「你們為什麼不乾脆要我把整個王國都讓出去？」但當他看到周圍嚴峻的面孔時，他知道這些人已做好準備——如果他不在大憲章上簽名，就要殺了他。於是他簽了。當他回到宮殿時，他狂怒到撲倒在地，尖叫咒罵。但大憲章已經簽署了，國王的權力再也不是從前那樣了。國王不再是至高無上的，他必須尊重人民的權利。

The most important rule of the Magna Carta was that no one could be
put in prison or punished unless they had first been properly tried and
found guilty by a court of law. We take it for granted, but it was not so
before the Magna Carta. People could be jailed and executed on the
whim of a king without any trial.

大憲章最重要的規定是，任何人都不能被監禁或懲罰，除非他們先經過法庭的適當審判並被判定有罪。我們視此為理所當然，但在大憲章之前並非如此。人們可以憑國王的一時興起就被關進監獄和處決，完全不經審判。

Another rule of the Magna Carta was that the king could not go to war
with another country nor raise taxes without the agreement of the people.

大憲章的另一條規定是，國王未經人民同意，不得對另一個國家發動戰爭，也不得增加稅收。

Of course, it was not possible to go round and ask every person in the
land whether they agreed, and so there was a Common Council as it was
called, a group of people who could speak for the whole country and
agree or disagree with the king. Without this Common Council the king
could do nothing.

當然，不可能到處去問國土上的每一個人是否同意，於是就有了一個被稱為「共同諮議會」（Common Council）的機構——一群可以代表全國發言、同意或反對國王的人。沒有這個共同諮議會，國王什麼也做不了。

This Common Council was the beginning of Britain's Parliament. At
first only noblemen and bishops were in this Common Council, but later
the city merchants came and, in the end, people from all walks of life
could be elected for it.

這個共同諮議會就是英國國會（Parliament）的前身。起初只有貴族和主教在共同諮議會中，但後來城市商人也加入了，最終來自各行各業的人都可以被選入其中。

So the present form of government had its first beginnings with the
Magna Carta signed by King John at Runnymede in 1215.

因此，現今的政府形式最初是從 1215 年 John 國王在 Runnymede 簽署大憲章開始的。

Of course, it took many centuries until Britain's form of government
came about, and quite a number of kings tried to do away with the rules

當然，英國的政府形式經過了好幾個世紀才形成，而且有不少國王試圖廢除這些規則。

# 7. Scotland and England
# 7. 蘇格蘭與英��蘭

Freedom began in the cities where serfs and villagers could become free
men. Then the king, the pinnacle of the feudal system, was forced to sign
the Magna Carta, and so more freedom came to all people of England.
But all this was only the first step on the path to freedom. We now come
to whole nations fighting for their freedom against foreign invaders who
oppressed them. And one of these nations was Scotland.

自由始於城市——農奴和村民可以在那裡成為自由人。然後封建制度的頂峰——國王——被迫簽署了大憲章，於是更多的自由來到了英格蘭的所有人民。但這一切僅僅是通往自由之路的第一步。我們現在要講的是整個民族為了自由而對抗壓迫他們的外國入侵者。而其中一個民族就是蘇格蘭。

In Roman times, warlike tribes populated Scotland: dominated by the
Picts and the Scots, who had come from Ireland. Christianity came to
these fierce warriors of the north around AD 400, and the first to teach
Christianity in this region was Ninian, who built a church and monastery
in Galloway. But the greatest work in bringing the message of Christ to
Scotland was done by Columba. From the monastery on the island of
Iona, he and his helpers travelled from tribe to tribe and did not rest
until all Picts and Scots had become Christians.

在羅馬時代，好戰的部落聚居在蘇格蘭：以 Picts 人和來自愛爾蘭的 Scots 人為主。基督教大約在西元 400 年來到了這些北方的驍勇戰士，第一個在這個地區傳播基督教的是 Ninian，他在 Galloway 建造了一座教堂和修道院。但將基督的訊息帶到蘇格蘭的最偉大工作是由 Columba 完成的。從 Iona 島上的修道院出發，他和他的助手從一個部落走到另一個部落，直到所有 Picts 人和 Scots 人都成了基督徒才停歇。

Later still the Picts and Scots became one nation, but they lost some
of their land to the Vikings who came and settled on the northern and
western isles around Scotland. In time, the descendants of the Vikings
too became part of the Scots nation. The Gaelic-speaking Scots remained
in the Highlands. But they were all now one kingdom.

後來 Picts 人和 Scots 人合為一個民族，但他們失去了部分土地，因為維京人來到蘇格蘭周圍的北部和西部島嶼定居。隨著時間推移，維京人的後代也成為蘇格蘭民族的一部分。說蓋爾語的蘇格蘭人留在了高地。但他們現在都是一個王國。

The Kingdom of Scotland was on good terms with the Kingdom of
England in the south. The king of the Scots, Malcolm Canmore, married
an English princess, Margaret, who was an educated woman and had a
considerable influence on ways of life in Scotland. More churches were
built, merchants came who brought fine clothes the Scots had never
seen before, and the queen set such an example of charity for the poor,
and showed such gentleness and kindness that she was later called St
Margaret of Scotland

蘇格蘭王國與南方的英格蘭王國關係良好。蘇格蘭國王 Malcolm Canmore 娶了一位英格蘭公主 Margaret，她是一位受過教育的女性，對蘇格蘭的生活方式有相當大的影響。更多的教堂被建造起來，商人們帶來了蘇格蘭人從未見過的精美衣物，而王后對窮人樹立了如此慈善的榜樣，展現了如此溫柔和善良，以至於她後來被稱為蘇格蘭的聖 Margaret。

When the Normans came to England under William the Conqueror,
the friendship between the north and south of Britain did not change.
The kings of Scotland even invited Norman noblemen to Scotland and
gave them land, so many of the later noblemen of Scotland were of Norman origin.

當諾曼人在征服者 William 率領下來到英格蘭時，不列顛南北之間的友誼並沒有改變。蘇格蘭國王甚至邀請諾曼貴族來蘇格蘭並給予他們土地，因此後來蘇格蘭的許多貴族都是諾曼血統。

Scotland had become a prosperous country, at peace with its neighbour to the south, when a great misfortune happened. One dark night in
1286 the king who was then ruling Scotland, Alexander III, was thrown
off his horse, fell down a cliff, and died. There was only a granddaughter
to succeed him, and she was a little girl, Margaret (the Maid of Norway).
Then she too died. Who was now to rule Scotland? The king who ruled
England at this time was Edward I, the grandson of King John. Edward
thought that he should become king of Scotland as well as of England
for no better reason than that he was a distant relative of the little girl
who had died.

蘇格蘭已經成為一個繁榮的國家，與南方的鄰國和平共處，這時一場大不幸發生了。1286 年的一個漆黑夜晚，當時統治蘇格蘭的國王 Alexander 三世從馬上摔下，跌落懸崖而死。繼承王位的只有一個孫女，一個名叫 Margaret（挪威少女）的小女孩。然後她也死了。現在誰來統治蘇格蘭？此時統治英格蘭的國王是 Edward 一世，John 國王的孫子。Edward 認為他應該同時成為蘇格蘭和英格蘭的國王，理由僅僅是他是那個已故小女孩的遠親。

A Scots nobleman called John Balliol, who had in the meantime been
crowned as King as Scotland, gave up when the English armies invaded
Scotland. The Scots suddenly saw their country overrun by the English,
and in a few weeks Edward had Scotland at his mercy. He left English
troops in different parts of Scotland to keep the people in order, and returned to England, well pleased with his easy success. But the Scots had
not given up. A brave Scots knight, William Wallace, gathered men who
were willing to fight for the freedom of Scotland, and they began to attack the castles and fortresses held by the English troops. Stronghold
after stronghold was taken.

一位名叫 John Balliol 的蘇格蘭貴族，此前已被加冕為蘇格蘭國王，在英格蘭軍隊入侵蘇格蘭時投降了。蘇格蘭人突然發現他們的國家被英格蘭人佔領，幾周之內 Edward 就讓蘇格蘭任由他擺布。他在蘇格蘭各地留下英格蘭駐軍以維持秩序，然後返回英格蘭，對自己輕鬆的成功十分滿意。但蘇格蘭人並未放棄。一位勇敢的蘇格蘭騎士 William Wallace 召集了願意為蘇格蘭的自由而戰的人，他們開始攻打英格蘭駐軍據守的城堡和堡壘。一個又一個據點被攻下。

King Edward was not going to let the Scots shake off the English yoke
so easily. He came with a great army from the south and took a terrible
revenge, destroying villages and cities and leaving a trail of ruin and
death.

Edward 國王不會讓蘇格蘭人如此輕易地甩脫英格蘭的枷鎖。他從南方帶著一支大軍前來，進行了可怕的報復，摧毀村莊和城市，留下一路的廢墟和死亡。

Wallace had no army strong enough and he was defeated at the Battle
of Falkirk in 1298. His men scattered and Wallace himself became a hunted man with no home but the wild hills. He found shelter among the
Highland crofters, but he could stay nowhere for long. King Edward had
promised a great reward for his capture and not only the English soldiers, but also some treacherous Scots were eager to gain the prize. In
the end a Scotsman betrayed Wallace and he was taken by the English,
brought to London and cruelly put to death.

Wallace 沒有足夠強大的軍隊，他在 1298 年的 Falkirk 戰役中戰敗。他的部下四散，Wallace 本人成了一個被追捕的人，除了荒野山丘之外無家可歸。他在高地的佃農中找到庇護，但哪裡都不能久留。Edward 國王懸賞巨額獎金捉拿他，不僅英格蘭士兵，一些背信棄義的蘇格蘭人也急切地想要得到這筆獎賞。最終一個蘇格蘭人出賣了 Wallace，他被英格蘭人抓獲，帶到倫敦殘忍地處死。

But if King Edward thought this was the end of the Scots' fight for
freedom, he was mistaken. A new leader rose among the Scots, a nobleman called Robert the Bruce. At first, only a few knights came to his side,
but in 1306 they crowned Bruce king of Scotland on the ancient Stone of
Destiny at Scone Palace.

但如果 Edward 國王認為這是蘇格蘭人爭取自由之戰的終結，那他就錯了。一位新的領袖在蘇格蘭人中崛起，一位名叫 Robert the Bruce 的貴族。起初只有少數騎士站到他這一邊，但在 1306 年，他們在 Scone 宮的古老命運之石上為 Bruce 加冕為蘇格蘭國王。

Things went badly for the new king of Scotland. His army was defeated by the English and, like Wallace, Bruce and his few companions
had to flee to the Highlands, where they were hunted from place to
place with a price on their heads.

這位新蘇格蘭國王的處境很糟糕。他的軍隊被英格蘭人擊敗，和 Wallace 一樣，Bruce 和他的少數同伴不得不逃往高地，他們被懸賞追捕，四處躲藏。

Many a time Bruce was so downhearted that he thought it would be
better to surrender to the English. But some say that one day he saw a
little spider trying to climb up one silken strand of its web - failing and
trying, failing and trying - until, at last it succeeded. Seeing how the little
spider had not given up, he decided he too would not give up and go on
trying to fight the English.

他的第三個巧妙想法是不用手把框架壓在紙上——手壓總會弄糊印刷。取而代之，他建造了一種能將紙張均勻壓到框架上的裝置，稱為「印刷機」（press）。現在他可以印一百份第一頁，然後重新排列框架中的字母印下一頁，再印一百份，依此類推。他可以在大約三週或更短的時間內印出一百本書。如果用手抄，一百個抄寫員需要整整一年才能抄出同樣數量的副本，或者一個抄寫員需要一百年！現在一個人幾週就能完成。

不久之後士兵們就意識到這種混合物有多大用處。他們製造了大型臼炮（看起來很像裝在輪子上的長金屬鍋），裡面裝滿火藥，上面放一塊巨石，然後用火點燃。如果石頭被投向城堡的城牆，它的力量足以粉碎城牆。

These were the first cannons, and they brought about the end of
walled castles as walls were no longer a protection. Soon people made

# 10. The Age of Discovery
# 10. 大發現時代

A new spirit was abroad in Europe, a spirit of curiosity; there was an
eagerness to find out things, to discover the unknown. The next great
step in discovering the unknown was made in the Iberian Peninsula, the
lands south of the Pyrenees, in Portugal and Spain. It happened while
England and France were still locked in the Hundred Years' War.

一種新的精神在歐洲蔓延——一種好奇的精神；人們渴望發現事物、探索未知。發現未知的下一個偉大步伐是在伊比利半島——Pyrenees 山脈以南的土地——Portugal 和 Spain 邁出的。這發生在英格蘭和法蘭西仍深陷百年戰爭之際。

Arabs had ruled large parts of Spain and Portugal for hundreds of
years, since the early 700s. When Arab power was at its height, it was like
a crescent moon stretching from the Pyrenees across North Africa to the
Holy Land. But by the year 1400, the Arabs had lost much ground in the
Iberian peninsula, only the south was still held by the Arabs, while in the
north there was a Christian kingdom, the Kingdom of Spain. In the west
there was another Christian kingdom, Portugal, and it was here that a
period of history began which is often known in history books as the Age
of Discovery.

阿拉伯人統治了 Spain 和 Portugal 的大部分地區數百年，從七世紀初開始。當阿拉伯勢力達到鼎盛時，它像一輪新月，從 Pyrenees 山脈橫跨北非直到聖地。但到了 1400 年，阿拉伯人在伊比利半島已經失去了大片土地，只有南部仍由阿拉伯人控制，而北部有一個基督教王國——Spain 王國。在西部還有另一個基督教王國 Portugal，正是在這裡，歷史上一段常被稱為「大發現時代」的時期開始了。

It was one man who started the Age of Discovery. He was Prince
Henry, the third son of the king of Portugal. His mother was an English
princess, and perhaps he inherited his life-long love for the sea from her.
Prince Henry was also a Knight Templar. The Knights Templar, who had
been crusader knights, possessed great wealth, which they used to start
new enterprises, for example mines for metal-ore. The greed of King
Philip IV of France and of Pope Clement V destroyed the Knights Templar
almost everywhere. However, in Portugal the Knights Templar survived
and changed their name to the Order of Christ. Prince Henry was the
Grand Master, the leader, of this Order of Christ, and as Grand Master he
continued the task of the Knights Templar. This task was to strengthen
Christianity, to weaken the power of the Arabs and the Ottoman Turks,
and even more important, to open people's minds to the wisdom of the
world.

開啟大發現時代的是一個人。他是 Portugal 國王的第三個兒子 Henry 王子。他的母親是一位英格蘭公主，也許他對大海的終生熱愛就是從她那裡繼承的。Henry 王子也是一名聖殿騎士。聖殿騎士團曾是十字軍騎士，擁有巨大的財富，他們用這些財富開創新事業，例如金屬礦場。法蘭西國王 Philip 四世和教宗 Clement 五世的貪婪幾乎在所有地方摧毀了聖殿騎士團。然而在 Portugal，聖殿騎士團倖存下來，並改名為基督騎士團（Order of Christ）。Henry 王子是這個基督騎士團的大團長——領袖，作為大團長，他繼續執行聖殿騎士團的使命。這個使命是加強基督教、削弱阿拉伯人和鄂圖曼突厥人的力量，更重要的是，打開人們的心靈去接受世界的智慧。

Already as a young man, Prince Henry became famous for his part in a
great land and sea battle in which the Portuguese took the port of
Ceuta, opposite Gibraltar, from the Arabs.

Henry 王子年輕時就因參加一場偉大的海陸戰役而聞名，在那場戰役中，葡萄牙人從阿拉伯人手中奪取了 Gibraltar 對面的 Ceuta 港。

But Prince Henry was not only a man of action, he was also a man of
thought and of new ideas. The trade in spices had the merchants of
Venice buying the spices from the Ottoman Turks. But the spices came
from much further east, from islands in the Far East. The Turks brought
the spices from India and became rich selling them to Venice. But if ships
from Portugal could reach India, they could bypass the Turks and make
Portugal rich.

但 Henry 王子不僅是一個行動派的人，他也是一個有思想、有新點子的人。在香料貿易中，Venice 的商人從鄂圖曼突厥人那裡購買香料。但香料來自更遠的東方——遠東的島嶼。突厥人從印度帶來香料，通過賣給 Venice 而致富。但如果 Portugal 的船隻能到達印度，他們就可以繞過突厥人，使 Portugal 富裕起來。

But how could ships from Portugal get to faraway India? Today it is
easy to say, 'Sail around Africa! But at the time of Prince Henry nobody
from Europe had ever sailed around Africa, no one even knew whether
one could sail around it, no one knew how big or how small Africa was.*
There was no knowledge, but there were strange tales.

但 Portugal 的船隻怎麼才能到達遙遠的印度呢？今天要說「繞非洲航行」很容易！但在 Henry 王子的時代，歐洲從來沒有人繞非洲航行過，甚至沒有人知道能不能繞過去，沒有人知道非洲到底有多大或多小。*沒有知識，但有奇怪的傳說。

If you look at the map you see a great bulge on the west coast of
Africa. Such a bulge is called a cape. This cape was only known from
hearsay, no European had seen it, but it was called Cape No because it
was said that no one could sail beyond it. The sun, they said, is so hot
there that all the water of the ocean boils away and ships get stuck in the
salt. Even the bravest sailors were scared at the mere thought of sailing
in this direction.

如果你看地圖，會看到非洲西海岸有一個大凸起。這樣的凸起叫做海角（cape）。這個海角只是道聽途說，沒有歐洲人親眼見過，但它被稱為「否定角」（Cape No），因為據說沒有人能航行越過它。他們說那裡的太陽如此炎熱，海水都被蒸乾了，船隻會卡在鹽裡。即使是最勇敢的水手，光想到要朝這個方向航行就嚇壞了。

Prince Henry wanted the Portuguese ships to sail along the African
coast and to find a way to India, but there was no map and nothing was
known of the way. Even if one ship by sheer luck found a way, what use
was that unless the captain of the ship could make a good, reliable map
for others to follow.

Henry 王子想讓葡萄牙的船隻沿著非洲海岸航行，找到一條通往印度的路，但沒有地圖，對航路一無所知。即使一艘船純靠運氣找到了路，除非船長能繪製一張可靠的地圖供他人跟隨，否則有什麼用呢。

So Prince Henry set up a school of navigation, the first of its kind in
the world. In this school experienced captains from many nations, mapmakers, even Arab sailors and scientists, instructed the Portuguese seamen how to use the instruments that show the latitude of a place, how
to use a compass and how to observe the sun and stars with instruments, astrolabes, and how to make maps.

於是 Henry 王子建立了一所航海學校，這是世界上第一所這樣的學校。在這所學校裡，來自許多國家的經驗豐富的船長、地圖繪製師，甚至阿拉伯水手和科學家，教導葡萄牙海員如何使用顯示地點緯度的儀器、如何使用指南針、如何用儀器和星盤觀測太陽和星星，以及如何繪製地圖。

Only when they had learned all of this did the Portuguese ships set
out. But they did not go straightaway around Africa. The first ships went
only a short distance, then they came back and reported what they had
seen. The next ships went a bit further and again came back with reports. And from these reports, maps were made to guide other ships.

只有當他們學會了所有這些之後，葡萄牙的船隻才出發。但他們並沒有直接繞行非洲。第一批船隻只走了一小段距離，然後就回來報告他們看到了什麼。下一批船走得更遠一些，同樣帶著報告回來。根據這些報告，繪製出地圖來引導其他船隻。

Prince Henry received all these reports and saw to it that proper maps
were made, but he stayed behind and supervised all this work. The Portuguese people laughed about him, calling him, 'Prince Henry, the Navigator who never goes to sea'. But later on they very respectfully called
him only 'Prince Henry the Navigator'.

Henry 王子接收所有這些報告，並確保繪製出適當的地圖，但他本人留在後方監督所有這些工作。葡萄牙人嘲笑他，稱他為「從不出海的航海家 Henry 王子」。但後來他們非常尊敬地只稱他為「航海家 Henry 王子」（Prince Henry the Navigator）。

One of the earliest ships Prince Henry sent out ran into a storm and
was blown westwards. They lost sight of all land and thought they would
die of hunger and thirst, but then they saw an island, which they named
Porto Santo. It was one of the islands now called the Canary Islands.
There were no people on it, but the climate was very pleasant.

Henry 王子派出的最早一批船中，有一艘遭遇暴風被吹向西方。他們失去了所有陸地的蹤影，以為會餓死渴死，但後來他們看見了一座島嶼，命名為 Porto Santo。它是現在稱為加那利群島的島嶼之一。島上沒有人，但氣候非常宜人。

The ships came safely back to Portugal and Prince Henry sent Portuguese peasants to settle on these beautiful islands. They took seeds
with them and soon their crop flourished. Unfortunately, the colonists
also took rabbits with them. As there were no wild animals on the islands
the rabbits multiplied and ate all the crops. The colonists had to leave.
Soon another island further west was discovered, a large island that was
called Madeira, which means 'wooded island'.

船隻安全返回 Portugal，Henry 王子派葡萄牙農民前往這些美麗的島嶼定居。他們帶了種子，很快莊稼就繁茂起來。不幸的是，殖民者還帶了兔子。由於島上沒有野生動物，兔子大量繁殖，把所有莊稼都吃光了。殖民者不得不離開。不久，更西邊發現了另一座島嶼，一座叫做 Madeira 的大島，意思是「多林之島」。

But these discoveries in the west were not Prince Henry's goal. He
wanted to find a way south. His ships went further and further south,
and from these longer and longer journeys along the coast of West
Africa, they began to bring back things of wonder. They brought back
ivory, which they had bought from the indigenous people for a few glass
beads, and, even more exciting, was gold dust. Soon people not only in
Portugal but all over Europe talked about strange faraway lands where
the rivers ran with gold

但這些西方的發現並非 Henry 王子的目標。他想找到一條向南的路。他的船隻越走越南，從沿著西非海岸越來越長的航程中，他們開始帶回令人驚歎的東西。他們帶回了象牙——用幾顆玻璃珠從當地人那裡買來的——更令人興奮的是金沙。很快不僅 Portugal 人，全歐洲的人都在談論那些河流裡流淌著黃金的遙遠奇異土地。

If it was not gold and not ivory, it was something else. The Portuguese
sailors, seeing the African people, assumed that these black people were
enemies of the Christian faith. They attacked peaceful tribes on the
coast, took prisoners and brought them home to be used as slaves. So
began one of the most shameful chapters in the history of white people:
the African slave trade.

如果不是黃金，不是象牙，那就是別的東西。葡萄牙水手看到非洲人時，假定這些黑人是基督教信仰的敵人。他們攻擊沿海的和平部落，抓走俘虜帶回國當奴隸使用。白人歷史上最恥辱的篇章之一就此開始：非洲奴隸貿易。

The ships went still further south, and some went up rivers into the
interior of Africa. Every month brought new knowledge, new discoveries.
But in 1460 Prince Henry suddenly fell ill and died; he did not live to see
his dreams come true.

船隻繼續向更南方航行，有些沿著河流深入非洲內陸。每個月都帶來新的知識、新的發現。但在 1460 年，Henry 王子突然生病去世；他未能活著看到自己的夢想成真。

The enthusiasm, the spirit of discovery that he had roused in the Portuguese sailors lived on, however. They reached the southernmost tip of
Africa, the Cape of Good Hope. They sailed around the Cape and, thirtyeight years after Prince Henry's death, the Portuguese captain Vasco da
Gama reached India.

然而，他在葡萄牙水手中激發的熱情和探索精神延續了下來。他們到達了非洲最南端——好望角（Cape of Good Hope）。他們繞過好望角，在 Henry 王子去世三十八年後，葡萄牙船長 Vasco da Gama 抵達了印度。

The news of the Portuguese discoveries, the news that ships had sailed
to unknown shores, spread all over Europe. People's minds were stirred
by these stories. In the old days people had some hazy idea that there

Portugal's rivals, Venice and Genoa, were still engaged in the spice

第二天早上，Pizarro 派兩名軍官帶著翻譯去見印加國王，邀請國王進行一次友好的拜訪。這兩名軍官看到大城市中的景象同樣驚訝，就像印加人看到 Spain 人時一樣驚訝。Peru 的印加文明即使在今天也令人讚嘆。他們用重達十六到二十噸的巨大石塊建造塔樓和宮殿，這些石塊從很遠的地方運來，被切割得如此精確，當它們拼合在一起時，嚴絲合縫，連一張紙都塞不進去——完全不需要水泥。

Yet the Incas had neither rollers nor wheels for transport. They had
not discovered the wheel, neither had they horses nor oxen. They had
no iron to cut the stones either. The tools and weapons they had were
made from obsidian, a glassy volcanic stone.

然而印加人既沒有滾筒也沒有輪子來運輸。他們沒有發明車輪，也沒有馬匹和牛。他們也沒有鐵來切割石頭。他們擁有的工具和武器是用黑曜石——一種玻璃狀的火山岩——製成的。

Their cities were bigger than any in Europe at that time. The wide,
paved streets were so clean that the Spaniards' feet remained as clean as
their hands. These cities had things no European city had at that time:
pipes that brought fresh water from the mountains, underground sewers that took the dirt away. They had public hospitals and public baths
with hot-water systems. And the people of the cities never locked their
doors, for thieves or robbers did not exist among them.

他們的城市比當時歐洲的任何城市都大。寬闊的鋪砌街道如此乾淨，Spain 人走過後腳和手一樣乾淨。這些城市有當時歐洲城市所沒有的東西：從山上引來淡水的管道、排走污物的地下水道。他們有公立醫院和帶有熱水系統的公共浴場。城市裡的人從不鎖門，因為他們之中不存在小偷或強盜。

All the land and all the food grown on the land belonged to the king,
and the king's officers went around and distributed the food according
to the needs of every family. There was no buying or selling and no one
ever went hungry.

所有的土地和土地上種植的所有食物都屬於國王，國王的官員們四處巡視，根據每個家庭的需要分配食物。沒有買賣，也沒有人挨餓。

The king was regarded as a kind of god on earth - he was called the
son of the sun-god. The sun was the highest god of the Incas, and the
walls of the great temple of the sun were lined with gold an inch thick.
The temple of the moon was lined with silver.

國王被視為地上的一種神——他被稱為太陽神之子。太陽是印加人最高的神，太陽大神廟的牆壁鑲著一英寸厚的黃金。月亮神廟的牆壁則鑲著白銀。

It was a high civilisation, but it also had its dark side. At great festivals
the priests made human sacrifices - men and women were killed on the
altars in honour of their gods. But the men and women chosen to be
sacrificed never resisted: their life belonged to the king, the god on
earth, and if his priests wanted that life they could take it.

這是一個高度文明，但它也有陰暗面。在盛大的節慶中，祭司會進行人祭——男人和女人在祭壇上被殺死以尊崇他們的神靈。但被選中獻祭的男女從不反抗：他們的生命屬於國王——地上的神——如果他的祭司要他們的生命，他們可以取走。

And now this great and powerful king was invited by Francisco Pizarro,
the former swineherd, for a friendly visit. This visit was to be the end of
the mighty kingdom of the Incas.

而現在，這位偉大而強大的國王被 Francisco Pizarro——那個從前的豬倌——邀請進行一次友好的拜訪。這次拜訪將是強大的印加帝國的終結。
