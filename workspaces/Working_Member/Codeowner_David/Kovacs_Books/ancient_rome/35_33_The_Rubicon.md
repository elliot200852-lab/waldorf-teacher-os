was a command: "Disband your army, send your soldiers home, and return to Rome."
What should Caesar do? If he obeyed the Senators' command and
came back to Rome alone, his life would be at the mercy of Pompey who
might perhaps treat him generously, but never again would Caesar have
a chance to gain power for himself. If Caesar disobeyed, he would be a
rebel against his own government, and Roman soldiers led by Pompey
would fight against him.

to a little river, called the Rubicon. This river was the borderline between
Gaul and Italy. By this River Rubicon he had to make his decision. He
