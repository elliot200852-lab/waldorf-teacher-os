## 兩種工具速查 Quick Reference

```
工具 A：間接問句（Indirect Question）
  位置：緊跟在「動詞」後面
  動詞：know / wonder / ask / find out / figure out / understand...

  She didn't know   WHAT  it would feel like inside.
  She wondered      IF    the nun would let her in.
  She couldn't      WHY   the building made her ashamed.
    figure out
  She didn't        HOW   she was supposed to stand there.
    understand

  wh-word 工具箱：
    what（什麼）  why（為什麼）  how（怎麼）
    when（什麼時候）  if / whether（會不會）  where（哪裡，8-2 已學）

  ★ 語序提醒：
     ✓ I didn't know WHY she did that.         （主詞 + 動詞）
     ✗ I didn't know WHY DID SHE do that.      （不用問句語序！）

─────────────────────────────────────────

工具 B：關係代名詞（Relative Pronoun）
  位置：緊跟在「名詞」後面（在名詞上貼「標籤」）

  the dress    [THAT  made her look older]
  the old man  [WHO   said it was his birthday]
  the canteen  [WHICH had long windows]

  選用規則：
    who   → person（人）
    which → thing / place（東西、地點）
    that  → both（都可以）

  中文的「的」→ 說今天是他生日【的】老人
  英文的 who  → the old man [who said it was his birthday]
              → 標籤在名詞「後面」（英文 vs 中文相反）

─────────────────────────────────────────

★ 如何判斷？（視覺決策樹）

         指著 who / which / that / what / why / how / if
                           ↓
                前面的字是什麼？
               ╱                    ╲
            名詞                    動詞
   (dress / man / shop)    (know / wonder / figure out)
               ↓                      ↓
      工具 B：關係代名詞         工具 A：間接問句
    the dress [THAT...]      I didn't know [WHY...]
```

### Task B — 合併句子（Combine）

Combine each pair into **one longer sentence**.

The suggested tool is marked: **[IQ]** = Indirect Question ／ **[RP]** = Relative Pronoun

You can change the suggestion if another tool fits better — just be ready to explain why.

**Example [RP]:**
She wore a dress. + The dress made her look older.
→ She wore the dress **that** made her look older.

**Example [IQ]:**
She wanted to know. + Why did the nun look at her like that?
→ She wanted to know **why** the nun looked at her like that.

**After writing — three questions to ask yourself:**

1. Read your indirect questions aloud. Do they sound like the inside of your head — wondering, not knowing?

2. Read your relative pronouns. Do they zoom in on a specific detail — the exact person, the exact thing?

3. Does the paragraph feel like a story, or a list? Stories have rhythm — some sentences breathe long, some stop short.
