# 12. The Blossom

The plants that are like children before they go to school - mosses, ferns,
algae - are all plants without blossoms. And the plants that have flowers
- tulips, violets, daffodils, roses - they are all like school-children. In the
beauty of the blossoms, in the scent, it is like seeing what it means to
learn, to make the inner light work in you. Just as it is not always easy to
learn, to remember, to think, so it is not so easy for plants to bear blos-
soms. In fact, the flowering plants must prepare themselves before they
can bear blossoms. A flowering plant - a rose, or a wild plant, the but-
tercup, for example - grows and grows for quite some time, while there
are still only green leaves visible.

> 那些像上學前的孩子的植物—— moss（苔蘚）、fern（蕨類）、algae（藻類）——都是沒有花的植物。而那些有花的植物—— tulip（鬱金香）、violet（紫羅蘭）、daffodil（水仙花）、rose（玫瑰）——它們就像上學的孩子。花朵的美麗和香氣，就像讓你看到學習的意義、讓內在之光在你心裡工作的意義。就像學習、記憶、思考不是件容易的事，植物要開花也不是那麼容易的。事實上，開花植物在開花之前必須先做好準備。一棵開花植物——比如玫瑰，或一種野生植物 buttercup（毛茛、金鳳花）——它會長啊長啊，長好一陣子，這時候你只看得到綠色的葉子。

With the buttercup the leaves change even as the plant grows. The
first leaves, that are near the ground, near the earth, are large, but later
on the leaves that develop higher up, are smaller and finer. But they are
still just green leaves.

> 金鳳花的葉子在植物生長的過程中會改變。最初的葉子，靠近地面、靠近大地的那些，又大又寬；但後來長在比較高處的葉子，就變得更小、更細緻了。不過它們仍然只是綠色的葉子。

Then a wonderful thing happens: the buttercup stops growing taller,
it grows special little green leaves that are quite different from the green
leaves that came before, and these little green leaves stand close to-
gether and stick together, so that they form a little green shell. If you
take a knife and open that little shell just as it has formed, you would find
nothing in it. But if you wait a little while, a few more days, then this little
shell opens up to reveal a lovely yellow blossom! And the little shell is
now like a cup, which holds up the petals. These little green leaves which
make a shell at first, and then a cup, are together called the calyx which
means cup. And the little leaves themselves are called sepals.

> 然後一件奇妙的事發生了：金鳳花不再往上長了，它長出了特別的小綠葉，跟之前的綠葉完全不同。這些小綠葉緊緊地靠在一起、黏在一起，形成一個小小的綠色外殼。如果你拿一把刀，在它剛形成的時候切開來看，裡面什麼都沒有。但如果你再等一會兒，等個幾天，這個小外殼就會打開，露出一朵可愛的黃色花朵！這時小外殼變成了一個杯子，把花瓣托了起來。這些先是形成外殼、後來變成杯子的小綠葉，合在一起叫做 calyx（花萼），意思就是杯子。那些小葉子本身叫做 sepal（萼片）。

The sepals, that together form the calyx, are at first like hands in
prayer, and then they are like hands holding a precious gift, and that is
the flower.

> 萼片合在一起形成花萼，起初就像雙手合十祈禱的樣子，然後變成像雙手捧著一份珍貴禮物的樣子——而那份禮物，就是花朵。

40The coloured leaves of the flower are called petals. But the gift that
the calyx has received is not only these lovely, coloured petals it has also
received something else. In the very middle, in the centre of the petals,
standing straight up, there is something that looks like the sceptre of a
king, or queen - it is called the pistil.

> 花朵上那些有顏色的葉片叫做 petal（花瓣）。但是花萼收到的禮物不只是這些美麗的彩色花瓣，它還收到了別的東西。就在花瓣的正中央，直直地站著一個東西，看起來像國王或女王的權杖——它叫做 pistil（雌蕊）。

right hand, but in his left hand he held a golden ball, an orb. When a king

> 在古時候，國王的右手不只拿著金色的權杖，左手還拿著一顆金色的球，叫做 orb（寶球）。國王在加冕典禮上坐在寶座上的時候，一手握著權杖，一手握著寶球——一根棒子和一顆球。

If you look a bit closer at the sceptre that stands in the middle of the
flower, you will see that, further down, it widens out and looks like an
orb. In the plant it is one thing, the upper part is like a scepter, the lower
part is like an orb. But, in botany they are not called sceptre and orb. The
upper, rodlike, sceptre part is called the pistil and the little ball, the lower
part, which is like an orb, is called the ovary.

> 如果你再仔細看看站在花朵中央的那根權杖，你會發現它往下的部分變寬了，看起來就像一顆寶球。在植物裡它們是一體的：上面的部分像權杖，下面的部分像寶球。但在植物學裡，它們不叫權杖和寶球。上面那根像棒子的權杖部分叫做 pistil（雌蕊），下面那顆像寶球的小球叫做 ovary（子房）。

This is not all that the calyx holds up, however. It holds up the petals,
the pistil and ovary (which are really one), but it also holds up something
else. Around the proud royal sceptre and orb - around the pistil and
ovary - in a circle, like a crown, there stand fine stalks known as stamens.
The stamens have little golden heads and these golden heads are made
up of a fine golden dust, called pollen.

> 不過花萼托起來的還不只這些。它托著花瓣、雌蕊和子房（其實它們是一體的），但它還托著另一樣東西。圍繞著那驕傲的皇家權杖和寶球——圍繞著雌蕊和子房——像皇冠一樣圍成一圈，站著一些細細的小莖，叫做 stamen（雄蕊）。雄蕊有金色的小頭，這些金色的小頭是由一種細緻的金色粉末組成的，叫做 pollen（花粉）。

You recall that at the beginning of this book, you learned that the
plants are children of sun and earth, and in every part of a flowering
plant there is something that is given by the earth, and something that is
given by the sun. You can easily see that the green pistil and the round
ovary (itself like a little earth) are the gift of the earth. And the stamens,
standing in a circle, with the golden pollen, are the gift of the sun.

> 你還記得在這本書的開頭，你學到植物是太陽和大地的孩子，在開花植物的每一個部分裡，都有大地給的東西，也有太陽給的東西。你很容易就能看出來：綠色的雌蕊和圓圓的子房（它本身就像一個小小的地球）是大地的禮物。而那些圍成一圈、頂著金色花粉的雄蕊，則是太陽的禮物。

What a wonderful gift the calyx hold up: the petals, the pistil and
ovary, and the stamens with their golden pollen.

> 花萼托起的是多麼美妙的禮物啊：花瓣、雌蕊和子房，還有那些頂著金色花粉的雄蕊。
