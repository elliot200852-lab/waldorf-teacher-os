# 17. Seeds and Cotyledons

We divided the flowering plants into two groups. The flowering plants
are those that learn the wonderful art of bearing blossoms from the wis-
dom in the sunlight. Some of the blossom-bearing plants are like young
children, what they learn is still simple, their leaves and blossoms are still
simple.

> 我們把開花植物分成了兩大群。開花植物就是那些從陽光的智慧中，學會了開花這門美妙技藝的植物。有些會開花的植物就像年幼的孩子，它們學到的東西還很簡單，它們的葉子和花朵也還很簡單。

The tulip, beautiful as it is, has simple leaves, that is, the veins are par-
allel. The petals are thick, fleshy and a bit clumsy, in comparison to the
petals of a pansy, or a rose, which feel very fine. It is an early flower that
grows from a bulb, it does not wait for the full power and warmth of the
sun.

> 鬱金香雖然很美，但它的葉子是簡單的，葉脈是平行的。它的花瓣厚厚的、肉肉的，比起 pansy（三色堇）或 rose（玫瑰）那種摸起來很細緻的花瓣，就顯得有點笨拙。它是一種早開的花，從球莖長出來，它不等太陽達到最強最溫暖的力量就開了。

There is another difference between the two great plant groups, be-
tween the simpler plants and the more perfect plants. And to under-
stand this difference we shall consider the growing seed.

> 這兩大植物家族之間，較簡單的和較完美的之間，還有另一個不同之處。要理解這個不同，我們就要來看看正在生長的種子。

If you look at a little seed, you can hardly tell what plant will grow
from it, unless you already know what plant the seed came from. It is like
a little book that you have not read, and unless somebody tells you what
is in the book, you don't know. The sun and the earth, the water and the
air, open the little book and show you what is in it.

> 如果你看著一顆小小的種子，你幾乎猜不出它會長成什麼植物，除非你已經知道這顆種子是從哪種植物來的。它就像一本你還沒讀過的小書，除非有人告訴你書裡寫了什麼，不然你就是不知道。太陽和大地、水和空氣，會替你打開這本小書，讓你看到裡面藏了什麼。

Imagine that you know what kind of plant is hidden in that seed, and
you want this plant to grow in your garden. First of all you must make
sure that the little seed will have a chance to grow properly. If there are
weeds or grass where you put the seed into the earth, then the tiny
plant that will come from the seed will not have a chance to grow prop-
erly; weeds or grass must be cleared away.

> 假設你知道這顆種子裡藏著什麼植物，而你想讓它在你的花園裡生長。首先，你得確保這顆小種子有機會好好長大。如果你放種子的地方長滿了雜草，那麼從種子冒出來的小小植物就沒辦法好好生長；雜草必須先清除掉。

You then have to do a little digging. You must make the soil loose
around the seed, and if it is too hard, the seed will not grow. With some
children, it takes quite a bit of digging before their minds are ready to
receive the seed. Another thing 52to keep in mind is to choose the right
time for sowing the seed. The different flowers in your garden have dif-
ferent times that are best for sowing. Of course, spring flowers must be
sown earlier than autumn flowers. And the gardener knows the best
time for sowing each kind of flower.

> 然後你要稍微翻翻土。你得讓種子周圍的泥土鬆軟，如果土太硬，種子就長不出來。有些孩子也是這樣，要花好一番功夫翻鬆他們的心田，他們才準備好接受那顆種子。還有一件事要記住，就是要選對播種的時間。你花園裡不同的花，各有各最好的播種時機。當然，春天的花要比秋天的花更早播種。園丁知道每一種花最好的播種時間。

and for crops, ever since the time of ancient Persia. And they found in

those ancient times, that the moon has something to do with the growth
of plants. Not only the sun and the earth and the water and the air, but
the moon also has something to do with the growth of plants.

> 幾千年來，人們一直在播種——種花，也種莊稼——從古代 Persia（波斯）的時代就開始了。他們在那遠古的年代就發現，月亮和植物的生長有著某種關係。不只是太陽、大地、水和空氣，月亮也和植物的生長有關。

What they found was this: if you plant a seed just three days before
the full moon, the plant will grow stronger, better, and faster, than if you
plant it at any other time. The best time for a seed to be planted is three
days before the full moon, when the moon itself is still "waxing" (that is
"growing").

> 他們發現的是這樣的：如果你在滿月前三天播下種子，植物會長得更強壯、更好、更快，比在其他任何時間播種都好。播種最好的時機，就是滿月前三天，那時候月亮本身還在「盈」（也就是在「漸圓」）。

In our time, the knowledge that the waxing moon helps growing
plants, has been forgotten - many people don't know about it, or don't
believe in it. But those who do know it, and plant their seeds at the right
time, they do get better results; their plants do grow faster and stronger.

> 在我們這個時代，月亮盈虧幫助植物生長的知識已經被遺忘了——很多人不知道，或者不相信。但那些知道的人，在對的時間播下種子，他們確實得到了更好的成果；他們的植物確實長得更快、更壯。

The seed has been planted in the earth, it has been watered properly,
the sun has been shining upon the earth, and the first thing to happen is
that little seed begins to swell, just like a sponge swells, and gets bigger
when you fill it with water. Then, from the seed tiny roots grow down-
wards into the earth and tiny leaves grow upwards towards the light.

> 種子已經種進泥土裡了，也好好澆了水，太陽照耀著大地。最先發生的事情，就是那顆小種子開始膨脹，就像海綿吸了水會脹大一樣。然後，從種子裡，小小的根往下鑽進泥土，小小的葉子往上朝著光長去。

The little leaves that come first, the leaves which come from the seed,
are not at all like the real leaves that come later; they are either egg-
shaped or heart-shaped. You know that in olden times when a king came
into a city, heralds went before him. Heralds are men who announce to
the people: "The king is coming, the king is coming!" And the king would
only come once the heralds had announced him; they cleared the way
for him. The little leaves that come from the seed, the seed-leaves, are
like these heralds. They break through the earth and prepare 53the way
for the true leaves, and for the flower that comes later. In botany, these
seed-leaves or "herald-leaves"
are called cotyledons. The true leaves
grow from the stalk, not from the seed.

> 最先冒出來的那些小葉子，從種子裡長出來的葉子，和後來長出的真正葉子一點都不像；它們不是蛋形就是心形。你知道在古時候，國王進城的時候，會先派先鋒官走在前面。先鋒官就是向人們宣告「國王來了！國王來了！」的人。國王要等先鋒官宣告完、把路清好了才會來。從種子長出來的那些小葉子，種子葉，就像這些先鋒官。它們破土而出，為真正的葉子、為後來的花朵開路。在植物學裡，這些種子葉，或者叫「先鋒葉」，被稱為 cotyledon（子葉）。真正的葉子是從莖上長出來的，不是從種子長出來的。

Now we come to the two groups of plants that you have heard so
often: the flowering plants with leaves of parallel veins, and the flow-
ering plants whose leaves have a network of veins. They also have differ-
ences in terms of these heraldleaves. The plants which have simple
leaves, parallel veins, they only have one herald, their seed sends out
only one cotyledon (monocotyledons in botany). The more perfect
plants, the plants whose true leaves have a network of veins, their seeds
send out two heralds, they have two cotyledons (dicotyledons).

> 現在我們回到你已經聽了很多次的兩大植物家族：葉脈平行的開花植物，和葉脈像網一樣交織的開花植物。它們在先鋒葉上也有不同。那些葉子簡單、葉脈平行的植物，只有一個先鋒官，它們的種子只送出一片子葉（植物學裡叫 monocotyledon（單子葉植物））。比較完美的植物，真正的葉子有網狀葉脈的那些，它們的種子會送出兩個先鋒官，有兩片子葉（叫做 dicotyledon（雙子葉植物））。
