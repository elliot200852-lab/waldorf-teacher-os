# 9. Trees and the Earth

We have learned about children who are four years old, who still don't
go to school, and we have compared them with conifer trees, the
straight fir trees and the pine trees. If you compare a tall fir tree with a
dandelion, it is quite small in comparison. Yet the dandelion knows more;
it has learned more from the sunlight than the fir tree - the dandelion
has proper blossoms, and later on it has proper fruit, proper seeds.

> 我們之前認識了那些四歲大、還沒上學的孩子，也把他們比作針葉樹——筆直的 fir（冷杉）和 pine（松樹）。如果你拿一棵高大的冷杉跟一朵 dandelion（蒲公英）比，蒲公英真的好小好小。可是蒲公英懂得更多呢；它從陽光那裡學到的東西比冷杉還多——蒲公英有真正的花，後來還結出真正的果實、真正的種子。

You may think it is strange that the tall fir tree is like a four-year-old
child before going to school, and the dandelion, which is so tiny in com-
parison, is like a much older child - it is much more awake than the tall fir
tree. The size of the tree compared to the size of the little flower does
not matter, however. To understand it, let us think of another tree, for
instance, a cherry tree or an apple tree in full blossom - it is a lovely
sight! The cherry tree or the apple tree in blossom, in itself, is like a little
flower garden, except that all the flowers are the same kind of flowers.
Each tree is a flower garden of the same flowers at blossom time.

> 你可能覺得很奇怪：高大的冷杉像一個還沒上學的四歲孩子，而那麼小的蒲公英反而像一個年紀大得多的孩子——它比高高的冷杉清醒多了。不過，樹的大小跟小花的大小根本不重要。要理解這件事，讓我們想想另一種樹，比方說一棵開滿花的 cherry tree（櫻桃樹）或 apple tree（蘋果樹）——那景象真是美極了！一棵盛開的櫻桃樹或蘋果樹，本身就像一座小小的花園，只是裡面所有的花都是同一種花。每棵樹在花開時節，就是一座開滿同樣花朵的花園。

And in winter these same trees are just as bare as the earth under
them, the wood, the trunk and branches; in winter it is just like the earth
itself! When spring
comes there are green shoots
coming
from the
earth, and green shoots coming from the wood - the wood has become
like the earth again. The wood behaves like the earth.

> 到了冬天，這些樹變得跟它們腳下的大地一樣光禿禿的——那些木頭、樹幹和樹枝，在冬天看起來就跟大地本身一模一樣！等到春天來臨，綠色的嫩芽從大地冒出來，也從木頭上冒出來——木頭又變得像大地了。木頭的表現就跟大地一樣。

But there is a difference: the plants down on the ground each have
their own root in the earth; the leaves and blossoms on the tree have all
the roots of the tree on which they grow.

> 不過有一個不同的地方：地面上的植物，每一株都有自己扎進泥土裡的根；而樹上的葉子和花朵，它們共用的是整棵樹的根。

The plants that grow on earth send their roots into the earth, and the
earth makes the roots hard, and some roots even become wood. It is the
strength of the earth that hardens the roots so that some of them be-
come as hard as wood. It is not 33the sun; the sun makes the fine flower.

> 長在地上的植物把根送進泥土裡，大地讓根變得堅硬，有些根甚至變成了木頭。是大地的力量讓根硬起來，硬到有些跟木頭一樣。這可不是太陽做的事；太陽負責的是造出美麗的花朵。

You can now understand that where a tree grows, where you see a
hard wooden trunk rising from the ground, it is the earth-force, the
earth-strength itself which goes upwards; it is as though a hill were
pushed upwards, pushed up until it becomes a tree.

> 現在你可以明白了：一棵樹長出來的地方，你看到堅硬的木頭樹幹從地面升起，那其實是大地的力量、大地的力氣往上推；就好像一座小山丘被往上推、一直推，推到最後變成了一棵樹。

Because the wood of a tree is really the earth pushed upwards, when
a tree gets old and begins to decay, to crumble, what happens? If you
put your hand into a hole in an old decaying tree, you find wood that has

> 因為樹木的木頭其實就是大地往上推的結果，所以當一棵樹老了、開始腐朽、開始碎裂的時候，會發生什麼事呢？如果你把手伸進一棵腐朽老樹的洞裡，你會發現木頭已經碎成了鬆軟的泥土。所以木頭的表現跟大地一樣，一點也不奇怪——大地光禿禿的時候它也光禿禿，大地變成小花園的時候它也跟著開花。

The fungi and the lichen and the moss all know that the wood of the
trees is a kind of earth; that's why you find fungi and lichen and moss
growing on trees - they are children of earth and they grow just as hap-
pily on wood as on the earth. But there are also insects, beetles, and
worms that live in the earth, and they too live just as happily in the wood
of trees - they are all aware that wood is a kind of earth.

> Fungi（真菌）、lichen（地衣）和 moss（苔蘚）都知道樹木的木頭是一種大地；所以你才會看到真菌、地衣和苔蘚長在樹上——它們是大地的孩子，長在木頭上跟長在泥土上一樣開心。還有那些住在泥土裡的昆蟲、甲蟲和蟲子，它們住在樹木的木頭裡也一樣快活——它們都知道木頭就是一種大地。

Now you become aware that the green leaf, the blossom, and the
fruit is the real plant in the tree, and the tree itself, the wood, is only a
great hill pushed upwards. Think again of the fir tree and the dandelion.
The fir's real plant is the needle and cone, not the wood. And on that
"real" plant, the needles are very simple leaves compared to the dande-
lion leaves. And the dandelion flower is not only one blossom, but hun-
dreds of blossoms growing together.

> 現在你會發現：綠色的葉子、花朵和果實才是樹上真正的植物，而樹本身、那些木頭，只不過是一座被推高的大山丘。再想想冷杉和蒲公英吧。冷杉真正的植物部分是它的針葉和毬果，不是木頭。而在那個「真正的植物」上面，針葉跟蒲公英的葉子比起來簡單得多。蒲公英的花也不只是一朵花，而是好幾百朵小花聚在一起呢。

You might ask why there are different kinds of wood. It is because the
green plants that grow on the wood need different kinds of earth. It is
the same with plants that grow below the ground - many of them need a
special kind of earth; and the little green plants on the trees need their
own special earth, and that earth is the particular wood of that tree.

> 你可能會問：為什麼木頭有那麼多不同的種類呢？那是因為長在木頭上的綠色植物需要不同種類的大地。長在地面下的植物也一樣——很多植物需要特別的泥土；而樹上的小綠色植物也需要屬於它們自己的特別的大地，而那個大地，就是那棵樹特有的木頭。
