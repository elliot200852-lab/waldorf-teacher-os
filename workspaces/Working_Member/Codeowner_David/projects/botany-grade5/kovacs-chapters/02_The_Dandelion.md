# 2. The Dandelion

If you walk over a field in spring or summer you are quite likely to see a
common wild flower. And when you see it, you can hardly help it, you
want to pick it up and blow at it; and when you blow, little white stars
come off and fly away in all directions. Of course, this plant is the dande-
lion.

> 如果你在春天或夏天走過一片田野，你很可能會看到一種常見的野花。當你看到它的時候，你幾乎忍不住想把它摘起來，對著它吹一口氣；當你一吹，小小的白色星星就會飛散開來，朝著四面八方飄去。當然，這株植物就是 dandelion（蒲公英）。

If you had walked across this field a few weeks earlier, however, then
the dandelion would have looked quite different; it would have had no
little stars that fly away, but many golden-yellow petals instead. This
golden blossom of the dandelion looks like a little sun. And this golden
blossom of the dandelion also loves the sun; it only opens up when the
sun rises, and when the sun sets, or even when there is a dull day and
the sky is overcast with clouds, the dandelion flower is closed. The
dandelion flower is only open when there is sun, and at night or on a
cloudy day it is closed.

> 不過，如果你在幾個星期前走過這片田野，那時候的 dandelion（蒲公英）看起來會完全不同；它不會有那些會飛走的小星星，取而代之的是許多金黃色的花瓣。蒲公英金色的花朵看起來就像一個小小的太陽。而蒲公英的金色花朵也很喜愛太陽；它只有在太陽升起的時候才會打開，當太陽下山，或者甚至只是陰天、天空被雲遮住的時候，蒲公英的花就合起來了。蒲公英的花只有在有太陽的時候才是張開的，到了晚上或陰天它就閉合了。

Later on, there are the little stars that fly off, but earlier there is the
golden-yellow flower that greets the sun, and earlier still, what would
you see in the field - just green dandelion leaves. They have a curious
shape these green leaves, with sharply pointed fringes. People thought
these sharp points looked like lions' teeth, which in French translates to
dents de lions. This became "dandelion."

> 後來呢，就有了那些會飛走的小星星。但在更早之前，有的是向太陽打招呼的金黃色花朵。再更早之前呢，你在田野上會看到什麼？只有綠色的蒲公英葉子。這些綠色的葉子有一種奇特的形狀，邊緣有尖尖的鋸齒。人們覺得這些尖尖的鋸齒看起來像獅子的牙齒，用法文來說就是 dents de lions。這就變成了 "dandelion" 這個名字的由來。

So you can see three stages of the same flower, the dandelion. First it
is just green leaves, then it is a golden-yellow blossom, and then it is
fruit, because these flying white stars are the fruits of the dandelion.

> 所以你可以看到同一種花——蒲公英——的三個階段。首先它只是綠色的葉子，然後它變成金黃色的花朵，接著它變成了果實，因為那些會飛的白色小星星就是蒲公英的果實。

Now we shall see how the dandelion changes from green leaves, to
flower, then to fruit.

> 現在我們來看看蒲公英是怎麼從綠葉變成花朵、再變成果實的。

Early in the year, when the green, lion-teeth leaves arrive, it is not very
warm, as the warmth and light of the sun are not very strong yet. But
something else helps the plant when it comes out of the ground. It is
always around you, but you only feel it when there is a wind - it is the air.
It is the air, together with the light of the sun which makes the green
leaves.

> 一年之初，當那些綠色的獅子牙齒葉片冒出來的時候，天氣還不太暖和，太陽的溫暖和光線還不是很強。但當植物從地裡鑽出來的時候，有另外一樣東西在幫助它。它一直都在你身邊，但你只有在有風的時候才感覺得到它——那就是空氣。就是空氣，加上太陽的光，造就了綠色的葉片。

But it gradually gets warmer, and the heat gets stronger, and the
warmth and light work on the leaves,
and through the warmth of the
sun the top leaves are transformed, they are no longer green, but
become the golden-yellow petals of the blossom. So it is no wonder that
blossoms like the dandelion follow the sun; that they open up when the
sun rises, and close when it gets dark. It is not so surprising, because the
dandelion's coloured petals are made by the warmth of the sun.

> 但天氣漸漸變暖了，熱度也越來越強，溫暖和光在葉片上作用著。通過太陽的溫暖，最頂端的葉片被轉化了，它們不再是綠色的，而是變成了花朵金黃色的花瓣。所以難怪像蒲公英這樣的花會追隨太陽——它們在太陽升起時張開，天黑的時候就合起來。這一點也不令人驚訝，因為蒲公英那些有顏色的花瓣就是被太陽的溫暖所造就的啊。

However, the sun does not just give its warmth to the flower-petals, it
also gives it to the earth from which the plant grows. Imagine it is a
warm day and the sun shines for many hours upon a stone. Of course,
the stone gets warm, and if you hold your hand to it you feel the warmth
of the sun coming back from the stone.

> 然而，太陽不只是把溫暖給了花瓣，它也把溫暖給了植物生長的大地。想像一下，在一個溫暖的日子，太陽照射在一塊石頭上好幾個小時。當然，石頭就變暖了，如果你把手放到石頭上，你會感覺到太陽的溫暖從石頭裡散發回來。

But the whole earth from which the plants grow, does the same as
the single stone, only it takes much longer. The warmth of the sun goes
quite deep into the earth - several feet deep - and then the warmth
comes back from the earth and into the air, just as you can feel the
warmth coming back from the stone. But it takes much longer coming
back from the earth, days and weeks, and it is not such a strong warmth
that you would easily notice it.

> 但是植物生長的整個大地，跟一塊石頭做的事情是一樣的，只是要花更長的時間。太陽的溫暖會深入大地好幾英尺深——然後那溫暖會從大地裡回來，散發到空氣中，就像你能感覺到溫暖從石頭裡散發回來一樣。但從大地裡回來要花更長的時間，需要好幾天甚至好幾個星期，而且那溫暖也沒有那麼強烈，你不容易注意到它。

For the plants, though, the warmth that comes back from the earth is
something very real and very important. For the warmth coming back
from the earth, rising from the earth, forms the fruit - in the dandelion's
case, the little "stars" which fly away when you blow on them.

> 然而對植物來說，從大地回來的溫暖是非常真實、非常重要的。因為從大地回來、從大地升起的那份溫暖，形成了果實——在蒲公英的情況下，就是那些你一吹就會飛走的小「星星」。

So while the sun is still quite weak the air and light forms the green
leaves, then the heat of the sun gets stronger and forms the coloured
petals, after which the heat of the sun lessens, but warmth, heat, comes
back from the earth and forms the fruit, the little flying stars.

> 所以，當太陽還很弱的時候，空氣和光形成了綠色的葉子；然後太陽的熱度變得更強，形成了有顏色的花瓣；之後太陽的熱度減弱了，但是溫暖、熱度從大地回來了，形成了果實，那些會飛的小星星。

That warmth coming back from the earth lasts quite some time,
even through the winter. Human beings would hardly notice it, but dur-
ing the winter the green dandelion leaves don't stand up, they lie flat on
the ground, and in this manner get some warmth from the earth. For the
dandelion leaves this warmth coming back from the earth is very real
and important.

> 從大地回來的那份溫暖會持續很長一段時間，甚至貫穿整個冬天。人類幾乎不會注意到它，但是在冬天，蒲公英的綠葉不是站立的，它們是平貼在地面上的，這樣就能從大地那裡得到一些溫暖。對蒲公英的葉子來說，從大地回來的這份溫暖是非常真實也非常重要的。

So we have:
green leaves - air and light
blossom - warmth of the sun
fruit - warmth coming back or reflected by earth.

> 所以我們得到了：
> 綠葉——空氣與光
> 花朵——太陽的溫暖
> 果實——從大地回來的、或被大地反射回來的溫暖。

And what about the root in the earth? The roots are helped by water.
You know that when you water a plant, it is no use spraying the leaves or
blossoms, as they can't take in water. Plants can only take in the water
they need through the roots - that's why you soak the earth around the
plant with water, so that it goes to the roots and from the roots to the
leaves, and blossom.

> 那在大地裡的根呢？根是被水所幫助的。你知道，當你澆花的時候，對著葉子或花朵噴水是沒有用的，因為它們沒辦法吸收水分。植物只能透過根來吸收它們需要的水——所以你才要把植物周圍的土壤澆透，讓水滲到根部，再從根部輸送到葉子和花朵。

And so, air and warmth, and earth and water work together and give
the plant leaves, blossom, fruit, and down below, roots.

> 就這樣，空氣和溫暖，大地和水，一起合作，給了植物葉片、花朵、果實，還有在下面的根。
