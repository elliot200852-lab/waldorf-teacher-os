# 21. The Nettle

You have heard of the flowers and plants that we grow in our
gardens
for their beauty, like tulips and roses, or plants we grow because we eat
them, like the different kinds of cabbages. But now we are going to learn
about a plant that few people like; a plant that does not have beautiful
flowers or juicy fruit, nor does it have a pleasant smell, and yet it really is
a nice plant. It is the stinging nettle.

> 你們已經聽過那些我們在花園裡種的花和植物了——有些是因為美麗而種的，像 tulip（鬱金香）和 rose（玫瑰）；有些是因為可以吃而種的，像各式各樣的高麗菜。但是現在，我們要來認識一種很少人喜歡的植物；一種既沒有美麗的花朵，也沒有多汁的果實，連香味也沒有的植物，然而它真的是一種很好的植物。它就是 stinging nettle（蕁麻）。

The first thing to learn about the stinging nettle is that it is a plant
that has great strength. You see, the plants in our gardens need a lot of
protection - they must have the right soil, they must be protected from
weeds, they must be watered if there is not enough rain. Our garden
plants all need a helping hand from us, but the nettle is a wild plant and
it can grow in any kind of soil; it can even grow in the worst soil.

> 關於蕁麻，第一件要學的事就是：它是一種力量很大的植物。你看，花園裡的植物需要很多保護——土壤要對、要幫它們除雜草、雨水不夠時還要幫它們澆水。我們花園裡的植物都需要我們伸出援手，但蕁麻是一種野生植物，它在任何土壤裡都能生長，就算是最差的土也沒問題。

The nettle is not only a strong plant; if you look at it carefully you
could even say it is beautiful. Just look at the beautiful order of the
leaves. They are in four rows, and the two leaves of the bottom row
stand in a cross formation with the two leaves of the second row, the
third row in a cross with the second, and the fourth in a cross with the
third. And each leaf is beautiful in its own right, with fine-feathered
edges ending in a point.

> 蕁麻不僅是一種強壯的植物；如果你仔細看，你甚至可以說它是美麗的。看看葉子排列得多麼漂亮有秩序。它們排成四列，最下面那一排的兩片葉子和第二排的兩片葉子呈十字交叉，第三排和第二排又呈十字，第四排和第三排也是。每一片葉子本身都很美，邊緣有精細的鋸齒，末端收成一個尖點。

But these beautiful leaves have little "hairs," and it is these hairs that
sting you. These "hairs" are not really hair; they are really stiff bristles,
rather like fine glass spikes. When you touch these bristles they break,
the broken bits penetrate the skin, but the juice that was inside the bris-
tle is the thing that makes your skin feel as if it were burnt, that is what
stings you.

> 但是這些美麗的葉子上有小小的「毛」，就是這些毛會螫你。這些「毛」其實不是真正的毛，它們是硬硬的刺毛，很像細細的玻璃尖刺。當你碰到這些刺毛，它們會斷裂，斷掉的小片刺進皮膚裡，而原本藏在刺毛裡面的汁液，就是讓你的皮膚感覺像被燒到一樣的東西——那就是螫你的東西。

The nettles that grow in our part of the world only sting, and the sting
soon passes without doing any real harm. But in hot countries, in the
tropics, there are nettles whose sting is much worse and it can make
you ill for days. So our nettle is really a nice fellow, he doesn't do any real
harm.

> 生長在我們這裡的蕁麻只會螫人，而且螫一下很快就過去了，不會造成真正的傷害。但在熱帶的炎熱國家，有些蕁麻的螫傷要嚴重得多，可以讓你難受好幾天。所以我們的蕁麻其實算是個好傢伙，它不會真的傷害你。

The nettle does grow blossoms, pale light purple blossoms, but they
are small and have no scent, and these blossoms don't attract any in-
sects. The nettle has its pollen carried away by the wind. The nettle is,
however, different in one way from the other plants you have learnt
about. Some nettles have flowers with stamens only, no pistil and ovary,
and others have flowers with pistil and ovary, but no stamens. So, the
wind brings the pollen of the stamen to the nettle blossoms that have
only pistil and ovary.

> 蕁麻也會開花，淡淡的淺紫色花朵，但花很小，而且沒有香味，這些花也不會吸引昆蟲。蕁麻的花粉是靠風帶走的。不過，蕁麻有一點和你們學過的其他植物不同。有些蕁麻的花只有雄蕊，沒有雌蕊和子房；另一些蕁麻的花則只有雌蕊和子房，沒有雄蕊。所以，是風把雄蕊的花粉帶到那些只有雌蕊和子房的蕁麻花上。

The nettle does not attract bees or butterflies with its flowers, yet it is
a great friend of some butterflies. These butterflies - especially the red
admiral - lay their eggs upon nettle leaves, and the caterpillars that
come from the eggs feed on the nettle leaves; they feed on the leaves
that sting us, but they don't seem to sting the caterpillars. And from
these caterpillars come the beautiful red admiral butterflies. When the
gardeners use weedkillers to get rid of nettles, unfortunately, they also
get rid of the butterflies. The nettle does not have beautiful blossoms of
its own, but it is kind to the caterpillars, and, one could say, produces the
"flying blossoms," the butterflies.

> 蕁麻的花不會吸引蜜蜂或蝴蝶，但它卻是某些蝴蝶的好朋友。這些蝴蝶——尤其是 red admiral（紅蛺蝶）——會把卵產在蕁麻的葉子上，從卵裡孵出來的毛毛蟲就以蕁麻的葉子為食。牠們吃的正是那些會螫我們的葉子，但似乎不會螫到毛毛蟲。從這些毛毛蟲，就變出了美麗的紅蛺蝶。當園丁用除草劑來消滅蕁麻的時候，很不幸地，他們也一起消滅了蝴蝶。蕁麻自己沒有漂亮的花朵，但它善待毛毛蟲，你可以說，它製造出了「會飛的花朵」——蝴蝶。

In spring, the young nettle leaves can be gathered and cooked in a
soup, and in the old days when people knew more about the healing
powers of plants, they used to give nettle soup to invalids, to help them
get strong again. It worked very well, and even works today if people
bother to pick the young leaves. In addition, the fibres of the nettle were
used to make a strong yarn to weave a strong, warm, kind of cloth.

> 春天的時候，可以採集蕁麻的嫩葉煮成湯。在古時候，人們比較了解植物的療癒力量，他們會把蕁麻湯給病人喝，幫助他們恢復體力。效果非常好，就算到了今天，只要有人願意去採嫩葉，一樣有效。另外，蕁麻的纖維還可以做成很結實的紗線，織成一種又強韌又溫暖的布。

So, the nettle is actually a good fellow, it is rather like people who are
rough and uncouth on the outside, but have a really good and kind
heart inside, and will come and help you when you are in need.

> 所以，蕁麻其實是個好傢伙，它就像那些外表看起來粗魯又不修邊幅的人，但內心裡卻有一顆真正善良、溫暖的心，在你需要幫助的時候，會來伸出援手。
