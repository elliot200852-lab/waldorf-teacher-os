# 24. The Palm Tree

The root of a plant belongs to the earth, and it loves the earth, it grows
downwards. Roots will always grow away from light because they like the
darkness; they like to feel covered by Mother Earth. But the blossom
turns towards the light, it opens its petals to the light, and the petals get
their lovely colours and their scent from the light. The green leaves and
the stem, they stand in between: as the colour green stands between
bright yellow and the dark blue in your paint-pots, and is a mixture of
both; so the green leaves and the stem stand between the sun-loving
blossom and the earth-loving roots. The whole plant belongs to both, to
sun and to earth. The earth is the mother and the sun is the father of the
plants. In the blossom, the plant shows its love for Father Sun, and in the
root, its love for Mother Earth; in the green leaves and stem, it shows its
love for both together.

> 植物的根屬於大地，而且它愛著大地，所以向下生長。根總是朝著遠離光的方向長，因為它喜歡黑暗，喜歡被大地母親覆蓋的感覺。但花朵卻轉向光，向著光打開花瓣，花瓣那美麗的顏色和芳香也是從光那兒得來的。綠色的葉子和莖，它們站在中間——就像你顏料盒裡的綠色站在明亮的黃色和深沉的藍色中間，是兩者的混合一樣，綠色的葉和莖也站在喜愛陽光的花朵和喜愛大地的根之間。整株植物同時屬於兩者——屬於太陽，也屬於大地。大地是植物的母親，太陽是植物的父親。在花朵裡，植物展現了它對太陽父親的愛；在根裡，展現了對大地母親的愛；在綠葉和莖裡，它展現的是對兩者共同的愛。

But the plants in the world are not all the same. In hot countries,
where the warmth and light of the sun is very strong, the blossoms get
big and colourful, and the whole plant wants to reach towards the sun;
the leaves get large, the stems grow tall, and the tree trunks grow high-
er and higher. But the further north you go, the more the plants shrink -
the blossoms get smaller, stems and tree trunks get shorter. In the far
north, in the Arctic, the birch tree becomes a dwarf-birch, a little dwarf, a
tiny birch that clings to the earth.

> 但世界上的植物並不都是一樣的。在炎熱的國家，陽光和溫暖特別強烈的地方，花朵長得又大又鮮豔，整株植物都想要伸向太陽；葉子變得巨大，莖長得高高的，樹幹也越長越高。但你越往北走，植物就越縮小——花朵變小，莖和樹幹也變矮了。在極北之地，在北極圈，白樺變成了矮白樺，一個小矮人，一棵緊緊貼著大地的小小白樺。

So one could say that the whole plant-world becomes more like a
blossom the nearer we come to the tropical lands of the Equator, and
more like a root the nearer we come to the cold lands in the north.
When we have this in mind we can understand trees of the tropical lands
better, trees such as the palm trees.

> 所以我們可以說，整個植物世界越靠近赤道的熱帶地區就越像花朵，越靠近北方寒冷的地方就越像根。當我們心裡記住這一點，就能更了解熱帶地區的樹木了，比如 palm trees（棕櫚樹）。

A palm tree looks so different from our trees because the trunk
shoots straight up without any branches, there is no spreading out from
the trunk. A palm goes straight up towards the light and the leaves are
on top. But all the strength of the tree that has not been used for
branches and twigs now goes into the leaves, and that is why the leaves
of a palm tree get so big, because the strength that other trees use for
branches goes into these leaves. They are big leaves indeed: the leaves
of the coconut palm reach a length of five metres (fifteen feet), more
than twice as tall as a man.
But they are so high up that they don't look
as large as they really are. They are high up because a coconut tree can
grow to a height of thirty metres (a hundred feet). These large leaves are
not just broad leaves, they are feathered, which means that they look
like ferns.

> 棕櫚樹看起來和我們的樹是那麼不同，因為它的樹幹直直往上衝，沒有任何枝條，不會從樹幹向外伸展。棕櫚直直朝著光長上去，葉子全在最頂端。但樹木所有沒有用在枝條和細枝上的力量，全都跑進了葉子裡，所以棕櫚樹的葉子才會長得那麼大——因為別的樹用在枝條上的力量，棕櫚全用在了葉子上。那些葉子確實很大：coconut palm（椰子樹）的葉子能長到五公尺長（十五英尺），比一個人的兩倍還高。但因為它們長在那麼高的地方，看起來就沒有實際那麼大了。它們長得那麼高，是因為椰子樹可以長到三十公尺（一百英尺）。這些大葉子不是一般的闊葉，而是羽狀的，看起來像蕨類一樣。

There are many kinds of palm trees, particular to different regions
and climates. The coconut palm likes the coast where damp air from the
sea blows through its leaves. Its fruit, the coconut, sometimes falls into
the sea. And as it can wait six months before it begins to sprout, the sea
often carries it to another island or coast before it has sprouted. Co-
conut palms, therefore, can be found anywhere in warm coastal regions
of Africa, India, and South America.

> 棕櫚樹有很多種，分布在不同的地區和氣候帶。椰子樹喜歡海岸，那裡有帶著濕氣的海風吹過它的葉子。它的果實——椰子——有時候會掉進海裡。而因為椰子可以等上六個月才開始發芽，海水常常在它發芽之前就把它帶到另一個島嶼或海岸了。所以，在非洲、印度和南美洲溫暖的海岸地區，到處都能找到椰子樹。

Now when you take a fruit that we are familiar with - an apple for in-
stance - you find the seeds within the core, or in a cherry, the seed is in-
side the stone. With the coconut, which is a big fruit, you have to look
more carefully to find the seed. On the hard shell you will see three dots,
and when you crack the shell, you see a brown skin. Under the skin is the
white "flesh" of the coconut and inside the flesh is the "milk" as it is
known. But the flesh and the milk are only there to feed the seed until it
is big enough and strong enough to surface and grow into a tall, strong
palm tree. The seed is quite small at first - it looks like a small, soft fir-
cone and you will find it underneath the shell where the three dots are.
This little cone will slowly absorb the milk and the flesh, and grow bigger
and stronger, developing tiny green leaves. It has the strength to
begin to grow into a tall tree then.

> 當你拿起一顆我們熟悉的水果——比如蘋果——你會在果核裡找到種子；或者 cherry（櫻桃），種子就在果核裡面。椰子是一種很大的果實，你得更仔細地找才能看到種子。在堅硬的殼上你會看到三個小點，敲開殼以後你會看到一層褐色的皮。皮底下是椰子白白的「果肉」，果肉裡面是被稱為「椰奶」的液體。但果肉和椰奶只是為了餵養種子，直到種子長得夠大、夠強壯，能冒出地面長成一棵高大強壯的棕櫚樹。種子一開始其實很小——看起來像一顆小小的、軟軟的松果，你可以在殼底下那三個小點的位置找到它。這顆小松果會慢慢吸收椰奶和果肉，越長越大、越長越壯，冒出小小的綠葉。到那時候，它就有力量開始長成一棵高大的樹了。

We think of coconuts as a food, something to eat. But to the people of
Africa and India the coconut has many more uses. When a coconut is first
taken from the tree, it has a smooth outer skin, either green or brown.
Within this skin you find rough, fuzzy hair known as "coir," and within the
coir, you find the hard shell. This rough hair is used to make mats and
nets, and the large leaves are used to make roofs on huts, the "feathers,"
the strips of the leaves, are woven into baskets, and the long middle ribs
are twisted together to make strong ropes. The young shoots of new
leaves make a tasty vegetable, and the sap of the palm tree, if it is left
standing for some months, becomes sweet palm wine. The wood is of
course used for building.

> 我們想到椰子，覺得它是食物，是拿來吃的。但對非洲和印度的人來說，椰子的用處遠遠不止於此。當椰子剛從樹上摘下來時，它有一層光滑的外皮，綠色或褐色的。在這層皮裡面，你會找到粗糙蓬鬆的毛，叫做 coir（椰殼纖維），在椰殼纖維裡面才是堅硬的殼。這些粗毛被用來做地墊和漁網；大片的葉子被用來蓋茅屋的屋頂；葉子的「羽片」——那些長條狀的部分——被編成籃子；中間長長的葉脈則被擰在一起做成結實的繩索。新葉的嫩芽是美味的蔬菜，棕櫚樹的樹液如果放上幾個月，會變成甜甜的棕櫚酒。木材當然也被用來蓋房子。

There is an even more important use for the coconut. In the tropical
lands of Africa, India, South America, and especially in the islands of the
Pacific Ocean, there are large plantations of coconut palms. In these
plantations the white flesh of the coconut is taken and dried in the sun.
This dried flesh is called copra, and it does not smell nice, it smells like oil
that has gone rancid. In the white, dried copra there is oil. The copra is
put into a press, and the oil pressed out. The finer oil that comes from
the copra was once used to make margarine, and the less fine oil is used
for making soap and cosmetics. The soaps we use often contain oil that
comes from the coconut. So this tall, tropical coconut tree is important
to us.

> 椰子還有一個更重要的用途。在非洲、印度、南美洲的熱帶地區，尤其是太平洋上的島嶼，有大片大片的椰子園。在這些種植園裡，人們把椰子白色的果肉取出來，在太陽底下曬乾。這種曬乾的果肉叫做 copra（椰乾），聞起來不太好，像是變了味的油。在白色的乾椰肉裡有油脂。把椰乾放進壓榨機裡，就能把油榨出來。從椰乾榨出的比較精細的油，以前被用來做人造奶油；比較粗的油則被用來做肥皂和化妝品。我們用的肥皂裡，常常就含有來自椰子的油。所以這種高大的熱帶椰子樹，對我們來說是很重要的。
