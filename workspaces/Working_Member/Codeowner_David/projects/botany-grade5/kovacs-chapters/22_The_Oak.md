# 22. The Oak

You have heard in geography how important trees are. In the Scandi-
navian countries the forests are a source of wealth for the people, be-
cause they provide timber for building, for furniture, for packing cases,
for paper, and for matchsticks - whole forests are often cut down for this
purpose. But you have also heard that trees and forests are important in
other ways too, and where people have cut down forests to use the
wood without re-planting, they have paid a terrible price for it. Huge
areas of South America, Africa, and Asia would not be barren; they would
have fertile soil if people had not cut down all the trees. Without tree
roots to hold the precious soil in place, the rains washed the soil away,
and most of it was swept into the sea via streams and rivers. So the trees
are the protectors of the soil. But the trees are not only protectors of the
soil, they help to make new soil by shedding their leaves. Over time, tree
leaves become wonderful soil as they decay.

> 你們在地理課上已經聽過樹木有多重要了。在北歐的 Scandinavia（斯堪地那維亞）國家，森林是人民的財富來源，因為森林提供了建築、家具、包裝箱、紙張和火柴用的木材——常常為了這些用途，整片森林都被砍光了。但你們也聽過，樹木和森林在其他方面也很重要。在那些人們把森林砍光卻不重新種植的地方，他們付出了可怕的代價。南美洲、非洲和亞洲大片大片的土地不會變得荒蕪——如果人們沒有把樹木全砍掉的話，那裡會有肥沃的土壤。沒有了樹根抓住珍貴的泥土，雨水就把土壤沖走了，大部分經由小溪和河流被沖進了大海。所以，樹木是泥土的守護者。但樹木不只是守護泥土而已，它們透過落葉來幫助製造新的土壤。隨著時間過去，樹葉腐爛之後會變成很棒的泥土。

In ancient times, for example, when the wise Celtic Druids lived, peo-
ple revered the trees of the forest; they held them in awe. They felt no
temple with marble pillars could be as beautiful as the trunks of forest
trees reaching up from the ground, and the "vault" of green leaves and
branches above. That is why they worshiped in forest-glades, in the great
forests that once covered Britain, of which little is left now. There was
one forest tree that the Druids treated with great reverence, the oak
tree. The name of this wise people comes from the oak tree, for drus or
drys, was their name for the oak tree (in Greek, oak is drys too) and a
"Druid" means a man who is like the oak.

> 在古時候，比如在智慧的 Celtic Druids（凱爾特德魯伊）生活的年代，人們崇敬森林裡的樹木，對它們懷著敬畏之心。他們覺得沒有任何大理石柱的神殿比得上森林樹幹從大地向上伸展的壯麗，以及頭頂上綠色枝葉交織成的「拱頂」。這就是為什麼他們在森林裡的空地上敬拜，在曾經覆蓋整個 Britain（不列顛）的大片森林中——如今已所剩無幾了。有一種森林裡的樹木，德魯伊們對它特別尊崇，那就是 oak tree（橡樹）。這群智者的名字就來自橡樹，因為 drus 或 drys 就是他們對橡樹的稱呼（在希臘文裡，橡樹也叫 drys），而「Druid（德魯伊）」的意思就是「像橡樹一樣的人」。

The oak tree is certainly a picture of strength. The wood is very hard,
the branches are knobbly and thick and the foliage is a deep green.
Think how different the slender trunk of a birch tree looks compared
with the might and strength of the oak tree. The birch is a tree that
looks loveliest when it is young, but the oak tree gets more beautiful,
more majestic, the older it is. And the oak can get very old indeed, easily
two hundred years. In Allonville, Normandy, is an oak tree that is sixty
feet high and thirty feet thick, which is one thousand years old.

> 橡樹確實是力量的化身。它的木材非常堅硬，枝條粗壯多節，樹葉是深沉的綠色。想想看，白樺那纖細的樹幹和橡樹的雄偉有力比起來，看起來是多麼不同。白樺是一種年輕的時候最好看的樹，但橡樹卻是越老越美麗、越老越莊嚴。而且橡樹確實能活得非常非常久，輕輕鬆鬆就活兩百年。在 Normandy（諾曼地）的 Allonville（阿隆維爾），有一棵橡樹高六十英尺、粗三十英尺，已經一千歲了。

But the oak does not form its mighty trunk, and its hard, strong wood
quickly. The oak is a slow-growing tree, like a person who does not
believe in doing things quickly and carelessly, but a person who wants to
do everything well and carefully, a person who works steadily, but slowly
and thoroughly.

> 但是橡樹並不是很快就長出它那雄偉的樹幹和堅硬結實的木材的。橡樹是一種生長緩慢的樹，就像一個不相信凡事要快快做、草草了事的人，而是一個希望每件事都做得好、做得仔細的人，一個穩穩地、慢慢地、徹徹底底地工作的人。

In the spring, when all other trees are already dressed in their new
green foliage, the branches of the oak tree are still bare; it takes its time
and its leaves come out later than the others do. But the oak also holds
its leaves for longer than most other trees. In autumn, when all other
trees have lost their leaves, the oak tree still holds onto its leaves, even
though they are already brown and dried up, and you can hear them
rustle in the wind.

> 春天的時候，當所有其他的樹都已經換上了新綠的衣裳，橡樹的枝條還是光禿禿的。它不急，它的葉子比別的樹晚出來。但橡樹留住葉子的時間也比大多數的樹更久。秋天的時候，當所有其他的樹都已經落了葉，橡樹仍然抓著它的葉子不放，雖然那些葉子已經變成了褐色、乾枯了，你可以聽到它們在風中沙沙作響。

By the time the oak tree's green leaves appear, it is so late in spring
that its blossoms appear at the same time. But an oak tree waits for thir-
ty years before any blossoms grow upon it. For the first thirty years of its
life an oak only has leaves and no blossoms.

> 等到橡樹的綠葉終於冒出來的時候，春天已經過了很久，所以它的花也同時出現了。但是一棵橡樹要等上三十年，才會開出花來。在它生命的頭三十年裡，橡樹只有葉子，沒有花。

A fellow like the oak tree that wants to be strong and firm, could not
very well be expected to have blossoms with beautiful coloured petals;
that kind of blossom would not go with the oak at all. It would be like ex-
pecting an eagle to sing sweetly like a blackbird, or to twitter like a
sparrow. No, the blossoms of the oak tree are tiny catkins, and you hard-
ly notice them at all. The oak tree cannot produce sweet fruit either, that
would not be in style for a tree of such strength. The fruit of the oak tree
is the acorn in their little cups.

> 像橡樹這樣一心想要強壯、堅定的傢伙，你實在不能指望它開出帶有美麗彩色花瓣的花朵。那種花配在橡樹身上完全不對味。那就好像要一隻老鷹像黑鶇一樣甜美地歌唱，或者像麻雀一樣嘰嘰喳喳。不，橡樹的花朵是小小的柔荑花序，你幾乎注意不到它們。橡樹也不會結出甜美的果實，那不符合一棵如此強壯的樹的風格。橡樹的果實是 acorn（橡實），裝在小小的杯子裡。

Now acorns, little as they are, are not the kind of seeds that can be
scattered by the wind, they fall down under their tree. And from all these
acorns under the oak tree not a single new tree would grow, for a seed
needs sunlight to grow even after it has sprouted. The foliage of the
old tree lets a little sunlight through, but not enough to allow a new tree
to grow under it.

> 橡實雖然小小的，卻不是那種可以被風吹散的種子，它們會掉落在自己的樹底下。而這些掉在橡樹底下的橡實，沒有一顆能長成新的樹，因為種子發芽之後也需要陽光才能生長。老橡樹的枝葉雖然會透進一點點陽光，但不夠讓一棵新樹在它底下成長。

So how do new oak trees grow? A certain large bird with light brown
and white feathers, and black and white wings and tail, likes acorns. It is
called a jay. The jay wants to eat as many as he can find in autumn. There
are plenty of acorns, and the jay can't eat all of them, but he does not
want to leave them either, so he takes lots of acorns away, and when he
thinks he has found the right spot, he digs a hole in the ground with his
beak, puts an acorn into the hole, and covers it with earth. He does this
with all the acorns he finds. Later he comes back to dig up the acorns
that he has put into the ground, but he always misses some, and next
spring,
here and there,
the tiny shoots of new oak trees
come up
through the ground. So the jay helps to make new oak trees grow.

> 那麼新的橡樹是怎麼長出來的呢？有一種大鳥，長著淡褐色和白色的羽毛，翅膀和尾巴是黑白相間的，很喜歡吃橡實。這種鳥叫做 jay（松鴉）。松鴉到了秋天想盡辦法吃掉所有找得到的橡實。橡實很多，松鴉吃不完，但牠也捨不得丟下，所以牠會叼走很多橡實。當牠覺得找到了合適的地點，就用喙在地上挖一個洞，把橡實放進去，再用泥土蓋起來。牠對找到的每一顆橡實都這樣做。後來牠會回來挖出那些埋在地裡的橡實，但總有一些被牠遺忘了，到了第二年春天，這裡那裡，新橡樹的小小嫩芽就從地面冒了出來。就這樣，松鴉幫助了新橡樹的生長。

A tough, strong tree like the oak cannot grow pretty blossoms or
sweet fruit, but there are also occasions when the oak tree shows that it
has sweetness. In the same way you sometimes meet a person who
seems tough and rough, but under that roughness, when you get to
know this person better, there is a gentle heart, which only shows itself
on special occasions.

> 像橡樹這樣堅韌強壯的樹，長不出漂亮的花或甜美的果實，但有些時候，橡樹也會展現它的甜蜜面。就像你有時候會遇到一個看起來很粗獷、很硬派的人，但在那粗獷的外表底下，當你更了解這個人以後，你會發現有一顆溫柔的心，只在特別的時候才顯露出來。

There is a particular wasp called the gall wasp. This wasp makes a hole
in the oak leaves with its sting, and it lays its eggs in these holes, and
then flies away. It leaves its children to the care of the oak tree, and this
strong, hard tree becomes a gentle protector to the tiny wormlike lar-
vae, which come from the eggs. Around each little larva, the leaf grows a
little hollow ball the size of a cherry, and you can often see these balls
(gallapples) on oak leaves. Inside these little green balls there is a sweet
juice, and the tiny insect eats that juice and lives on it, until it becomes a
wasp and can fly away. So you could say the oak has two kinds of fruit:
the acorn, and the little gall-apples that are only grown for the larvae of
the gall-wasp. The little balls, the gall-apples have a sweet juice, and also
a very bitter juice within them, and this other juice is used for tanning
leather. Animal skins are kept soft and supple by tanning in the juice
of gall-apples.

> 有一種特別的黃蜂，叫做 gall wasp（癭蜂）。這種黃蜂用螫針在橡樹葉子上鑽一個洞，把卵產在洞裡，然後就飛走了。牠把孩子交給橡樹照顧，而這棵堅硬強壯的樹就變成了那些從卵裡孵出來的小小蟲狀幼蟲的溫柔守護者。在每一條小幼蟲的周圍，葉子會長出一個櫻桃大小的空心小球，你常常可以在橡樹葉子上看到這些小球，叫做 gallapples（癭果，也叫橡癭）。在這些小綠球裡面有一種甜甜的汁液，小昆蟲就吃這些汁液、靠它生活，直到牠變成一隻黃蜂，能夠飛走為止。所以你可以說橡樹有兩種果實：一種是橡實，另一種是只為癭蜂幼蟲而生的小癭果。這些小球——癭果——裡面有甜的汁液，同時也有一種非常苦的汁液，而這種苦汁是用來鞣皮革的。動物的皮就是靠浸泡在癭果的汁液裡，才能變得柔軟有彈性。

The Druids were quite right when they thought there was something
special about the oak tree. For itself, its only fruit is the acorn, but for the
little creatures, it grows the gall apple with sweet juice inside.

> 德魯伊們認為橡樹有某種特別之處，他們說得一點也沒錯。對橡樹自己來說，它唯一的果實是橡實；但為了那些小小的生物，它長出了裡面有甜汁的癭果。
