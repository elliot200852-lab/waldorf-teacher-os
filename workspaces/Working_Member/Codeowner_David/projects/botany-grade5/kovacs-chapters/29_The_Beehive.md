# 29. The Beehive

The queen bee lays her eggs in the regular, hexagonal cells, made by the
worker bees. The egg lies for three days quietly in its cell, and then it
opens and something that does not look the least bit like a bee comes
out. It looks like a tiny white caterpillar - it is to the bees what caterpillars
are to butterflies - and this bee-caterpillar is called a "larva."

> 蜂后把卵產在工蜂做好的整齊六角形巢室裡。卵在巢室裡靜靜地躺了三天，然後裂開了，爬出來的東西一點也不像蜜蜂。牠看起來像一條小小的白色毛蟲——對蜜蜂來說，就像毛蟲之於蝴蝶——這種蜜蜂的毛蟲叫做 "larva"（幼蟲）。

The queen bee does not feed or take care of the little larvae; her only
task is to lay eggs. But there are worker bees that have the special task
of looking after the larvae. The little things cannot feed themselves; they
are fed by the worker bee nurses.

> 蜂后不餵養也不照顧小幼蟲；她唯一的任務就是產卵。但有些工蜂專門負責照顧幼蟲。那些小東西不能自己吃東西，都由工蜂保姆來餵養牠們。

The larva is fed by its nurses for six days, and then it does what the
butterfly caterpillar does, it changes into a pupa. For twelve days the
pupa lies in its little cell of wax as though dead. After the twelve days
have passed, the pupa splits and out comes a little bee. So it is exactly
three weeks, twenty-one days, from the time the egg is laid until the bee
emerges.

> 幼蟲被保姆餵養了六天，然後牠就做了和蝴蝶毛蟲一樣的事——變成了 pupa（蛹）。蛹在小小的蠟巢室裡躺了十二天，像死了一樣。十二天過後，蛹裂開了，一隻小蜜蜂就出來了。所以從產卵到蜜蜂誕生，正好是三個星期、二十一天。

The little bee has three days to get used to the busy life in the hive.
And on the third day, the bee becomes a worker itself. No one tells the
young bee what it should do, and it is not told how to do it, or when it
should do it. Scientists have watched bees carefully and they observed
that no older bee stopped its work to spend any time with the young
bees; they are all too busy with their own jobs, and yet, the young bee
knows what to do. There is no school for bees; they know all they need
to know without learning.

> 小蜜蜂有三天的時間來適應蜂巢裡忙碌的生活。到了第三天，牠自己也成了一隻工蜂。沒有誰告訴這隻年輕的蜜蜂應該做什麼，也沒有誰告訴牠怎麼做，或者什麼時候做。科學家仔細觀察過蜜蜂，他們發現沒有任何一隻年長的蜜蜂會停下工作來花時間帶年輕的蜜蜂；牠們都忙著自己的事。可是年輕的蜜蜂就是知道該做什麼。蜜蜂沒有學校；牠們不用學習就知道所有該知道的事。

First the young bee goes to empty cells and cleans them, as there
must not be any dirt or dust in the cells. Bees are very clean creatures
and won't allow any dirt in their hive.

> 首先，年輕的蜜蜂會去空的巢室把它們打掃乾淨，因為巢室裡不能有任何灰塵或髒東西。蜜蜂是非常愛乾淨的生物，絕不允許蜂巢裡有髒污。

After two days of cleaning duties, the young bee becomes a nurse. It
goes to the cells where the honey is stored and takes food to the little
larvae. After twelve days of this work the 91 young bee knows, somehow,
that it must begin another task, and it becomes a wax maker.

> 打掃了兩天之後，年輕的蜜蜂變成了保姆。牠去儲存蜂蜜的巢室取食物，帶去餵小幼蟲。做了十二天這個工作之後，年輕的蜜蜂不知怎地就知道，牠必須開始另一項任務了，於是牠成了一隻造蠟工。

After a time as wax maker, the day comes when the young bee leaves
the beehive and flies for the first time. Initially, it only flies around the
hive in small circles with its head turned towards the hive, as though
afraid it might get lost if it couldn't see its home. It takes a few days

> 做了一段時間的造蠟工之後，有一天，年輕的蜜蜂要離開蜂巢，第一次飛出去了。起初，牠只是繞著蜂巢飛小小的圓圈，頭一直朝向蜂巢，好像怕看不到家就會迷路一樣。要練習好幾天，蜜蜂才準備好飛長途去採集花蜜。

Bees have no bags or boxes to carry the sticky liquid nectar with
them. Instead they have a second, special stomach - a special bag - in
addition to the one they use for eating. The nectar that the bee gathers
from the flowers goes into this special stomach until that stomach is full,
then the bee flies home to the hive, empties the second stomach, and
another bee takes the nectar to store it in cells. The collector bee flies
away again to find more nectar.

> 蜜蜂沒有袋子也沒有箱子來裝黏黏的液態花蜜。取而代之的是，牠們除了用來吃東西的胃之外，還有第二個特別的胃——一個特別的袋子。蜜蜂從花裡採到的花蜜就存進這個特別的胃裡，等到這個胃裝滿了，蜜蜂就飛回蜂巢，把第二個胃裡的花蜜倒出來，另一隻蜜蜂再把花蜜收進巢室裡儲存。採集蜂又飛走去找更多花蜜了。

If one bee discovers an orchard that the other bees of the hive have
missed, it has an interesting way of telling its fellow bees that there is a
rich treasure of nectar waiting for them. Bees have no language; no
voice. (The humming or buzzing we can hear is made by their wings and
is no use for telling something to other bees.) Having discovered the or-
chard, the bee fills its special bag with nectar, flies back to the hive and
begins a kind of dance on a honeycomb. It runs in a circle one way, and a
curve the other way and waggles its back end, and it seems that from
this "dance" the other bees know how to get to the orchard, as they all
get quite excited and set off in the right direction, towards the orchard.

> 如果有一隻蜜蜂發現了一個果園，是蜂巢裡其他蜜蜂還不知道的，牠有一個很有趣的方式告訴同伴們，那裡有豐富的花蜜寶藏等著牠們。蜜蜂沒有語言，也沒有聲音。（我們聽到的嗡嗡聲是翅膀發出的，沒辦法用來跟其他蜜蜂說事情。）發現了果園之後，蜜蜂把特別的袋子裝滿花蜜，飛回蜂巢，開始在蜂巢片上跳起一種舞蹈。牠先往一個方向跑一個圓圈，再往另一個方向跑一條弧線，還搖擺牠的尾部。看起來其他蜜蜂就是從這個「舞蹈」裡知道怎麼到達果園的，因為牠們都變得很興奮，朝著正確的方向——往果園——出發了。

There are also other duties for the worker bee. Some worker bees
have to stand guard at the entrance to the hive. They touch every incom-
ing bee with the little feelers (antennae) on their heads and recognize if
it belongs to the hive or not. There are robber bees and wasps that try to
get in and steal the honey, and any intruder is immediately killed with a
sharp and poisonous sting. When bees sting, they give up their lives;
they loose their sting and they die.

> 工蜂還有其他的職責。有些工蜂要站在蜂巢入口當守衛。牠們用頭上的小觸角（antennae）碰觸每一隻進來的蜜蜂，辨認牠是不是自己蜂巢的成員。有些強盜蜂和黃蜂會試圖闖進來偷蜂蜜，任何入侵者都會立刻被鋒利的、有毒的螫針刺死。蜜蜂螫人的時候，牠們也付出了自己的生命；牠們失去螫針，然後就死了。

A beehive also needs fresh air inside, just as we need fresh air in a
room, but beehives have no windows that can be opened, and 92they
only have a little hole as an entrance. There are always some worker
bees on duty near the entrance, and they use their wings like fans, and
produce a steady draught of fresh air into the hive.

> 蜂巢裡面也需要新鮮空氣，就像我們在房間裡需要新鮮空氣一樣。但蜂巢沒有可以打開的窗戶，只有一個小小的洞當入口。總是有一些值班的工蜂守在入口附近，牠們用翅膀像扇子一樣搧，為蜂巢送進持續不斷的新鮮空氣。

It is also important to keep a steady temperature in the hive. It must
not get too hot in summer, or too cold in winter. The temperature of a
beehive is like the temperature of our own bodies, always the same. In
fact, the temperature of a beehive is practically the same as the temper-
ature of the human body: 37°C (99°F). When you hold your wet hands in

In the summer, when it gets warmer inside, the bees bring water into
the hive and spray the honeycombs and inside walls with it - the bees
cool the temperature of the hive down by letting water evaporate inside.
They bring water inside to make the hive sweat. We sweat outside the
skin; a beehive sweats inside.

> 夏天，蜂巢裡面變熱的時候，蜜蜂會把水帶進蜂巢，噴灑在蜂巢片和內壁上——蜜蜂靠著讓水在裡面蒸發來為蜂巢降溫。牠們把水帶進去，讓蜂巢「出汗」。我們在皮膚外面流汗；蜂巢在裡面流汗。

In the winter, when it gets too cold, the bees huddle together in the
centre of the hive, clinging to one another. The bees on the outside of
the cluster wave their feelers, fan their wings, and waggle the back parts
of their bodies. In the same way that human beings get warm by moving
their arms, so the bees' bodies grow warmer by moving about. This also
warms the air inside the hive.

> 冬天，天氣太冷的時候，蜜蜂就擠在蜂巢的中央，彼此緊緊攀附在一起。在外圍的蜜蜂揮動觸角、搧動翅膀，搖擺牠們身體的後半部。就像人類揮動手臂會變暖一樣，蜜蜂的身體也因為活動而變得溫暖。這也讓蜂巢裡面的空氣暖和起來。

Think of all the different kinds of work that bees do: cleaning, nursing
larvae, making wax and wax-cells, collecting nectar, storing nectar in
cells, guarding the hive, fanning for fresh air, spraying water in hot
weather, and warming the air in cold weather. All this work is carried out
by the bees in turn. Every bee knows exactly what kind of job it should
do at any given time; there is never a fight about who should do this job
or that job; and there is never a job done unwillingly or carelessly. It is
truly wonderful that they know how to do all this.

> 想想蜜蜂做的這些各式各樣的工作：打掃、照顧幼蟲、製造蠟和蠟巢室、採集花蜜、把花蜜儲存在巢室裡、守衛蜂巢、搧風送新鮮空氣、天熱時噴水降溫、天冷時讓空氣暖和。所有這些工作都由蜜蜂輪流來做。每一隻蜜蜂在任何時候都確切知道自己該做什麼工作；從來不會為了誰做這個、誰做那個而爭吵；也從來沒有一件工作是不情願或馬馬虎虎做的。牠們知道怎麼做這一切，真的是太奇妙了。
