# 5. Botany in Class Five

If we consider the stage of development at which the children of ten or
eleven are, an analogy from history may help.
It would be a mistake to assume that human beings have always
thought logically, intellectually, as we do now. In fact, there is a definite
point in time when logical thought as we know it begins - when scientific
inquiry as we know it begins - when rational thought, speculation, anal-
ysis, makes its entry. The time when the faculty of rational, logical
thought makes its appearance is ancient Greece, the time of the Greek
philosophers, Socrates, Plato, Aristotle.

> 如果我們想一想十歲、十一歲孩子正處在什麼樣的發展階段，從歷史裡找一個對照或許會有幫助。若以為人類從古至今一直都像我們現在這樣用邏輯、用理智來思考，那就錯了。事實上，歷史上有一個明確的時刻，我們所熟悉的那種邏輯思考才開始出現——科學式的探究才開始出現——理性的思辨、分析，才正式登場。這個理性邏輯思考能力登場的時代，就是古希臘，就是 Socrates（蘇格拉底）、Plato（柏拉圖）、Aristotle（亞里斯多德）這些希臘哲學家的時代。

Older civilizations - India, Babylon, Egypt, even Greece itself before
the age of philosophy - cultivated a different faculty. In India, Babylon or
Egypt a myth was as valid an explanation of the world, of natural phe-
nomena, as a scientific explanation is valid for us. Now a myth, a story of
gods, heroes, monsters is not just the wild roaming of fancy. A true myth
of antiquity is something which does contain poetic fantasy, but also a
logic of its own. One could say that in the ancient civilizations, these two
faculties, fantasy and logic, are not yet separated, they are still a unity.
The Greek philosophers, the first rational, logical thinkers, appear at a
certain point in time, simply because that is the time when fantasy and
logic became separate and independent functions of the human mind
That is also the time when poetry emerges as a separate art.

> 更古老的文明——India（印度）、Babylon（巴比倫）、Egypt（埃及），甚至希臘自己在哲學時代之前——培養的是一種不同的能力。在印度、巴比倫或埃及，一個神話對世界、對自然現象的解釋，就跟科學解釋對我們一樣真實有效。而神話——那些關於諸神、英雄、怪獸的故事——可不只是天馬行空的幻想。古代真正的神話裡，既有詩一般的想像力，也有它自己的道理。可以這樣說：在古老的文明裡，想像力和邏輯這兩種能力還沒有分開，它們還是一體的。希臘哲學家——最早的理性邏輯思考者——會在歷史的某個時刻出現，正是因為那個時候，想像力和邏輯開始分道揚鑣，成為人類心智中各自獨立的功能。那也是詩歌作為一門獨立藝術開始出現的時代。

Now what took place in human history on a large scale has its coun-
terpart in the development of the individual human being. Around the
age of eleven or twelve there is a beginning of that great divide where
fantasy and logic part company.

> 在人類歷史上大規模發生的事，在每一個人的成長裡也有對應。大約在十一、十二歲左右，那個想像力與邏輯分道揚鑣的巨大分水嶺，就悄悄開始了。

It shows itself, for instance, as curiosity. Some children become fasci-
nated by facts, any facts, of science, geography, history. It shows itself in
other ways: some children get argumentative. 6They like to argue for
argument's sake, but it is in reality no more than a new toy they have dis-
covered; they like to play with this new toy.

> 這個變化會以不同的方式顯現出來。比如說，好奇心。有些孩子開始對各種事實著迷——科學的、地理的、歷史的，什麼事實都好。它也會用別的方式表現：有些孩子變得愛爭辯。他們為了辯論而辯論，但其實那不過是他們發現了一個新玩具，他們喜歡玩弄這個新玩具罷了。

Another side of this development is an enhanced feeling of one's own
like that" with quite a new emphasis - it is not at all like the "likes" and
"dislikes" of younger children.

> 這個發展的另一面，是對自己的喜好有了更強烈的感受——「我就是喜歡這樣」，帶著一種全新的強調——這跟更小的孩子的「喜歡」和「不喜歡」完全不同。

Even the naughtiness of children at this stage is different. Younger
children can be naughty because they can't help it; they follow some
urge they are unable to control. But children of this age are much more
deliberately naughty. It is much more like a scientific experiment: "How
much can I get away with?" "How far can I go?" And all this is connected
with the process of awakening. For the emergence of logic, this parting
of the ways between logic and fantasy, is an awakening. In the deliberate
naughtiness, in the argument, in the enhanced feeling of one's own
importance, in the curiosity, in all this the child experiences an awak-
ening, as from a dream.

> 就連這個階段孩子的調皮搗蛋也不一樣。更小的孩子搗蛋，是因為他們控制不了自己，他們跟著某種無法抑制的衝動走。但這個年紀的孩子搗蛋，卻是有意為之的。那更像是一場科學實驗：「我能逃過多少？」「我能走多遠？」而這一切，都跟覺醒的過程有關。因為邏輯的浮現，想像力與邏輯的分道揚鑣，本身就是一種覺醒。在那些故意的搗蛋裡、在爭辯裡、在更強烈的自我感受裡、在好奇心裡——在這一切當中，孩子正經歷著一場覺醒，彷彿從夢中醒來。

But there is one thing we must keep in mind. At this stage there is
only the beginning of the process, it is not yet completed. If at this stage
you give children just facts, the bare facts of science or of geography,
then you are not really helping the growth and development that takes
place in the child. They need the facts, they want the facts, but they must
be linked in a way that still satisfies the feeling - the fantasy - the poetry
in the child. Give them only the bare facts, then their fantasy, imagi-
nation and original creative ability, dies or withers.

> 但有一件事我們必須記在心裡。在這個階段，那個過程才剛剛開始，還沒有完成。如果在這時候你只給孩子事實——科學或地理的赤裸事實——那你並沒有真正幫助孩子內在正在發生的成長與發展。他們需要事實，他們渴望事實，但這些事實必須用一種仍然能滿足孩子內在感受的方式來串連——滿足那份想像力——那份詩意。如果你只給他們赤裸裸的事實，那麼他們的想像力、創造力和原初的創造能力，就會枯萎凋零。

That is why the botany lesson was given in the form set out here. The
plants are given in the sequence of the evolutionary system, beginning
with lower plant forms, fungi, algae, which have no flowers, pollen,
seeds, and leading up to the flowering plants.

> 這就是為什麼植物學課程要用這裡呈現的方式來教。植物是按照演化的順序來介紹的，從較低等的植物形態開始——fungi（真菌）、algae（藻類），它們沒有花、沒有花粉、沒有種子——然後一路引導到開花植物。

But the idea of evolution, of higher or lower plants, would be mean-
ingless to the child. It has to be brought near to them by comparing the
plant families with their own development. By comparing fungi with the
baby stage, algae with the toddler 7making its first steps, mono-
cotyledons with the first years at school - by these analogies the children
are introduced to evolution (although I never mentioned the word) in a
way which makes them aware of their own evolution, their own devel-
opment, something they can feel, not merely know. Of course such
analogies contain an element of fantasy, they are poetic analogies, but
this is exactly what the child still needs.

> 但是演化的概念、高等植物或低等植物的說法，對孩子來說毫無意義。要讓他們靠近這件事，就得把植物家族跟孩子自身的成長做比較。把真菌比作嬰兒階段、把藻類比作剛學走路的小小孩、把 monocotyledons（單子葉植物）比作剛上學的那幾年——透過這些類比，孩子們在不知不覺中接觸了演化（雖然我從來沒有提過這個詞），而且是用一種讓他們能感受到自身演化、自身成長的方式，不只是頭腦上的知道。當然，這樣的類比帶有想像的成分，它們是詩意的類比，但這正是孩子在這個階段仍然需要的。

I must also say something about the place of the botany lessons in the
science curriculum of the school. We proceed in science as we proceed
in geography - that is we begin with what is the nearest and then move
farther and farther out. In geography we start with the local city, then
the local country, the neighbouring countries, the continent and then
the other continents. In science we begin with the kingdom which is
nearest to human being - the animal kingdom - at age nine and ten
(Class Four). Next we take the plant kingdom at age ten and eleven (Class
Five). The mineral world, that is geology and the first steps in physics,
come at age eleven and twelve (Class Six). Physics and chemistry come at
age twelve to fourteen (Classes Seven and Eight). Mechanics, which is far-
thest away from our feeling, comes at age fourteen (Class Eight).

> 我還必須說一說植物學課在學校科學課程中的位置。我們教科學的順序，跟教地理一樣——也就是從最近的開始，然後一步步往外走。地理從自己所在的城市開始，然後是自己的國家、鄰近的國家、所在的洲，然後才是其他的洲。科學也是從離人類最近的王國開始——動物界——在九到十歲時教（四年級）。接下來是植物界，在十到十一歲時教（五年級）。礦物世界，也就是地質學和物理學的第一步，在十一到十二歲時教（六年級）。物理和化學在十二到十四歲時教（七、八年級）。力學，離我們的感受最遙遠的，在十四歲時教（八年級）。

Charles Kovacs
