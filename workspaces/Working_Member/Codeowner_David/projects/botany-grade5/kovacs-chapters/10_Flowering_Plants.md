# 10. Flowering Plants

Trees are really a kind
of earth-hill, pushed upwards,
and the leaves,
blossoms, and fruit, that grow on the branches, are like other plants
growing down on the ground. The only difference is that the leaves and
blossoms up in the tree's "garden" don't have individual roots, instead
they share all of the tree's roots.

> 樹，其實就是一座被往上推的大地山丘，而長在樹枝上的葉子、花朵和果實，就像地面上生長的其他植物一樣。唯一的不同是：樹上那座「花園」裡的葉子和花朵沒有各自的根，它們共用的是整棵樹的根。

But the first trees we heard about, the conifers, do not have true blos-
soms, they have green leaves like tiny needles and on most conifers they
remain green all year round, summer and winter. That is why these trees
are also called evergreens. They do not have true blossoms; they have
not learned from the sun how to make blossoms, and therefore, they are
like children who are four or five years old, who have not gone to school
yet.

> 但是我們最先認識的那些樹——conifer（針葉樹），它們沒有真正的花朵。它們的綠色葉子像細細的針，而且大部分的針葉樹一年四季、不管夏天冬天都保持綠色。所以這些樹也叫做 evergreen（常綠樹）。它們沒有真正的花，因為它們還沒有從太陽那裡學會怎麼開花。所以呢，它們就像四、五歲的小孩，還沒有上學。

Now we come to the stage and to the age when children go to school.
Now we come to the time when you begin to learn to read and to write.
The inner light of thought had already been at work within you. When
you could count up to twenty or thirty, could do little drawings, and
called yourself "I" for the first time, the inner light had been working
within you.

> 現在我們來到了孩子上學的階段和年紀。現在我們來到了你開始學習讀書寫字的時候。那道思考的內在之光，其實早就在你心裡運作了。當你能數到二十、三十，能畫小圖畫，第一次叫自己「我」的時候，那道內在之光就已經在你心裡工作了。

But once you came to school, this inner light had to achieve much
more. To learn to write and read needs much more effort by the inner
light in you, than anything you did before. Just think of adding and sub-
tracting and of the multiplication tables - it needs quite a bit of inner
light to learn things like that! If you love this inner light, then you are like
the plants which have beautiful flowers; you are like the red and pink
and blue and white and yellow blossoms which our eyes enjoy; you are
like the flowers that fill the air with lovely scents.

> 可是一旦你上了學，這道內在之光就得做更多的事了。學會讀和寫，需要你心裡那道光付出比以前任何時候都大的努力。想想看加法、減法和乘法表吧——學那些東西可需要不少的內在之光呢！如果你喜愛這道內在之光，那你就像那些開出美麗花朵的植物；你就像那些紅色、粉色、藍色、白色、黃色的花朵，讓我們的眼睛好歡喜；你就像那些讓空氣中充滿芬芳香味的花兒。

All the flowers and blossoms that open themselves so joyfully to the
sun, are like children who are open and willing to 35learn. And of course,
people who think more of mischief, who don't care to pay attention, are
like toadstools that turn away from the light. And some toadstools are
poisonous, and only grow where there is something dead.

> 所有那些歡歡喜喜地向太陽張開的花朵，就像那些敞開心胸、願意學習的孩子。當然啦，那些一心想著搗蛋、不肯專心的人，就像 toadstool（毒蕈）一樣背對著光。有些毒蕈還有毒呢，而且只長在有死掉的東西的地方。

The plants that bear flowers are called flowering plants, they are like
children who come to school and really take in what they hear. But the
flowering plants, the plants whose blossoms fill the fields and gardens

with beautiful scents, do not have a school, and do not have teachers as
human beings have. Their school and their teachers are the rays of the
sun.

> 那些會開花的植物叫做 flowering plant（開花植物），它們就像來到學校、真正把聽到的東西吸收進去的孩子。但是開花植物——那些用花朵的芬芳填滿田野和花園的植物——它們沒有學校，也沒有像人類那樣的老師。它們的學校和它們的老師，就是太陽的光芒。

But it is not only the light of the sun, there is something else at work.
At night-time when you look up to the sky, you see the countless stars,
and their light also comes to us down on the earth. In the daytime when
the sun shines, you may think there are no stars in the sky, but you are
mistaken, because the stars also shine during the day. However, the light
of the sun is so strong, so powerful that we cannot see the stars, but
they are there, they shine just the same. In the daytime, along with the
light of the sun, the light of the stars also shines down on earth. Our
human eyes may not notice it, but for the plants, the starlight is just as
real as the sunlight.

> 但不只是太陽的光在工作，還有另一種力量。夜晚你抬頭望向天空，你看到數不清的星星，它們的光也傳到了我們地球上。白天太陽照耀的時候，你可能以為天上沒有星星，但你錯了，因為星星白天也在發光。只是太陽的光太強、太有力量了，我們看不到星星，但它們在那裡，一樣在閃耀。白天的時候，星星的光和太陽的光一起照射到大地上。我們人的眼睛也許注意不到，但對植物來說，星光跟陽光一樣真實。

From the sun the plants learn to make a flower, but from the stars
they learn to give their flowers starlike shapes. Some are like six-pointed
stars,
some are like five-pointed stars,
some are like four-pointed stars,
and some are like stars with many rays. Some plants even show their star
in their fruit - like the "star" in an apple, or in oranges and lemons.

> 植物從太陽那裡學會開花，但從星星那裡學會讓花朵變成星星的形狀。有些花像六角星，有些像五角星，有些像四角星，還有些像有很多光芒的星星。有些植物甚至在果實裡也藏著一顆星——像 apple（蘋果）裡面的「星星」，或者 orange（柳橙）和 lemon（檸檬）裡面的也是。

You could say that the stars on high are the heavenly flowers, the
flowers of God. And our flowers here on earth are like a "mirror" that re-
flects the light of the heavenly flowers.

> 你可以說，天上的星星就是天堂的花朵，是上天的花。而我們地上的花朵就像一面「鏡子」，映照著天上花朵的光芒。

Our earthly flowers are of course, only tiny reflections, tiny pictures of
the wonderful lights of heaven. But when you are in school and you re-
ally learn and take things in, then this tiny bit of cleverness, of wisdom, of
intelligence that human beings absorb, is also a tiny reflection, a tiny
"mirror," a picture of the great, infinite wisdom of God.

> 我們地上的花朵當然只是微小的倒影、微小的圖像，映照著天上那些奇妙的光。但是當你在學校裡真正學習、真正吸收的時候，人類所接收到的那一點點聰明、一點點智慧、一點點靈性，也同樣是一個微小的倒影、一面小小的「鏡子」，映照著上天那無窮無盡的大智慧。
