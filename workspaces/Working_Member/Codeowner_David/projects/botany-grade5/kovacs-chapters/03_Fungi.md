# 3. Fungi

Each of the four elements play a part in how the plant grows: water and
roots; air and leaves; sun-heat and blossom; heat coming back from the
earth and fruit. But not all plants have leaves, blossoms, fruit, or even
roots; there are many plants that are without proper roots,
proper
leaves, blossoms, or fruit.

> 四種元素各自在植物的生長中扮演著一個角色：水與根；空氣與葉；太陽的熱與花朵；從大地回來的熱與果實。但並不是所有的植物都有葉、花、果實，甚至根；有許多植物沒有真正的根、真正的葉、花朵或果實。

You recall that when you really understand something, it is like an
inner light. And as you grow up and learn more and more, there will be
more and more of this inner light in you, and you will know and under-
stand more and more. Now these plants have no inner light, their light
comes from outside, from the sun. But the sunlight, too, is full of wis-
dom, and in the same way that human beings learn from their inner
light, so the beings of nature, the plants, learn from the sunlight.

> 你還記得嗎，當你真的理解了一件事的時候，就像有一道內在的光。隨著你長大，學到越來越多的東西，你裡面的這道內在之光也會越來越多，你就會知道和理解更多。這些植物沒有內在的光，它們的光來自外面，來自太陽。但是太陽的光也充滿了智慧，就像人類從內在的光學習一樣，大自然的生命——植物——也從太陽光學習。

Plants like the dandelion or the rose - plants that have roots and
stems and leaves and blossom and fruit - they have learned all the plant
wisdom they can learn. Other plants without leaves, blossoms or fruit
have not learnt enough. Which plants do not learn enough from the
sunlight?

> 像 dandelion（蒲公英）或 rose（玫瑰）這樣的植物——有根、有莖、有葉、有花、有果實的植物——它們已經學會了所有植物能學到的智慧。而那些沒有葉片、沒有花朵或果實的植物，還沒有學到足夠的東西。哪些植物沒有從太陽光那裡學到足夠的智慧呢？

When you are ten or eleven years old, you can read and write and do
arithmetic. When you were in the kindergarten you could not do any of
these things. And before that when you were a tiny tot, you were not
even clever enough to be in the kindergarten, and before that, you were
a toddler who could barely walk. And even before that you were babies,
you could not speak yet, you could only make funny noises and you
could not walk, you could only wiggle your tiny arms and legs.

> 當你十歲或十一歲的時候，你已經能夠閱讀、寫字和做算術了。而你在幼兒園的時候，這些事情你一樣都不會。在那之前，你還是個小小孩，連進幼兒園的本事都還不夠。在那之前呢，你還是個剛剛學走路的幼兒。甚至在那之前，你還是個嬰兒，你還不會說話，你只會發出一些好玩的聲音，你也不會走路，你只能揮動你小小的手臂和雙腿。

There are plants which are like babies, others are like toddlers, others
are like young children, but plants like the dandelion or the rose, plants
with beautiful blossoms, with leaves and fruit, they are almost like adults.

> 有些植物就像嬰兒，有些像學步的幼兒，有些像小小孩，但像 dandelion（蒲公英）或 rose（玫瑰）這樣的植物，擁有美麗的花朵、葉片和果實，它們幾乎就像大人了。

You can't remember what it was like to be a baby, because a baby is
asleep most of the time - no one can remember what it is like when they
are asleep. And the baby - even when it wails or wiggles or drinks, does
not know what it is doing - even then, it is more asleep than awake.

> 你不記得當嬰兒是什麼感覺了，因為嬰兒大部分的時間都在睡覺——沒有人能記得自己睡著時的事情。而嬰兒呢——即使它在哭、在動、在喝奶，它也不知道自己在做什麼——即使在那些時候，它也是睡著的時間比醒著的時間多。

The plants that are like babies, that are more asleep than awake, are
mushrooms, or as they are called in botany, the fungi.

> 那些像嬰兒一樣、睡著的時間比醒著的時間多的植物，就是 mushroom（蘑菇），或者用植物學的說法叫做 fungi（真菌）。

What does a baby enjoy? You can see it enjoys drinking its milk,
nothing else. It would not want to be outside and play. Mushrooms do
not like to be out in the open, in the sunlight, they prefer the shade
where it is dark; they grow where it is damp and shady and keep away
from the sunlight. Green leaves and blossoms only grow when there is
sunlight, but the mushroom, the fungus, has no leaves and no blossoms,
so it is the baby of the plant world.

> 嬰兒喜歡什麼呢？你可以看到它喜歡喝奶，就只有這樣。它不會想要到外面去玩。Mushroom（蘑菇）也不喜歡待在空曠的地方、在陽光下，它們更喜歡陰暗的地方；它們長在潮濕陰暗的地方，遠離陽光。綠葉和花朵只有在有陽光的時候才會長出來，但蘑菇、真菌，沒有葉子也沒有花朵，所以它就是植物世界裡的嬰兒。

Think of a blossom, of a flower that opens towards the light, towards
the sun. If you look at the "cap" of a mushroom, it does the opposite: it
turns against the light and opens towards the earth, towards darkness.
The real flower turns towards the light, whereas the mushroom's cap
turns away from it.

> 想想看一朵花、一朵向著光、向著太陽張開的花。如果你看看蘑菇的「帽子」，它做的事情恰恰相反：它背對著光，向著大地、向著黑暗張開。真正的花朵朝著光轉去，而蘑菇的帽子卻從光那裡轉開。

On the underside of that mushroom's cap, between the "folds," there
is a fine dust. It is not seed because seeds are made by the sun, and it is
not pollen, the fine dust in flowers. The tiny grains of dust that fall from
the underside the mushroom are called "spores."

> 在蘑菇帽子的底面，那些「褶皺」之間，有一層細細的粉塵。它不是種子，因為種子是由太陽造出來的；它也不是 pollen（花粉），花朵裡面的那種細粉。從蘑菇底部掉下來的那些微小顆粒叫做 spore（孢子）。

When such a tiny spore falls to the ground, a new mushroom does
not grow right away, something quite different happens. From the spore
a very fine and white thread grows, and from this one thread other
threads grow, and from these threads others grow; if we could see it, it
would be like a tree of fine threads - but a tree that grows and spreads
under the earth in darkness.

> 當這樣一個微小的孢子掉到地上的時候，一朵新的蘑菇並不會馬上長出來，而是發生了一件很不一樣的事情。從孢子裡長出一根非常細的白色絲線，從這一根絲線又長出其他的絲線，再從那些絲線又長出更多絲線；如果我們能看得見，它會像一棵由細絲組成的樹——但這是一棵在大地底下、在黑暗中生長和蔓延的樹。

This tree of fine, white threads, therefore, is the mushroom plant. And
from this plant, or small, underground "tree," a kind of "fruit" grows, or
as it is called, a "fruiting body," and that is the mushroom that you can
see growing above the
ground. The mushroom is a strange kind of
"fruit" that comes from a tangle of white threads below the ground.

> 所以，這棵由細白絲線組成的「樹」，才是真正的蘑菇植物。從這株植物，或者說這棵小小的地下「樹」，會長出一種「果實」，也就是所謂的「子實體」，那就是你看到長在地面上的蘑菇。蘑菇是一種奇特的「果實」，它來自地底下一團纏繞交錯的白色絲線。

When you pick up a mushroom and you see the tiny threads at its
"foot," these threads are not roots, they are bits of that "tree" or "plant"
below. The mushroom has no roots - it is such a baby amongst plants
that is has no proper roots, it has no green leaves, it has no blossom, and
the "cap" does the opposite of a blossom, it turns away from the light.

> 當你摘起一朵蘑菇，看到它「腳」那裡的細小絲線時，那些絲線不是根，它們是地下那棵「樹」或「植物」的一小部分。蘑菇沒有根——它在植物之中就是這樣一個嬰兒，它沒有真正的根，沒有綠色的葉子，沒有花朵，而那個「帽子」做的事恰恰跟花朵相反——它是從光那裡轉開的。

The "proper" or higher plants, the plants that grow in the sunlight,
have both blossoms and fruit. The mushroom is such a baby that it can't
distinguish between blossom and fruit, so that what you see is actually
the blossom and the fruit at the same time. But it is a blossom that turns
away from the light and a fruit that shoots up from the darkness, a fruit
not ripened by the light and warmth of the sun.

> 那些「正式的」或者說高等的植物，在陽光中生長的植物，既有花朵也有果實。蘑菇是這樣一個小嬰兒，它沒辦法區分花朵和果實，所以你看到的其實同時是花朵也是果實。但它是一朵從光那裡轉開的花，也是一個從黑暗中冒出來的果實，一個沒有被太陽的光和溫暖催熟的果實。

There are even "mushrooms" that grow below the ground, and these
are called truffles. People like to eat truffles, but because they are under
the earth truffle dogs (or very occasionally pigs) are used to sniff them
out. The dog starts digging when it smell truffles under the ground, and
then the people take the truffles for themselves - the dog must be satis-
fied with ordinary dog-food.

> 甚至有些「蘑菇」是長在地底下的，它們叫做 truffle（松露）。人們很喜歡吃松露，但因為它們在地底下，所以需要用松露犬（偶爾也用豬）來把它們嗅出來。當狗聞到地底下有松露的時候就會開始挖掘，然後人們就把松露拿走了——可憐的狗只能吃一般的狗糧。

You can see how the fungi or mushrooms cling to Mother Earth, just
like tiny babies, and you know that babies grow very quickly - in the first
months of life you grow much faster than you are growing now. That is
also something the plant-babies, the fungi, do. They grow very fast: one
day you see nothing, then after a good rainfall mushrooms appear, sev-
eral inches high. You will never see a flower grow so quickly.

> 你可以看到，fungi（真菌）或蘑菇是怎麼緊緊依附著大地母親的，就像小小的嬰兒一樣。而你知道嬰兒長得非常快——在生命的頭幾個月，你長得比現在快得多。植物界的嬰兒——真菌——也是這樣。它們長得非常快：前一天你什麼都沒看到，一場好雨過後，蘑菇就冒出來了，好幾英寸高。你從來不會看到一朵花長得這麼快。

A flower learns to produce lovely coloured petals and a sweet scent
from the sunlight. The mushrooms have neither petal nor sweet scent -
they do not learn from the sunlight, and stay babies forever.

> 一朵花從陽光那裡學會了長出美麗的彩色花瓣和甜美的香氣。蘑菇呢，既沒有花瓣也沒有甜美的香氣——它們不從陽光那裡學習，永遠都是嬰兒。
