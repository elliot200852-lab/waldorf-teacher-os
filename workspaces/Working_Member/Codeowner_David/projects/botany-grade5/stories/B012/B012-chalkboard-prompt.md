# B012 黑板畫 Prompt

## 中文描述

畫面以深色黑板為底（深藍至黑色漸層）。中央是一朵台灣百合的剖面圖，白色喇叭形花冠被縱切開來，露出花的內部結構——四個同心環清晰可見。最外層的花被片如同一雙正在打開的手，從合攏的祈禱姿態轉為手心朝上、捧著禮物的姿態。花的內部，金黃色的雄蕊圍成一圈皇冠狀，花粉以細小的光點表現。正中央，綠色的雌蕊筆直站立如權杖，底部的子房圓潤飽滿如寶球。

左側以淡筆畫出一朵完整的梅花作為對照——五片花瓣，花萼與花瓣分明。右下角以柔和的粉筆標註四個環的名稱。整體色調以白、金、綠為主，大地的褐色從下方升起，太陽的金光從上方灑落，在花的中心交匯。

## English Prompt

Waldorf-style chalkboard drawing on a dark blackboard background (deep blue-black gradient). Chalk-like textures with soft, blended colors throughout. Landscape format.

Center: a longitudinal cross-section of a Taiwan lily (Lilium formosanum), its white trumpet-shaped blossom sliced open to reveal the internal structure. The four concentric rings of the flower are clearly visible from outside to inside. The outermost tepals are depicted as hands transitioning from a prayer position (closed) to an open, cupped position holding a precious gift — the left side of the flower shows the closed prayer gesture, the right side shows the opened hands cradling the blossom within. The six tepals glow in soft white with faint purple-brown streaks on the outer surface.

Second ring inward: delicate petals (in the lily's case, merged with the tepals) rendered in luminous white-cream chalk.

Third ring: six stamens arranged in a crown-like circle around the center, their anthers depicted as small golden orbs dusted with fine golden pollen particles that seem to float like tiny stars. The pollen glows warm gold, representing the sun's gift.

Innermost center: a single green pistil standing straight like a royal sceptre, with a three-lobed stigma at the top. Below it, a round, full ovary rendered in deep green, resembling a small globe or orb — the earth's gift. Fine root-like lines descend from the ovary downward into dark earth tones.

Upper area: warm golden light radiates downward from above, suggesting the sun's gift flowing into the stamens. Lower area: rich earth-brown tones rise upward, flowing into the green pistil and ovary.

Left side, smaller scale: a complete plum blossom (Prunus mume) with five distinct white petals and a clearly separate green calyx, shown as a contrast to the lily's fused tepals.

Lower right corner: faint chalk labels in gentle curves marking the four rings — calyx, petals, stamens, pistil — written in both Chinese and English in small, elegant script.

Overall mood: luminous, reverent, precise yet organic. Colors are chalky and soft-edged — white, warm gold, deep green, earth brown, faint purple. No hard outlines. The composition balances scientific clarity with Waldorf aesthetic warmth. The cross-section feels like opening a sacred space, not a clinical dissection. Suitable for a Grade 5 classroom.
