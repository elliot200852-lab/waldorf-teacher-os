# B011 黑板畫 Prompt

## 中文描述

畫面以深色黑板為底，採用左右分割構圖，呈現單子葉與雙子葉的對比世界。

左側為單子葉的天地：一叢稻穗從水田中挺立，金黃的穗粒微微低垂，旁邊一株翠綠的竹子拔地而起，竹節分明，竹葉修長。稻葉與竹葉上清晰可見平行排列的葉脈線條。底下的土壤中，鬚根如鬍鬚般向四方散開，旁邊隱約可見一顆圓潤的球莖。

右側為雙子葉的天地：一株茶樹枝葉繁茂，白色五瓣小花點綴其間；旁邊一棵樟樹的枝幹蒼勁有力，葉片上網狀脈絡交織如網。底下的土壤中，一條粗壯的主根深深向下扎去，分出側根如倒立的樹冠。

畫面中央上方，一輪金色太陽將光芒均勻灑向兩側，象徵太陽對所有植物的召喚。整體色調以粉筆質感呈現，柔和、溫暖，帶有教室的安定氛圍。

## English Prompt

Waldorf-style chalkboard drawing on a dark blackboard background. Chalk-like textures with soft, blended colors throughout. Landscape format, split composition.

Left side — the world of monocots: A cluster of rice plants (Oryza sativa) rises from a waterlogged paddy field, golden grain heads gently drooping. Beside them, a tall bamboo culm reaches upward with clearly segmented nodes and long, slender leaves. The leaves of both rice and bamboo show distinctly visible parallel veins running lengthwise. Below the soil line, fibrous roots spread outward like a beard in all directions, with a rounded bulb faintly visible nearby.

Right side — the world of dicots: A tea bush (Camellia sinensis) with lush dark-green foliage, dotted with small white five-petaled flowers. Next to it, a camphor tree (Cinnamomum camphora) branch with sturdy bark texture and broad leaves showing intricate reticulate (net-like) vein patterns. Below the soil line, a thick taproot plunges deep downward, branching into lateral roots like an inverted tree crown.

Upper center: a warm golden sun casting equal light to both sides, its rays streaming gently downward in soft ribbons, symbolizing the sun's call to all flowering plants.

The soil cross-section runs across the entire lower portion, connecting both sides and revealing the contrasting root systems — fibrous roots on the left, taproot on the right. A subtle visual transition divides the two halves without a hard line.

Overall mood: warm, contemplative, educational. Colors are chalky and soft-edged — gold, warm green, earth brown, cream white, gentle amber. No hard outlines. Everything has a soft glow as if drawn with care on a real classroom chalkboard. Suitable for a Grade 5 Waldorf botany lesson on monocots versus dicots.
