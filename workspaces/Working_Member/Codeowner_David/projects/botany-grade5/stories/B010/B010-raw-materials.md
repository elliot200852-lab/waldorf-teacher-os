# B010 開花植物導論 — 素材包

## 一、Kovacs 核心段落摘要（英文，Ch.10 Flowering Plants）

### Core Teaching Imagery

- **Tree as earth-hill**: A tree is a mound of earth pushed upward; its branches are a garden whose inhabitants (leaves, blossoms, fruit) share a single communal root system.
- **Sun as teacher**: The sun is the school and the teacher of the plant world. Conifers have not yet "learned from the sun" how to blossom; flowering plants have. Learning to flower parallels learning to read and write.
- **Stars as heavenly flowers / flowers as earthly mirrors**: Stars are the flowers of heaven. Earthly flowers are tiny mirrors reflecting celestial light. Star-shapes visible in blossoms (six-pointed, five-pointed, four-pointed) and inside fruit (the star in an apple cross-section) are imprints of starlight.

### Storytelling Techniques

- Direct address to child as "you," weaving the child's own biography into botanical narrative
- Developmental staging: Conifers = pre-school children (4-5 years old); Flowering plants = school-age children ready to learn
- Concrete to cosmic in one breath: from multiplication table to infinite wisdom of God in a single paragraph

### Child-Development Analogies

| Plant stage | Human development |
|---|---|
| Conifers (no blossoms) | 4-5 year olds, pre-school, inner light working but not yet schooled |
| Flowering plants | School-age children who open themselves to learning |
| Toadstools | Those who turn away from light |

### Key Quotes (Ch.10)

1. "Trees are really a kind of earth-hill, pushed upwards."
2. "They have not learned from the sun how to make blossoms, and therefore, they are like children who are four or five years old."
3. "If you love this inner light, then you are like the plants which have beautiful flowers."
4. "All the flowers and blossoms that open themselves so joyfully to the sun, are like children who are open and willing to learn."
5. "From the sun the plants learn to make a flower, but from the stars they learn to give their flowers starlike shapes."
6. "Our earthly flowers are like a 'mirror' that reflects the light of the heavenly flowers."

### Botanical Facts

- Conifers lack true blossoms; needle-shaped leaves, evergreen
- Flowering plants (angiosperms) produce blossoms in response to light
- Flower symmetry follows numerical patterns: 6, 5, 4 petals, multi-rayed
- Star patterns appear in fruit cross-sections (apple, orange, lemon)

## 二、台灣開花植物對應資料（中文）

### 概況數據

| 項目 | 數據 |
|------|------|
| 原生維管束植物總數 | 約 4,077 種 |
| 特有種數量 | 約 1,067 種 |
| 特有種比例 | 約 26.2% |
| 特有種比例最高的科 | 龍膽科 80.8%、茶科 63.2%、杜鵑花科 60% |
| 特有種數量最多的科 | 蘭科 92 種、菊科 64 種、薔薇科 49 種 |

台灣面積雖小（約 36,000 km²），位於東亞與馬來植物區系交界，海拔 0 至近 4,000 公尺，涵蓋熱帶至寒帶四種氣候帶，是第三紀冰河孑遺植物的避難所。

### 代表性物種

**1. 台灣百合（Lilium formosanum）**
- 全島分布，海平面至 3,000 公尺以上
- 白色喇叭狀花冠，外側帶紫紅色條紋
- 魯凱族視百合為勇敢與毅力的象徵
- 教學適用：從海邊到高山都能生長，說明植物對環境的適應

**2. 玉山杜鵑（Rhododendron pseudochrysanthum）**
- 台灣特有種，海拔 3,000 公尺以上高山
- 花色白至淡粉紅，曾被提議為國花候選
- 教學適用：高山環境下的開花策略

**3. 烏來杜鵑（Rhododendron kanehirae）**
- 台灣特有種，原僅分布於新北市烏來區北勢溪沿岸
- 野外族群因翡翠水庫興建幾近滅絕，目前復育中
- 教學適用：保育議題最佳案例

**4. 台灣一葉蘭（Pleione formosana）**
- 台灣特有種，海拔 1,500-2,500 公尺雲霧帶
- 每個假球莖只長一片葉子，花色粉紫
- 教學適用：蘭科植物的特殊生存策略

**5. 圓仔花 / 千日紅（Gomphrena globosa）**
- 全島低海拔平地、校園普遍栽培
- 花期極長，紫紅色球狀花序經久不凋
- 教學適用：校園最易觀察的開花植物

### 台灣俗諺

1. **「一枝草，一點露」**——每一棵小草都有屬於它的一滴露水。每個生命都有存在的價值與天地的照顧。
2. **「圓仔花不知醜，大紅花醜不知」**——每朵花都有自己的美，不必與他人比較。
